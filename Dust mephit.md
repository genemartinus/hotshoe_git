### Dust Mephit (Lesser)

- Small elemental neutral evil
- **Armor Class** 12
- **Hit Points** 10 (3d6)
- **Speed** 30 ft., fly 30 ft.
    
|STR|DEX|CON|INT|WIS|CHA|
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|5 (-3)|14 (+2)|10 (+0)|9 (-1)|11 (+0)|10 (+0)|
- **Skills** Perception +2, Stealth +4
- **Damage Vulnerabilities** Fire
- **Damage Immunities** Poison
- **Condition Immunities** Poisoned
- **Senses** Darkvision 60 ft., Passive Perception 12
- **Languages** Auran, Terran
- **Challenge** 1/4 (50 XP)

**Death Burst.** When the mephit dies, it explodes in a burst of dust. Each creature within 5 feet of it must succeed on a **DC 10 Constitution saving throw** or be **Blinded** until the end of its next turn. _(Reduced from 1 minute to 1 round to reflect lower CR)._

**Innate Spellcasting (1/Day).** The mephit can innately cast _Color Spray_ (DC 10), requiring no material components. Its innate spellcasting ability is Charisma. _(Replaced 'Sleep' with 'Color Spray' to reduce the chance of an accidental TPK at very low levels)._
### Actions

**Claws.** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one creature.  Hit:  `dice: 1d4+2`slashing damage.

**Blinding Breath (Recharge 6).** The mephit exhales a 15-foot cone of blinding dust. Each creature in that area must succeed on a **DC 10 Dexterity saving throw** or be **Blinded** until the end of the mephit's next turn. _(Reduced duration from 1 minute to 1 round)._

---
The Dust Mephit (Lesser) is the ultimate "Nuisance Saboteur." In the Hotshoe landscape, these creatures likely function as the scouts or scavengers of the more powerful thermal life forms.
While they have a low damage output, their entire kit is designed to inflict the Blinded condition. In a party of Level 1 adventurers, a blinded fighter or rogue is a dead weight, making the Mephit a high-priority target that punishes you for killing it too close.
Combat Strategy: The Gritty Saboteur
1. The "Color Spray" Opener
Because these are "Lesser" versions, they are smarter than a beast but more cowardly than a warrior.
 * The Ambush: Use their Stealth (+4) to hide in the thick ash fall. When the party passes, the Mephit flies within 15 feet and drops Color Spray.
 * Tactical Choice: This is a non-lethal way to take 1–2 players out of the fight for a round without the "accidental TPK" risk of the Sleep spell. It creates immediate chaos.
2. The "Breath and Bait" Maneuver
The Mephit wants to stay at the edge of the party's reach.
 * Blinding Breath (Recharge 6): It uses its 30 ft. fly speed to hover just outside of melee range (roughly 10–15 feet up). It exhales its cone to catch multiple targets.
 * Luring to Melee: If a player is blinded, the Mephit might swoop in for a Claw attack (+4 to hit with Advantage against blinded targets).
3. The "Suicide Bomber" (Death Burst)
This is the Mephit's final middle finger to the party.
 * Positioning: If the Mephit is low on health, it will intentionally fly into the face of a melee character (like Obur or a player Fighter).
 * The Burst: When the player kills it, the Death Burst triggers. Even if the player survived the fight, they are now blinded for a round, potentially leaving them vulnerable to a second wave of enemies (like Ash-Spore Husks).

Synergy: The Hotshoe Ecosystem
The Dust Mephit is a "force multiplier" when paired with your other monsters:
 * The Phoenix Duo: A Dust Mephit and an Ash-Beak Phoenix create a "Blindness Overload." The Mephit blinds with its breath, and the Phoenix dives in for the fire damage with Advantage.
 * Durn's Distraction: If the Black Gold team is "on the job," Durn might use a Mephit as a living distraction. He knows the Mephit is Vulnerable to Fire, so he might even toss a torch at one to trigger an early Death Burst near an enemy.
 * Phanzis's Telepathy: Phanzis Guos might keep a "pet" Lesser Mephit. He can use his Telepathy to direct the Mephit's Blinding Breath with surgical precision while he stays safe behind his Shield spell.
Roleplay Flavor: The Choking Whisper
 * The Sound: They speak Auran and Terran, which in Hotshoe probably sounds like the grinding of pebbles and the whistling of wind through a cracked pipe.
 * Visuals: Describe them as looking like "living soot." They don't have solid skin; they look like a whirlwind of ash held together by a malicious, glowing core.
 * The Coward's Exit: If a Mephit sees a player using fire (their vulnerability), they should shriek in Terran and immediately try to fly away, perhaps dropping their Color Spray as a parting gift.
