This addition to the Hotshoe ecosystem perfectly bridges the gap between the "Thermal" and "Carbon" worlds. Needle Grass acts as a biological filter, turning the volcanic waste (the ash) into a usable industrial resource (iron/magnetite).
However, in a town defined by secrets and scarcity, this discovery has turned a simple ground cover into a source of intense corporate—and alchemical—espionage.

🛠️ The Needle Grass Economy: "Magnetic Gold"
1. Dimble’s Secret Distillery
Dimble has moved beyond just brewing; he’s now a Micro-Metallurgist.
 * The Process: By fermenting "Ash Yam Mash" with Needle Grass roots, he uses the plant's hyper-accumulation to pull the iron out of the food supply.
 * The Result: He gets a "Cleaner" yam spirit (less metallic tang) and a byproduct of pure iron needles.
 * The "Pebble" Connection: Some whisper that his Fermenter’s Stone actually provides the magnetic pull required for the filtration process.
2. Millicent’s Alchemical Jealousy
Millicent is "desperately curious" because she needs those iron needles for her Conducive Inks.
 * The Conflict: She knows Dimble is sitting on a stockpile of pure, magnetically filtered iron. She might hire the players to "audit" Dimble’s cellar or "borrow" a sample of his slurry to reverse-engineer the magnetic filtration.

1. Kaelen’s Culinary "Pruning"
Kaelen wants the grass for the opposite reason: Purity.
 * The Goal: He wants to create a Cinder Berry Jam that doesn't taste like sucking on a copper coin. If he can use Needle Grass to "strip" the minerals from the berries before they are jarred, he’ll have the first luxury export in Hotshoe history.

⚔️ Needle Grass: Combat & Hazard Mechanics
For a Level 1 party, a patch of Needle Grass is as dangerous as a pit trap.
 * Hazard: The Scavenger’s Curse
   * Any creature that falls Prone or is forced to move through a patch of Needle Grass without heavy armor must succeed on a DC 12 Constitution Save.
   * On a Failure: The character takes 1 piercing damage and is "Splintered." They have Disadvantage on Dexterity checks involving fine motor skills (like picking locks or loading a crossbow) until the splinters are removed during a Short Rest with a healer's kit.
 * Tactical Use: The Scouring Strike
   * A player can spend 1 minute using a clump of Needle Grass to "sharpen" a dull blade. The next successful melee attack with that weapon deals an additional 1 point of damage, after which the "hone" is lost.
