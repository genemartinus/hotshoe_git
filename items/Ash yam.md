### Botanical Classification: _Dioscorea Cinis_ (The Ash Yam)

The **Ash Yam** is a geological anomaly as much as a biological one. It is a lithophilic tuber that thrives exclusively in the high-acidity, mineral-dense silts of active volcanic regions. While most flora withers under the touch of Mount Conniption’s breath, the Ash Yam uses the heat to accelerate its unique subterranean fermentation.
### Physical Characteristics

- **Vining Structure:** Above ground, the Ash Yam presents as a series of creeping, heart-shaped leaves. The foliage is a deep, bruised emerald with jagged silver veins. These leaves are covered in microscopic, glass-like hairs (trichomes) that trap falling volcanic ash, absorbing nitrogen directly from the debris.
    
- **The Tuber (The "Heart"):** The edible portion is a cylindrical root that can grow up to 18 inches in length.
    
    - **Periderm (Skin):** The outer layer is obsidian-black, rough, and scorched in appearance. It is incredibly tough, requiring a sharp blade to penetrate.  
        
    - **Pulp (Flesh):** The interior is a dense, marbled starch. It ranges from a pale lavender to a deep, royal violet. When cut, it bleeds a thick starchy bitter sap that smells faintly of rusty caramelized sugar.
        
- **Root System:** The plant does not seek water; it seeks heat. Its secondary roots wrap around subterranean basalt formations, drawing warmth to keep the tuber in a constant state of "slow-cook" while still in the earth.  See [[Thermal based life forms]].
    
---
### Lifecycle & Ecology

- **The "Fire-Bloom":** The Ash Yam only flowers during periods of high seismic activity. It produces a singular, soot-grey blossom that opens at night, pollinated by ash-moths.
    
- **Thermo-bio-chemical growth:** The thermo-bio-chemical processes in the roots that reach down to the heat provide the energy resources for growth similar to sunshine and photosynthesis.  Ash-yam has green leaves as a back-up redundant source of energy and production of the other chemical processes for growth.  
    
- **Relationship with the Volcano:** The plant acts as a heat sink. In areas where Ash Yams are densely packed, the ground temperature is noticeably cooler, as the roots absorb the thermal energy to drive biological growth.  This might make a case for agroforestry where Ash Yams can help to moderate the soil temperatures.

## Encounter scenarios
Millicent is secretly the largest consumer of Ash Yams in Hotshoe. To her, the tuber isn't food—it’s a biological filtration system and a source of high-density mineral reagents.  Here is how **Millicent Preston** utilizes the Ash Yam in her alchemical practice:

### Obsidian Ink

Millicent has created **Obsidial Ink** designed for preparation of fireproof ledger entries and magical scrolls.

- **The Process:**  Sharp crystalline obsidian dust clumps when mixed with fluid meant to withhold the rigors of magic craft.  Millicent is refining Ash Yam extracts as a binding agent that will be durable and adhere reliably to all objects for magic crafts.

### Thermal ressistant fabric

As an apothecary in a volcanic landscape, Millicent needs robust protective garments to resist the heat.  Similar to Durn's hotshoe polish, she has found a way to fireproof fabrics.

- **The Process:** She grinds the **obsidian-black skins** of the yam into a fine grit and mixes it with the inner pulp to create a thick, purple-black paste.
- Gloves and aprons made from fabrics impregnated with the paste provides incredible heat resistance. It’s the secret behind why her gear never looks singed,.

The Ash Yam is the missing link in your Hotshoe ecology. It is the bridge between the dying "Carbon" world and the rising "Thermal" world, acting as a biological engine that thrives on the very heat and ash that choke everything else.
Because it functions as a biological battery driven by a thermal gradient, it isn't just food—it’s a living alchemical reagent that Phanzis and Millicent would likely kill to control.
🍠 Alchemical Properties: The "Battery" in the Dirt
1. The Thermal Gradient (Energy Storage)
Because the plant generates starches through a temperature difference between its "Hot Core" and "Cool Surface," the harvested root retains a "Residual Charge."
 * The "Jumpstart" Mechanic: To cook or process an Ash Yam, you cannot simply boil it in water. It requires a high-intensity thermal shock (like being buried in active lava-sand or hit with a Fire Bolt). Without this, the starches remain "locked" and indigestible—tougher than ironwood.
 * Alchemical Use: Phanzis has discovered that if you slice a raw Ash Yam and press it against a piece of Dwarven Obsidian, it can "recharge" the latent magical energy in the stone, making the obsidian glow with a dull, internal heat.
2. The "Woody" Density (Structural Carbon)
The Ash Yam is so carbon-dense that it behaves more like a mineral than a vegetable.
 * The Sap: The sap is a thick, black resin. When mixed with Needle Grass iron-needles, it creates a "Thermal Solder" that can bond metal to stone—essential for repairing the town's crumbling masonry.
 * The Root: Once "fired" (cooked), the interior becomes a soft, glowing mash that provides 24 hours of "Internal Warmth," granting Resistance to Cold for 1 hour after eating.
⛏️ The Obsidian Mystery: The Dwarven Connection
The Dwarven Obsidian isn't just volcanic glass; it is "Tempered Ash." The dwarves of the old mines discovered that if they grew Ash Yams directly against the obsidian veins, the plant’s thermal gradient would "slow-cook" the glass, removing the brittleness and creating a material that could hold an edge sharper than steel.
The Problem:
Since the mines were lost, the supply of "Tempered" obsidian has vanished.
 * The Theory: The dwarves weren't just mining; they were farming. They used the Ash Yams to "knap" the glass at a molecular level using heat.
 * The Hook: Durn suspects that the reason the Xorn Younglings are so aggressive lately is that they are eating the "Yam-tempered" obsidian, which is far more nutritious to an elemental than raw rock.

⚠️ Environmental Hazard: The "Hot-Soil" Trap
Because Ash Yams require a thermal jumpstart, they often grow in "Heat Pockets"—patches of ground that look like normal ash but are actually 180°C just two inches down.
 * Detection: A player using a Xorn Toe will feel a massive "Twitch" near an Ash Yam patch, but the twitch is "Hot."
 * The Risk: Stepping into an Ash Yam patch without Orion's Yam Polish on your boots will melt your soles and deal 1d6 fire damage.
