The scald-scale serpent might have some magical properties that make it suitable for a magical garment.

The Scald-Scale Serpent skin is an alchemical goldmine because it bridges two opposing forces: Boiling Heat and Corrosive Salt. In a region like Hotshoe, where ash and acid are constant threats, a garment or weapon made from this skin is a top-tier survival tool.
Here are the potential magical properties and items that can be crafted from the serpent’s remains:
1. Armor: The "Brine-Warden" Leathers
Armor (Light or Medium), Rare
Crafted from the iridescent, salt-encrusted scales of the serpent, this armor is perpetually damp and cool to the touch.
 * Properties:
   * Thermal Regulation: You gain Resistance to Fire damage.
   * Caustic Rebuke: A creature that hits you with a melee attack while within 5 feet of you takes 1d4 Acid damage as the salt crystals on the armor react to the friction of the blow.
   * Slick Movement: You gain a Swim speed of 30 feet.
2. Weapon: The "Steam-Lash" Scimitar (or Whip)
Weapon (Any slashing), Uncommon
The blade or lash is inlaid with the serrated, hollow scales found along the serpent's spine, which store minute amounts of boiling brine.
 * Properties:
   * Boiling Edge: On a hit, the weapon deals an extra 1d4 Fire damage.
   * Blinding Spray (1/Day): As an action, you can flick the weapon to release a concentrated spray of hot salt. The target must succeed on a DC 12 Dexterity saving throw or be Blinded until the start of your next turn.
3. Wondrous Item: The "Vapor-Sight" Mantle
Wondrous Item (Cloak), Uncommon
A heavy cloak made from the serpent's underbelly. It feels slightly oily and smells strongly of the sea.
 * Properties:
   * Vibration Sense: While the hood is up and you are in a heavily obscured area (like thick ash or steam), you gain Blindsight out to 15 feet, sensing the vibrations in the air and floor.
   * Salt Stealth: You have Advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks while submerged in water or heavy mist.
🧪 Crafting Logistics in Hotshoe
Because the serpent is a CR 1/2 creature, harvesting the skin requires precision.
 * The Harvest: Requires a DC 13 Wisdom (Survival) check. On a failure, the "Brine Skin" acid stings the harvester for 1d6 acid damage, and the hide is ruined.
 * The Tannery Problem: Normal tanning oils won't work on Scald-Scale skin; they just slide off. Millicent Preston will tell you that you need Ash Yam Sap to "rough up" the scales so they can accept a magical enchantment.
 * Phanzis’s Contribution: Phanzis Guos is particularly interested in the scales because they hold a "memory of heat." He might offer to enchant the item for a lower cost if the players allow him to scrape off the "Salt-Gems" found near the serpent's throat for his Conducive Ink experiments.
⚠️ The Drawback: The "Drying" Curse
Just like the living serpent, these items are Water-Dependent.
 * Maintenance: If the item isn't soaked in saltwater (or treated with Orion's Yam Polish) once every 24 hours, it becomes brittle. While brittle, the armor's AC is reduced by 1, or the weapon loses its bonus fire damage, until it is rehydrated.

🧪 Material Utility & Market Value
1. Salt-Gems (The Heart of the Lake)
These are mineral-rich calcifications found in the serpent's gullet.
 * Utility: Can be crushed into Conducive Ink (Phanzis's favorite) or used as a "Snack" to peacefully distract a Xorn Youngling.
 * Value: 10 gp (Fragment) / 25 gp (Whole).
2. Intact Hide (Thermal Leather)
The primary material for the Brine-Warden Leathers or Steam-Lash.
 * Utility: Requires 1 Full Hide and 2 Sections to craft a complete set of armor.
 * Value: 30 gp (Full) / 10 gp (Section).
3. Boiling Brine (Liquid Fire)
Concentrated saltwater stored in the serpent’s throat that stays hot for 48 hours if kept in a lead-lined or magically insulated vial.
 * Utility: Can be used as a grenade (Range 10/20, 1d4 fire + 1d4 acid damage) or as a catalyst for Millicent's "Cinder-Sight Serum."
 * Value: 5 gp per vial.
🛠️ The "Crafting Bottleneck"
Even with a Pristine Hide, the party cannot simply sew a cloak. The salt crystals will shred normal thread. They need a Binder:
 * Needle Grass Fiber: Durn can show the players how to weave the metallic fibers of Needle Grass into a "Chain-Thread" that can hold the serpent scales together.
 * Ash Yam Treatment: To keep the hide from becoming Brittle, it must be soaked in a mixture of Ash Yam Sap and Lavender Oil for 24 hours during the crafting process.
> The Nightmare Factor: Jube Curnow warns that the scent of harvested serpent brine is a beacon for L’zoir swarms. If the party carries more than 3 vials of brine without sealing them in wax, they have Disadvantage on Stealth checks to avoid thermal-sensing predators.
