**Heated Body.** A creature that touches the snake or hits it with a melee attack while within 5 feet of it takes (1d4) fire damage minus DEX modifier.

### Actions

**Bite.** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. _Hit:_ `dice: 1d4` piercing damage **plus 1 fire damage**.

**Tail.** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target.  Hit:_ `dice: 1d4` bludgeoning damage **plus 2 fire damage**.

In D&D lore, Fire Snakes are actually the larval form of **Salamanders**. 

### Why it fits:

- **The "Heat Hunter" Synergy:** Like the L’zoir, Fire Snakes are attracted to heat. You might find them curled around a **Glowstone** or hiding inside the ribcage of an **Ash-Husk** to stay warm.
- **The "Touch" Hazard:** Contact with a Fire Snake hurts. This forces your players to think twice before rushing into melee range.
- **Movement:** They have a climb speed, allowing them to slither along the cavern ceilings alongside the flying L’zoir.

The Fire Snake (Lesser) is the perfect "Environmental Hazard" monster. While the Ash-Spore Husk blinds you for hitting it, the Fire Snake simply burns you for existing near it. At 9 HP, it is a glass cannon that specializes in deterrence. It doesn't need to kill a player to be effective; it just needs to make the players afraid to swing their swords.
Here is the combat strategy for these larval heat-seekers on the slopes of Mt. Conniption.
Combat Strategy: The Living Ember
1. The "Heated Body" Tax
The Fire Snake's most powerful tool is its passive Heated Body.
 * The Melee Deterrent: In D&D 5e, most Level 1 characters have a Dex modifier of +2 or +3. Since the damage is 1d4 - \text{Dex mod}, the snake might only do 1 or 2 damage per hit—but at Level 1, that "chip damage" adds up fast.
 * The Grapple Trap: If a player tries to grapple the snake to move it away from a fragile NPC like Dimble, they take the fire damage automatically.
2. Ceiling Stalker (Verticality)
With a 20 ft. Climb Speed, the Fire Snake should rarely be on the floor where it can be stepped on.
 * The "Drop" Attack: Use its Darkvision to wait on the ceiling of a volcanic tube. When a player passes underneath, the snake drops down.
 * Tail vs. Bite: * Use the Bite (+4 to hit) for consistent piercing.
   * Use the Tail if the player has resistance to piercing or if you want to narratively describe the snake "lashing out" with its heated body.
3. The "Heat-Sink" Ambush
As noted in your lore, these snakes hide inside Ash-Husks or around Glowstones.
 * The Hidden Heat: Place a Fire Snake inside the space of an Ash-Spore Husk. When the players hit the Husk and are blinded by the Smoldering Spores, the Fire Snake slithers out of the "corpse" to attack with Advantage.

Synergy: The Thermal Chain
The Fire Snake acts as a "Battery" for other Hotshoe creatures:
 * The Phoenix's Rest: Ash-Beak Phoenixes might nest near Fire Snakes. If a player attacks the nest, they have to deal with the diving birds and the burning snakes on the ground.
 * Durn's Shield: Durn might use his Mason’s Tools to create small "Snake-Vents" in a stone wall. He can poke his pry-bar through to fight, while the snakes provide a heated "no-man's-land" in front of his position.
 * Millicent’s Interest: Millicent Preston likely wants the venom or the heated scales for her potions. She might ask the party to "Live-Capture" one, which is a nightmare task given their Heated Body trait.
Roleplay Flavor: The Hiss of the Forge
 * The Sound: They don't just hiss; they sound like water hitting a hot frying pan—a sharp, steaming sssss.
 * Visuals: Their scales should look like cooling lava—black and crusted on the outside, but glowing with a bright orange light between the plates when they move.
 * The Larval Instinct: Describe them as being "mindless" in their pursuit of heat. They might ignore a player to coil around a torch or a wizard’s Light spell.
