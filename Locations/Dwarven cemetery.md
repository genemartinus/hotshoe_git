The daytime cemetery in Hotshoe is a landscape of brutal, geometric brilliance that punishes the unprotected eye. Unlike the soft, shaded graveyards of the lowlands, this "Dwarven Cemetery" is a sun-bleached expanse of volcanic glass and mineral-heavy stone that serves as a monument to the settlement's harsh reality.

The Blinding Canopy
The most dominant feature is the grove of Prismatic Willows that towers over the older plots. On a bright summer day, these trees provide the only safe shade in the wasteland. Their long, drooping branches are covered in leaves made of thin, reflective panes of volcanic mica. These glass leaves reflect 90% of the radiant heat, creating a shimmering, blinding dome that can be seen for miles. The ground beneath is insulated by a thick layer of these fallen reflective leaves, which clatter together in the wind with a beautiful, metallic chiming sound.
The Graves of Glass and Silt
The headstones reflect the stratified history of the hamlet:
 * The High-Mica Tombs: Wealthier dwarven families are buried under slabs of polished obsidian and mica-veined basalt that capture the sun, creating "Whiteout" glare points that make it difficult to identify details from a distance.
 * The Needle Grass Thickets: Between the monuments, patches of Needle Grass grow in the nutrient-rich ash. Their conductive iron blades glint with a sharp, silver light, hiding the dangerous 1d4 piercing hazard beneath a layer of deceptive beauty.
 * The Soft-Grave Perimeter: On the outskirts, the non-dwarven "Soft-Graves" are covered in waxy, silver-grey Cinder-Berry bushes. Their deep translucent orange berries resemble small glowing coals nestled in the silt, providing a stark color contrast to the monochrome ash.
Atmospheric Hazards
The daytime atmosphere is thick with more than just heat. The "Willow-Wind" often sheds tiny mica "dust" from the trees, creating a sparkling but lethal haze. Without a face mask, breathing in this environment leads to shortness of breath and chronic lung damage. The air shimmers with a heavy heat haze, making it difficult to distinguish a stationary, mineral-crusted Jade-Husk from a simple outcropping of glass-laden rock.

The **Hotshoe Cemetery** sits on a jagged shelf of Mt. Conniption, a stark, rectangular scar of "order" against the chaotic volcanic slope. Surrounded by a fence of **obsidian pickets**—sharpened bypass-waste from the old mines—the enclosure hums with a low, thermal vibration.

## **The Enclosure: The Iron & Glass Veil**

The obsidian pickets are tied together with rusted iron wire, and the gate is a heavy slab of basalt that requires a **DC 13 Strength (Athletics)** check to heave open. The ground inside is not grass, but a packed layer of dark, crushed pumice, meticulously leveled by the grieving.

## **The Headstones: Two Dozen Pillars of Memory**

There are exactly twenty-four headstones, arranged in three neat rows of eight.

- **The Material:** Each is a slab of grey granite, hauled up from the roots of the mountain before the "Jade-Glint" era.
    
- **The Decay:** Most of the names have been eroded by the "sandblasting" effect of the wind-blown ash, leaving only deep-cut Dwarven runes that **Helbo** or **Vem** can still trace with their fingers.
    
- **The Ritual:** At the base of each headstone is a small stone bowl meant for "Pure Water" offerings—now mostly filled with dry, grey soot.

## **The Sentinel: The Ash-Beak Phoenix**

Perched atop the tallest Frailejon is a bird the size of a hawk, its feathers shimmering like cooling embers.

- **Behavior:** It does not fly away when the party approaches. It watches with eyes that look like molten gold.
    
- **The Reaction:** If a character attempts to loot a grave or damage the obsidian fence, the Phoenix emits a piercing, ultrasonic shriek.
    
- **The Save:** **DC 12 Constitution**.
    
- **Effect:** On a failure, the character is **Deafened** for 1 minute. The shriek also acts as an alarm, potentially alerting the **Ash-Spore Husks** resting beneath the pumice.

The texture of the **Hotshoe Cemetery** is one of suffocating, monochromatic grit. Unlike a lush graveyard, this site is a study in **volcanic sedimentation**—a place where the line between the earth and the interred is blurred by a thick mantle of grey.

## **The Topography: A Basin of Bone-Grey Grit**

The ground is a deep, shifting bed of **pulverized pumice and airborne ash**. It does not crunch like gravel; it yields with a hollow, muffled thud under a boot, as if the mountain itself is trying to swallow the weight of the party.

- **The Texture:** The ash is fine as flour, coating the **obsidian pickets** in a velvety silver dust. In the corners of the enclosure, the wind has whipped the pumice into **miniature dunes**, partially submerging the lower headstones.
    
- **The Enclosure:** The obsidian pickets rise out of this grey sea like shards of frozen shadow. The contrast is stark: the absolute black of the volcanic glass against the lifeless, chalky grey of the silt.
    

## **The Headstones: Drowning in Silt**

The two dozen headstones are no longer standing tall. Over decades of eruptions, the "ground level" has risen.

- **The Submerged Memory:** Most stones are buried up to their "waists" in the pumice. The Dwarven runes are clogged with grit, requiring a character to physically scoop out the ash to read the names.
    
- **Thermal Veins:** Where the **Cinder Berry** bushes grow, the ash around their roots is scorched a dark, charred brown, creating "heat islands" in the otherwise uniform grey.

## **The Hotshoe Graveyard: The Mantle of Grit**

The ground of the Hotshoe Cemetery is a deep, shifting bed of **pulverized pumice and airborne ash**. It is a topography of suffocating grey. As seen in the environment of **image_0.png**, this ash is not topsoil; it is fine as flour, coating the surrounding rocks and debris in a velvety silver-grey dust. Footsteps are muffled, and moving more than 10 feet requires a **DC 10 Acrobatics** check just to keep from sinking calf-deep into the **pumice pits** (Difficult Terrain).

## **The "Decoration" of Obsidian Pickets**

The enclosure is defined by a fence of **obsidian pickets**, but as you noted, it is purely decorative. The slats are mismatched, irregular, and **hastily constructed**, rising out of the grey ash like jagged, frozen shadow. The front gate, made of this same chaotic, non-functional design, hangs crookedly, **permanently open** and clogged with a drift of silt. The pickets are tied together with rusted wire, which contrasts sharply against the absolute black of the glass and the grey of the ash.

## **The Graves & Headstones: The Disordered Rows**

There are two dozen headstones here, but they are "aligned" only in the loosest sense. Decades of eruptions and soil shifting have ruined all pretense of order.

- **Weathering:** The grey granite slabs are **severely weathered**. The names are sandblasted away, and the deep-cut Dwarven runes are choked with fine white dust, requiring a character to physically scoop out the ash to decipher them.
    
- **Decay:** Some stones have **fallen forward**, disappearing into the pumice. A few are **broken in half**, their jagged tops poking through the silt like broken teeth. Others lean at impossible angles, supported only by the pressure of the neighboring graves.
    
- **The "Resting":** Surprisingly, about half of the graves **appear undisturbed**. The ash covering them is smooth, suggesting that something (the mountain or the Sentinel) has seen fit to let those occupants rest in peace.

## **The Sentinel: The Ash-Beak Phoenix**

Perched directly atop a **broken headstone** is the **Ash-Beak Phoenix**, matching the exact visual of the largest bird in **image_0.png**.

- **Appearance:** It is a crow-like creature whose very feathers are made of sentient ash and cooling lava. Its eyes glow like **molten gold**, and a soft, rhythmic **pulse of ember-light** emanates from its chest.
    
- **The Presence:** A plume of ghostly **ash-smoke** (as seen in the photo) drifts constantly from its back. When it shifts its weight on the headstone, a cloud of fine white dust billows around it.
    
- **Behavior:** It does not attack on sight. It simply _watches_. As a Level 1 party, their first tactical challenge is to cross the non-closed gate and approach the cemetery without disturbing the **Dwarven Resting** by making too much noise (Stealth Check vs. Passive Perception 15).
