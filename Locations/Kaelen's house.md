Kaelen’s aspiration to become a "druid of Nerull" is a twisted interpretation of the god’s origins. Nerull was originally a human wizard who viewed death not as an end, but as a resource to be harvested for the preservation of creation. In Kaelen's mind, his work with the Hotshoe ecosystem—specifically the thermal-dependent Cinder-Berries and parasitic fungi—is a natural extension of Nerull’s original necromantic studies.
🏚️ Evidence in the Entryway
When the players walk through Kaelen’s front door, the signs of his devotion are subtle but pervasive. A successful DC 15 Wisdom (Perception) or Intelligence (Investigation) check reveals the following:
 * The Rust-Red Motif: While the house appears soot-stained, the rugs and wall-hangings are specifically rust-red, the traditional color of Nerull’s clergy.
 * Sickle "Tools": Several bone-handled sickles are hung by the door. While they look like gardening implements, they are arranged in a ritualistic pattern consistent with the "Reaper's Blade".
 * The "Mummified" Scent: A faint, sweet-rot smell lingers—not just of Cinder-Berries, but of the Carcass-Cap Fungus. The air feels heavy, as if the house itself is a "Living Conduit" to a colder place.
 * Mica-Dust Sigils: In the fine ash settled on the floorboards, a observant player might notice faint, circular patterns etched near the basement door—remnants of the "blood-soaked rituals" used to induct new cultists.
🕯️ The Hidden Altar: "The Hearth of Pluton"
Hidden behind a false wall of Ash Yam crates in the basement is Kaelen’s private sanctum. It is designed to mimic a shard of Pluton, Nerull’s gray realm.
The Altar's Appearance
 * The Material: The altar is a slab of jagged, unpolished obsidian from the Emerald Drift, stained a permanent, crusty brown from repeated sacrifices.
 * The Centerpiece: Atop the altar sits a crude, wooden carving of a skeletal figure in a cowl—the Reaper—clutching a miniature scythe made of Needle Grass.
 * The "Bound Soul" Lanterns: Hanging around the altar are three jars containing glowing, iridescent fungus. This is Kaelen’s attempt at a "Soul Binder" ritual; the spores inside pulse with a faint, rhythmic light as if mimicking a heartbeat.
 * The Sacred Text: A hand-copied scroll lies open, detailing the "Hymn of Nerull." It describes how the god shall "bind the forsaken dead in blood" to serve in the war against annihilation.
Mechanical Effects of the Altar
If the players enter this room, the "increased potency of necromantic magic" is palpable.
 * Necrotic Resonancy: Any spell that deals necrotic damage cast within 10 feet of the altar deals an additional 1d6 necrotic damage.
 * The Reaper's Gaze: Any creature of Good alignment feels a sense of "cold allure and colder pride" that imposes Disadvantage on Wisdom (Insight) checks while in the basement.
The Evidence of Betrayal: Among the offerings on the altar, the players might find a Leaded Glass Vial containing the "Ghost-Glow" spores, labeled with a name from the Soft-Grave—proof that Kaelen is using the dead of the hamlet to fuel his "druidic" ascension.
