## Description
Dimble's farm is an example of agriculture in the valley.  It is also part of the narrative about how Dimble is able to brew and sell ale at the [[Thirsty Pebble]].  The key to the narrative is the emphasis placed on the nature of the fermenter's pebble stone, a culinary variety of the alchemist's stone.  If the pebble produces ale out of any liquid then Dimble uses some ash yam skins to give it a 'local flavor' and avoid suspicion, especially from [[Millicent]].  She is quite keen to figure out how Dimble manages brewing with such limited acreage.  There's also the matter of a water source not to mention hops, unless that is the role played by the roasted ash yam skins.  The alternative is Dimble is doing all the fermenting and the role of the pebble is to 'upgrade' the ale to award winning quality with the yam skins sprinkled in afterward.
