This setup adds a perfect layer of "Small Town Debt" to the party’s arrival. **Phanzis** isn't just a landlord; he’s a man trying to recoup a loss, and the players represent a "security deposit" that can actually fight back.

## **The "Vegetable Garden Problem"**

Phanzis’s garden isn't growing carrots and peas; it’s a high-maintenance patch of **Thermal Lichen** and **Ash-Tubers** that are essential for the town's survival.

**The Hook:**

_"I’ve got a patch of Blue-Cap Lichen behind the house. It’s the only thing that keeps the lung-fever away in Hotshoe. But lately, something’s been... 'pruning' it. Not eating it. Just cutting it and leaving it to rot in the ash. I think it’s a **Jade-Glint Beetle**, or worse, a **Ghost-Ash Drifter** that’s taken a liking to the heat from my pipes."_

#### **Combat/Skill Encounter: The Midnight Pruner**

If the players agree to stay, they’ll have to pull "Garden Duty" during their first Long Rest.

| **Skill/Action**            | **Mechanic** | **Result**                                                                                                              |
| --------------------------- | ------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Thomasine (Perception)**  | **DC 15**    | She notices the "scent" of ozone before the creature strikes. The party isn't Surprised.                                |
| **Brevil (Athletics)**      | **DC 12**    | She can try to "pin" the creature against the basalt walls so it doesn't escape into the vents.                         |

## **Phanzis: The "Accidental Patron"**

**Voice/Vibe:** An older, weary man with ash-stained hands and a nervous habit of counting on his fingers. He’s Lawful Neutral—he wants his money back, but he doesn't want to see anyone die to get it.

- **His Offer:** _"Stay as long as you like. Keep the 'pruners' out of my caps, and maybe we can call the first month's 'rent' even. If you prove you can handle the mountain... well, I know a few folks with heavier problems and deeper pockets."_

---

## **DM Narrative Note: The "Leftovers"**

Inside the house, have the players find one item left by the previous owners:

> **A discarded journal** with the last entry reading: _"The wind doesn't just blow the ash; it speaks in the voices of the people we buried yesterday. We’re leaving before we start answering back."_

This reinforces the weight of fear carried by the setting and gives players a mystery to investigate beyond just fixing the door.

This single-story dwelling perfectly fits the "unfinished, yet serviceable" requirement for Hotshoe. Phanzis’s offer is essentially: "The roof only leaks when the ash is heavy, but you can see the sky."

---

## **The House: A Study in Volcanic Thrift**

The structure is a study in monochromatic survival. As seen in the reference, it is a low-slung building made of **irregular basalt blocks**, mortar-sealed with a thick, grey slurry of volcanic ash. It looks like it grew out of the pumice itself, crouching against the wind of Mt. Conniption.

**The "Unfinished" Elements (The Structural Holes):**

- **The Roof:** This is the most glaring unfinished element. The underlying structure is present, but the **thatch-and-straw layer** only covers about two-thirds of the roof. The final section (perhaps over the designated 'sleeping area') is just a mesh of **exposed, charred timber beams**, leaving that part of the interior open to the ash-fall. Phanzis might offer them a giant, oil-soaked tarp to cover the gap "until the next shipment comes in."
- **The Windows:** The frames and shutters are entirely absent, leaving gaping, rectangular **voids in the stone walls**. There is no glass in Hotshoe. **Brevil** or **Harsent** would have to hang heavy blankets or scavenge sheets of metal to keep the biting cold and the **Ghost-Ash Drifters** out at night.
    
## **Environmental Impact on the House**

Because of its unfinished state, the house itself behaves like a very static environmental hazard.

|**Feature**|**Mechanic**|**Narrative Effect**|
|---|---|---|
|**Exposure**|**+1 Daily Strain**|Because of the open roof section and the open windows, the party cannot fully escape the **Ash-Fall** here. Their daily Exhaustion Save DC increases by 1 until they patch the roof and cover the windows.|
|**Unframed Windows**|**Disadvantage on Stealth**|If the party tries to hide _inside_ the house at night, any light source (like **Emerick’s** _Light_ spell) streams out clearly, alerting the **Ash-Beak Phoenixes**.|
|**"The Ash-Moat"**|**Difficult Terrain**|The constant wind has piled a deep dune of fine white ash against the front door, requiring a **DC 11 Strength** check to force it open from the outside.|

## **The "Safe" Zones**

- **The Hearth:** At the very back of the single room is a simple, basalt-slab hearth. A small **thermal pipe** sticks out of the ground nearby, offering a localized point of heat (this is where Phanzis sleeps).
- **The "Nook":** The far-left corner is the _only_ space with a complete roof and three walls. This is where players can set up a safe, soot-free sleeping spot.

## **DM Narrative Tip: The "Inventory of Loss"**

When the party first enters, have them find exactly three things left behind by the couple who "skipped town":
1. **A pile of uncarved, grey granite headstones** (they were stonecutters).
2. **A single, perfectly preserved child’s boot** covered in fine ash.
3. **A half-finished _Protection from Evil_ circle** drawn in chalk on the basalt floor, hastily smeared.
This immediately tells the party that Hotshoe’s "horror" is personal and very close.

## **DM Script for Entering the House**

> _"You push through the deep drift of grey pumice that cokes the entryway. It is silent inside. While the stone walls (basalt blocks sealed with grey slurry) keep out the biting cold, the fine white ash-fall has found a way in. It coats the earthen floor like a fine snow. You look up and see that while the thatch roof is mostly complete, a narrow gap of charred timber beams leaves the left corner exposed. The gaping windows have no glass, allowing the brilliant, refracted light from the nearby Prismatic Willow to pulse with gold and blue light across the walls."_

## **What the Party Finds Inside (Choose 3):**

1. **The 'Missing' Key:** A heavy brass key, deliberately left on a basalt-block table, marked with the Dwarven rune for 'Debt'.
    
2. **The Preserved Boot:** A single, perfectly preserved child’s boot covered in fine ash (reinforcing the setting's pathos).
    
3. **The Smudged Circle:** A half-finished _Protection from Evil_ circle drawn in chalk on the floor, hastily smeared (suggesting the previous owners left out of fear).
    
4. **The Granite Headstones:** A pile of uncarved, rough grey granite slabs, confirming the original owners were stonecutters who "skipped town" before they could build their family plot.
