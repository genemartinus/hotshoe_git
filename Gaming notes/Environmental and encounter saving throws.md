
To make the **Hotshoe** environment feel as dangerous as the monsters inhabiting it, you can introduce hazards that target different Saving Throws. This ensures that every character—from the sturdy **Hara** to the agile **Harsent**—has a moment where their specific strengths (or weaknesses) matter.

Here are four additional environmental hazards for the slopes of **Mt. Conniption**:

---
## **The Choking Ash (Ongoing Hazard)**

Every 10 minutes of heavy exertion (like combat), have players make a **DC 10 Constitution Save**.

- **Failure:** The character is _Poisoned_ (disadvantage on attacks/checks) until they drink clean water or move to an indoor area.
    
- _Tip:_ **Helbo** and **Hara** have advantage on this because of their Dwarven/Gnome resilience!
    

## **Jade-Glint Obsidian (The "Spicy" Terrain)**

The ground is littered with poisonous volcanic glass.

- **Mechanical Effect:** If a character is shoved prone or falls, they take $1d4$ slashing damage + $1d4$ poison damage.
    
- **Strategy:** Remind **Vem** and **Brevil** they can _Shove_ enemies onto these shards for extra damage.

## **1. Thermal Vents (Dexterity Save)**

The ground in the Kantzai Frontier is thin. Steaming fissures can erupt without warning, especially during combat when the ground is being stomped on.

- **The Trigger:** A natural 1 is rolled on an attack, or a heavy spell (like _Thunderclap_) is cast.
- **The Effect:** A 5-foot-wide geyser of scalding steam erupts.
- **The Save:** **DC 12 Dexterity**.
- **Result:** $1d6$ fire damage on a failure, or half as much on a success.
- **Tactical Tip:** **Vem** or **Hara** can use their high AC to stand _over_ a vent to block it, or **Assunta** can use her whip to trip an enemy into one.

## **2. Jade-Glint Radiance (Charisma/Wisdom Save)**

The "sickly green" light of the Jade-Glint obsidian isn't just physical; it’s an epistemological "virus" that attacks the mind's ability to perceive reality.

- **The Trigger:** Spending more than an hour in a cavern heavily encrusted with Jade-Glint.
- **The Save:** **DC 11 Charisma** (to maintain a sense of self) or **Wisdom** (to maintain a sense of reality).
- **Result:** On a failure, the character suffers from **"The Ash-Blur."** They have disadvantage on **Perception** and **Insight** checks for $1d4$ hours as their thoughts become clouded by the mountain's "conflicting truths."
- **The Hero:** **Emerick** (with his +6 CHA save) and **Helbo** are the party's anchors here.

## **3. Static Ash Storms (Strength Save)**

As volcanic ash rubs together in high winds, it creates intense static electricity. These "dry storms" can physically push a person off a ledge.

- **The Trigger:** A sudden gust of wind on a narrow mountain pass.
- **The Save:** **DC 13 Strength (Athletics)**.
- **Result:** On a failure, the character is pushed 5 feet in the direction of the wind. If they are near a ledge, they must make an additional **DC 10 Dexterity Save** to grab the side.
- **The Hero:** **Brevil** (with her Athletics expertise) can literally hold the party together by grabbing those who slip.

## **4. Sulfur Pocket (Constitution Save)**

Heavier-than-air gases settle in low-lying valleys and trenches. They are invisible and smell like rotten eggs.

- **The Trigger:** Entering a low-lying area or a basement in the hamlet of Hotshoe.
- **The Save:** **DC 12 Constitution**.
- **Result:** On a failure, the character is **Stunned** until the end of their next turn as they retch and gasp for air.
- **The Hero:** **Thomasine** has a high **Passive Perception (16)**; you might rule that she can "smell" the danger before the party walks into it, giving them a chance to avoid the save entirely.

## **DM Tool: The "Ash-Dial"**

Consider keeping a "Danger Die" ($1d6$) on your table. Every time the players take a Short Rest in the wilds, roll it. On a **1**, one of these hazards occurs immediately, interrupting their rest and forcing them to find a more secure "thermal-shielded" location.

In the **Hotshoe** ecosystem, the "Ash and Water" conflict has produced life forms that are as much environmental hazards as they are traditional monsters. Because many of these creatures are thermal-based or "Jade-Glint" corrupted, their attacks often target the mind or the lungs rather than just physical Armor Class.

Here is the encounter summary for the flora and fauna of **Mt. Conniption**.

## **Strategic Combat Notes for the DM**

#### **1. The Prismatic Willow (The "Flashbang" Tree)**

These trees thrive near Jade-Glint veins. They don't move, but they "pulse" when they sense vibration.

- **The Interaction:** If **Harsent** or **Vem** misses a melee attack while standing near one, the vibration triggers a flash.
    
- **The Hero:** **Thomasine** has high Perception and can spot the "rhythm" of the pulses, allowing her to warn the party to close their eyes (granting Advantage on the save).
    

#### **2. Ash-Spore Husks (The Silent Killers)**

These are the remnants of miners or travelers preserved by volcanic ash. They release a cloud of fine soot when hit.

- **The Interaction:** This is a nightmare for **Emerick** and **Helbo**. If they fail the **CON save**, they lose their ability to use _Dissonant Whispers_ or _Healing Word_ because they are too busy coughing to speak the verbal components.
    
- **The Hero:** **Hara** and **Helbo** (Dwarven/Gnome resilience) are statistically more likely to keep their breath and stay in the fight.
    

#### **3. The Cinder-Berry Bush (The Sticky Trap)**

These bushes look like glowing embers. They are the "landmines" of the Kantzai Frontier.

- **The Interaction:** Moving through a space occupied by Cinder-Berries requires a save. If **Brevil** or **Assunta** is grappled, they lose their mobility, making them easy targets for other monsters.
    
- **The Hero:** **Harsent** can use his **Acrobatics** to move through the bushes without triggering the vines (DM's discretion to allow an Acrobatics check instead of a Dex save).
    

#### **4. The Ghost-Ash Drifter (The Mind-Bender)**

These are translucent, floating jellyfish-like creatures made of sentient ash. They represent the "Post-Truth" horror of the mountain.

- **The Interaction:** Their touch doesn't hurt the body; it hurts the memory.
    
- **The Hero:** **Emerick** is the only one with a high enough **CHA/INT** focus to truly "debate" these creatures or resist their mental drain.
    

---

## **DM Narrative Tip: "The Tally"**

When a character fails a save against a **Jade-Glint Beetle** or a **Ghost-Ash Drifter**, don't just say they take a penalty. Describe it as their _certainty_ wavering.

> _"Emerick, as the beetle's wings buzz, you suddenly can't remember if the water you're protecting is actually pure, or if you've been lied to by Warden Stefania. Your hand trembles as you aim your spell."_
