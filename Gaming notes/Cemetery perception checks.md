The Hotshoe Cemetery (the "Dwarven Cemetery") is a transition zone where the geological instability of Mt. Conniption meets the restless history of the miners. Because the landscape is dominated by reflective mica and heat-syncing basalt, your perception needs change drastically depending on the position of the sun.

☀️ Daytime Perception: The "Blinding White"
During the day, the primary challenge is Visual Overload. The sun reflects off the Prismatic Willows and the glass-veined headstones, creating a "Whiteout" effect.
| DC | Sensory Target | Tactical Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| 12 | Heat Haze Distortion | Identifying the difference between a stationary "Jade-Husk" and a shimmering heat mirage. Failure: The party is Surprised by a "dormant" undead. |
| 15 | Needle Grass Shadows | Detecting the thin, dark lines of Needle Grass hidden in the glare of the white ash. Failure: 1d4 piercing damage and a potential "Tetanus" save. |
| 18 | Mica-Flash Patterns | Spotting the rhythmic "flicker" of a Xorn’s eye as it peeks through a reflective obsidian slab. |
The "Glare" Penalty: Creatures without Mica-Lens Goggles have Disadvantage on visual Perception checks that rely on spotting fine details further than 30 feet away.

🌙 Nighttime Perception: The "Silent Tremor"
At night, the cemetery cools rapidly, but the stones retain a "Ghost-Heat." Here, the challenge shifts from Sight to Sound and Vibration.
| DC | Sensory Target | Tactical Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| 10 | The Chiming Wind | Hearing the "clatter" of Prismatic Willow leaves. If the chiming stops suddenly, it indicates a large creature (like a Scald-Scale Serpent) has entered the grove. |
| 14 | Subterranean Thrum | Feeling the rhythmic vibration of Xorn Younglings moving through the "hollow" burial chambers beneath your feet. |
| 17 | The Scent of "Burnt Sugar" | Detecting the faint smell of Cinder-Berries being crushed nearby. This usually means a scavenger is feeding or a trap has been tripped. |
The "Trance" Advantage: Vem and Emerick do not suffer the -5 penalty to Passive Perception for being "Asleep," making them the only ones capable of hitting the DC 17 check during a Long Rest.

🔍 Search Actions (Active Investigation)
If the players are actively searching the graves for Phanzis’s Ink or Milton’s Pigskin, use these specialized checks:
 * Intelligence (Investigation) - "The Ash-Read": DC 13. Looking at the way ash has settled on a tombstone. Freshly disturbed ash indicates a Lesser Jade-Husk recently crawled out.
 * Wisdom (Survival) - "Thermal Tracking": DC 15. Placing a hand on the basalt. A stone that is colder than the surrounding earth indicates a hollow chamber or a "Blue-Vain" of cooling minerals below.
 * Intelligence (History) - "Dwarven Masonry": DC 14 (Helbo has Advantage). Identifying which graves belong to the "Founding Masons." These graves are usually built with Needle Grass-reinforced mortar and are less likely to collapse.

Kaelen’s presence near the Secondary Cemetery—often called the "Soft-Grave" or the "Fallow-Plot" where the non-dwarven residents are buried—is a masterclass in hidden agendas. While the Dwarven Cemetery is all stone and glass, the Soft-Grave has more actual soil, making it a breeding ground for the Cinder-Berry bushes and, more importantly, Kaelen’s secret fungus.
Here are the perception checks that would alert the players that Kaelen is doing more than just "pruning the thorns."

🔍 Environmental Perception (Spotting the "Anomalies")
| DC | Sensory Target | Tactical/Story Insight |
|---|---|---|
| 12 | The "Inverse" Bloom | Spotting that the Cinder-Berries near Kaelen’s hut are glowing less than the ones further away. Insight: Something in the soil is "drinking" the thermal energy before the berries can. |
| 14 | The Damp-Patch | Noticing a 10-foot radius of ground that is unnaturally dark and moist. In Hotshoe, water evaporates in minutes; for the ground to stay damp, someone is intentionally "misting" it. |
| 16 | The "Drift-Pattern" | Seeing fine, pale-blue dust settling against the wind on the headstones. Insight: These aren't mineral particles; they are spores with a limited, semi-sentient flight path. |
👃 Olfactory & Auditory Checks (The "Senses of the Dark")
Since the fungus thrives in the "cool" shadows of the graves, players might smell or hear the harvest before they see it.
 * DC 13 Wisdom (Perception) - "The Bitter Almonds": The air near the graves doesn't smell like sulfur or woodsmoke. It smells like bitter almonds and rot. This is the scent of Carcass-Cap Fungus, which Kaelen is likely using to ferment his "Vespertine Jam."
 * DC 15 Wisdom (Perception) - "The Hissing Earth": If a player stands perfectly still, they hear a faint, rhythmic hissing sound—like a slow leak from a steam vent. The Reality: This is the sound of fungal spores being forcibly ejected from their "fruiting bodies" beneath the soil.

🕵️ Investigation & Medicine (Analyzing Kaelen's "Work")
If the players look closely at Kaelen’s tools or the plants themselves, they can perform active checks:
 * Intelligence (Investigation) - "The Tool-Trace": DC 14. Kaelen’s pruning shears are stained with a sticky, iridescent purple resin that doesn't match the silver-grey of the Cinder-Berry leaves.
 * Intelligence (Medicine) - "The Spore-Rash": DC 15. Brevil or Helbo might notice that Kaelen has small, circular "burns" on his forearms. They aren't from heat; they are fungal parasitic marks where spores have tried to take root in his skin.
 * Intelligence (Nature) - "The Root-Graft": DC 17. Identifying that Kaelen has been grafting Needle Grass roots onto the fungus. This "armors" the fungus against the heat, allowing it to survive in the open wasteland.

🎭 The "Amiss" Moment
If a player hits a DC 18 Perception check while Kaelen is "working," they see him carefully scraping a glowing, translucent film off the underside of a headstone and placing it into a Leaded Glass Vial—the same kind Phanzis uses for his ink.
The Implication: Kaelen isn't just a gardener; he is a bio-thief. He is harvesting "Ghost-Glow" spores, which feed on the residual memories (or psychic energy) of the deceased.
