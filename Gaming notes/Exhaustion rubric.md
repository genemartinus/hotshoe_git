
In the **Hotshoe Saga**, the environment of **Mt. Conniption** is a character in its own right. Standard 5e exhaustion rules can feel a bit flat for a high-stakes "Ash and Water" campaign, so this rubric is designed to make the survival of **Harsent, Thomasine, Assunta, Brevil, Emerick, Hara, Helbo, and Vem** feel like a tactical puzzle.

The goal is to move from **Level 0 (Fresh)** to **Level 6 (Dead)** based on the daily choices the party makes.

---

## **The Hotshoe Travel Rubric**

At the end of every 8 hours of travel (or one full day), the DM calculates the **Exhaustion Score**. If the score is **positive**, the character must make a **Constitution Saving Throw**.

## **Step 1: Calculate the Daily Strain Score**

Start at **0** and add/subtract based on the table below:

|**Category**|**Condition**|**Strain Modifier**|
|---|---|---|
|**Supplies**|**Clean Water** (1 gallon/day)|-1 (Success) / +2 (Failure)|
||**Rations** (Full meal)|-1 (Success) / +1 (Failure)|
|**Exertion**|**Normal Pace** (8 hours)|+0|
||**Forced March** (Each hour past 8)|+1 per hour|
||**Heavy Armor** (Hara/Vem in Splint/Chain)|+1|
|**Weather**|**Heavy Ash Fall** (Choking air)|+1|
||**Thermal Heat Wave** (Mt. Conniption active)|+1|
|**Gear**|**Ash-Filter Mask** (Brevil/Thomasine)|-1|
||**Alchemical Cooling Gel** (Emerick/Helbo)|-1|

---

## **Step 2: The "Threshold" Save**

After calculating the **Total Strain**, the character makes a **CON Save**:

- **DC = 10 + Total Strain Score.**
    
- **On a Failure:** Gain **1 Level of Exhaustion**.
    
- **On a Critical Failure (Natural 1):** Gain **2 Levels of Exhaustion**.
    

---

## **Exhaustion Levels (Hotshoe Variant)**

In the Kantzai Frontier, exhaustion isn't just "being tired"—it's the physical and mental breakdown of the body as the mountain's "Post-Truth" environment takes hold.

|**Level**|**Effect**|**Narrative Description**|
|---|---|---|
|**1**|Disadvantage on Ability Checks|**"The Lead-Limb":** Your muscles ache; even simple tasks like foraging feel heavy.|
|**2**|Speed Halved|**"Ash-Lung":** Every breath is a struggle; you can no longer keep pace.|
|**3**|Disadvantage on Attacks & Saves|**"Jade-Glint Tremor":** Your vision blurs; you can't tell the difference between a shadow and a foe.|
|**4**|Hit Point Maximum Halved|**"Organ Failure":** The sulfur and heat are literal poisons now.|
|**5**|Speed Reduced to 0|**"The Statue":** You collapse into the ash, unable to move.|
|**6**|Death|**"Claimed by the Mountain":** You become an Ash-Spore Husk.|

---

## **Tactical Character Roles for Travel**

To avoid a "death spiral," the party must use their Level 1 skills to mitigate these Strain scores:

- **Thomasine (Survival +4):** She can forage for **Ash-Resistant Flora**. A successful DC 15 Survival check can find enough "Water-Vine" to negate the **+2 Water Failure** for 2 party members.
    
- **Vem & Hara (High CON Saves):** As the "Tanks," they should carry the heaviest gear. Their +4 and +3 CON saves mean they can often fail the "Supplies" check but still succeed on the Save.
    
- **Helbo (Performance/Inspiration):** During the evening rest, Helbo can play her harp. Any ally who listens gains a **$d6$ Bardic Inspiration** to use on their **CON Save** at the end of the day.
    
- **Brevil (Thieves' Cant/Contacts):** If traveling through settled areas of the Kantzai Frontier, Brevil can find "Black Market" filters or stolen rations that don't appear on standard maps.
    

---

## **DM Tip: The "Ash-Rest" Mechanic**

To remove **1 Level of Exhaustion**, a character needs a **Long Rest (8 hours)** and **Full Rations/Water**.

- If they are in the **Hamlet of Hotshoe** (Safety), they remove **2 Levels**.
    
- If they are sleeping on the **Open Slope** (Danger), they must succeed on a **DC 12 Survival check** just to remove **1 Level**.
