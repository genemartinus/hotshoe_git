2. Brevil (The Scalpel)
Why she’s essential:
 * Safety Specialist: In the Emerald Drift, using fire is a death sentence due to Reactive Gas. Brevil’s **Blowgun and Shortsword deal high physical and poison damage** without triggering explosions.

 * The Alchemist: She is the only PC with the "kit" to actually use the Salt-Gems and Jade-Glint to upgrade the party’s weapons, fulfilling the goal of improving magical conductive ink.
3. Vem (The Anvil)
Why he’s essential:
 * Thermal Protection: With **AC 19 and the Shield Wall action**, Vem is the only thing standing between the "Glass Cannons" and a Scald-Scale Serpent.

 * The Sentry: His **Eladrin Trance** is the only defense against being ambushed by Xorn Younglings while the rest of the party is recovering from the grueling heat.
🛠️ Why this team works together:
 * The Tactical Loop: Vem holds the narrow mine entrance (Shield Wall). Helbo uses Guidance or Inspiration on Brevil. Brevil uses her Blowgun to snip a high-value target from behind Vem’s shield.
 * The Hazard Loop: If a Jade-Husk ruptures, Helbo resists the poison, Vem halves the damage, and Brevil uses her high Dexterity save to dodge the glass shards.

📉 The "Least Effective" Trio: Emerick, Brevil, and Hara
1. The "Glass & Spore" Fragility
 * The Conflict: Emerick (13 HP) and Brevil (16 HP) are both physically fragile. While Hara is a sturdy "Gnome Grenade," her 25 ft. speed and lack of Shield Wall (as a primary action) mean she cannot effectively "peel" enemies off two different mobile skirmishers at once.
 * The Hotshoe Hazard: If a Jade-Husk or Ash-Spore Husk explodes near this group, Emerick and Brevil lack the Dwarven Resilience (Poison resistance) that Helbo and Vem possess. A single failed Con save could leave the party's primary damage-dealers Poisoned and at a massive disadvantage for the rest of the crawl.
2. The "Reactive Gas" Explosion Risk
 * The Conflict: Emerick relies on Infestation and potentially other fire/energy-based spells. In the Emerald Drift, where the air is pressurized with Reactive Gas, Emerick is a walking liability.
 * The Mechanical Clash: Brevil needs to be close to targets to use her Shortsword and Assassinate feature. If Emerick triggers a gas pocket or a Husk's Reactive Gas trait while trying to help her, he will likely down his own ally.
3. The "Social & Detection" Blind Spot
 * The Conflict: Hara and Brevil both have Stealth Disadvantage due to their armor (Ring mail for Hara, though she has Stone Camouflage; Studded Leather for Brevil is fine, but Hara’s clanking is the issue).
 * The NPC Friction: This group lacks a Dwarf. In Hotshoe, Obur, Glour, and Durn are the gatekeepers to the best gear and information. While Helbo or Vem (as a high-status Eladrin) can negotiate with the "Old Guard," this trio consists of a "Pocket full of poison," a "Glass Cannon," and a "Gnome Grenade." To the suspicious residents of Hotshoe, they look like a group of looters rather than a rescue or research party.
🚧 Why They Struggle to Support Each Other:
 * The "Short-Range" Problem: Brevil wants to be within 5 feet for Sneak Attack. Hara wants to be within 5 feet for Shield Bash. Both are now in the "Blast Zone" of the Ash Husks.
 * The "Psychic Static" Failure: Emerick’s Mind Sliver is great, but it doesn't help Hara or Brevil resist the Caustic Ash or Scavenger's Curse. They have no way to "buff" their saving throws against the environment, unlike Helbo’s Guidance or Inspiration.
 * The Xorn Buffet: Hara and Brevil carry a fair amount of metal. Without a dedicated "Roadblock" like Vem to take the hits, a Xorn Youngling will bypass the small Hara and go straight for the "soft" Emerick to get to the minerals in the party's packs.
📜 The "Survival Odds" Verdict
This team would likely succeed in a standard urban or forest dungeon, but in Hotshoe, they are too susceptible to environmental attrition. They would spend more time recovering from Jade-Glint poisoning and Needle Grass splinters than they would harvesting chemical ingredients for magical ink.
