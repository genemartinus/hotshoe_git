Traveling across the Hotshoe valley is a constant negotiation with the environment’s thermodynamic extremes. The shift from the "Eruption Zone" heat to the high-altitude cold of the Paramo means players must manage their body temperature and physical strain through specific gear and behaviors.
In D&D 5e terms, these measures provide mechanical Advantage on saving throws against Exhaustion or allow for the Removal of Exhaustion levels during short or long rests.
1. Thermal Regulation: The "Wet-Husk" and "Soot-Slicker"
To combat the daytime heat and the "Whiteout" glare of the obsidian mines, players use specialized protective layers.
 * The Wet-Husk Mask: A multi-layered cloth mask soaked in a solution of crushed Steam-Lily nectar and water.
   * The Relief: Provides Advantage on Constitution saves against Exhaustion caused by "Mica-Dust" inhalation or sulfurous fumes.
   * The Contribution: If the mask dries out (after 4 hours of travel), the player immediately takes a -2 penalty to Passive Perception due to coughing and eye irritation.
 * The Soot-Slicker Poncho: A heavy, treated leather cloak that reflects radiant heat during the day and traps body heat at night.
   * The Relief: Wearing a slicker allows a player to ignore the effects of "Extreme Heat" during the day and "Extreme Cold" at night, preventing the hourly Exhaustion checks normally required by the environment.
2. High-Altitude Logistics: The "Slow Convoy"
Drawing from the historical record of the Pico de Orizaba expedition, distance in Hotshoe is measured in "Vertical Effort" rather than horizontal miles.
 * Acclimatization Rests: For every 1,000 feet of elevation climbed (from the hamlet to the Eruption Zone), the party must spend 1 hour in a "Base Camp" or a sheltered "High-Mica Tomb."
   * The Relief: Success prevents the "Altitude Sickness" version of Exhaustion, which reduces a player's speed by half.
   * The Contribution: Forcing a march without these rests increases the DC of the nightly Exhaustion save by +2 for every 1,000 feet climbed.
 * Pack Animal Support: Using Iron-Sow pigs or mountain-bred burros to carry gear (the "Vertical Convoy").
   * The Relief: Carrying less than half your capacity allows you to recover two levels of Exhaustion during a Long Rest instead of one, provided you have enough Slag-Brick fuel for a warm meal.
3. Chemical & Biological Support
The unique flora of the valley provides the "fuel" for physical endurance.
 * Ash Yam Foraging: These nutrient-dense tubers are the primary source of "Slow-Release" energy.
   * The Relief: Eating a cooked Ash Yam during a Short Rest allows a player to roll one additional Hit Die for healing without spending the die.
 * Cinder-Slake "Hot-Packs": Using the exothermic reaction of mineral salts to keep extremities warm during the night-chill.
   * The Relief: Placing a Cinder-Slake pouch in one's bedroll grants a +5 bonus to the Constitution save against Exhaustion during a Long Rest in "Extreme Cold" conditions.
