🔍 Encounter Deep-Dives
1. The Ash-Husk Thicket (Tactical Lesson: Blinding)
 * Setup: The players spot what looks like two fallen travelers covered in grey soot.
 * The Twist: The Dust Mephit isn't attacking yet; it’s using the Help action to ensure the Husks stay hidden.
 * Goal: Teach players to use ranged attacks. If they "pop" the Husks from 10 feet away, they avoid the Smoldering Spores. If they rush in, the Mephit drops Color Spray to ensure the whole party is blinded.
2. The Thermal Siphon (The "Cold" Truth)
 * Setup: The air suddenly turns freezing. Dimble is shivering, his "soldier-like posture" failing as the L'zoir drain his body heat.
 * The Twist: Dimble’s ale is actually chilling the air further, making the L'zoir sluggish (reduce their fly speed to 30 ft).
 * Goal: Introduce the Heat Thirst mechanic. Players can distract the L'zoir by lighting a torch or a campfire 30 feet away, drawing the "thermal vampires" off the gnome.
3. The Mineral Toll (Resource Management)
 * Setup: The ground rumbles. A Xorn Youngling emerges, pointing a stony claw at the Fighter's plate armor or the Rogue's daggers.
 * The Twist: It doesn't want to fight; it's just hungry. It speaks Terran (which Durn can translate if he's nearby).
 * Goal: A "Social Combat" encounter. Players must decide if their gold is worth more than their HP. If they fight, emphasize the Xorn's Nonmagical Resistance to show them that Mt. Conniption's "Elite" monsters are built differently.
🛠️ DM Tips for Level 1 in Hotshoe
 * The "Half-HP" Rule: Because your monsters (like the Phoenix and Mephit) have very low HP, consider having them flee or "dissipate" into ash once they take any damage. This keeps the game moving and prevents a lucky crit from feeling like a "waste" of a cool monster.
 * Environmental Cues: Use the Thermal Senses. Tell the players, "You feel a sudden warmth on your ankles," before a Fire Snake strikes, or "The smell of old salt fills the air," before the Scald-Scale Serpent sprays.
