---

**Brine Skin.** While the serpent is in saltwater, it has advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks. Additionally, any creature that touches the serpent or hits it with a melee attack while within 5 feet of it must succeed on a **DC 11 Constitution saving throw** or be "Stung" by the salt-crystals on its skin, taking `dice: 1d4`acid damage.
**Water-Dependent.** The serpent can breathe both air and water, but it must submerge itself in saltwater once every hour or its skin becomes brittle, reducing its AC by 2 until it rehydrates.

### Actions

**Boiling Bite.** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. _Hit:_ `dice: 1d4+2` piercing damage plus 2 (1d4) fire damage from the boiling water stored in its throat.

**Salt Spray (Recharge 5–6).** The serpent exhales a 15-foot cone of pressurized, boiling saltwater. Each creature in that area must make a **DC 11 Dexterity saving throw**. On a failure, a creature takes `dice: 2d4` fire damage and is **Blinded** until the start of its next turn as salt stings its eyes. On a success, it takes half damage and isn't blinded.

### Why it fits the Ecology:

- **The Salt-Fire Cycle:** These serpents often sun themselves on the "Black Sand" beaches created by cooling lava. When the **Ash-Beak Phoenix** tries to dive for them, the serpent sprays boiling brine to disorient the bird.
- **The L'zoir Interaction:** The **L'zoir** (Heat Drinkers) often hover over the boiling lakes to feed on the steam. The Scald-Scale Serpent views the L'zoir as "flying snacks" and will leap out of the water to snatch them from the air.
- **The Xorn Connection:** Xorns hate these lakes because the salt corrodes their stony hides, but they will occasionally hunt the serpents for the mineral-rich "Salt-Gems" that grow in the serpent's stomach.


The Scald-Scale Serpent is a formidable "Hazard Boss" for a Level 1 party. With 22 HP, it is significantly tankier than the other Hotshoe denizens, and its Brine Skin creates a "damned if you do, damned if you don't" scenario for melee fighters.
In the boiling, salt-crusted lakes of Mt. Conniption, this serpent is the apex predator of the shallows.
Combat Strategy: The Boiling Ambush
1. The "Salt-Stealth" Opener
The serpent should never be seen before it strikes.
 * Brine Skin Advantage: While in the saltwater, its Stealth (+4 with Advantage) makes it nearly invisible. It should wait until a player or a "flying snack" (like a L'zoir) gets within 5 feet of the water's edge.
 * The Breach: It starts with Salt Spray (15-foot cone) from just below the surface. This blinds the party immediately, preventing them from seeing where the serpent retreats to.
2. The "Sting" Tax
Like the Fire Snake, the serpent punishes aggression.
 * Melee Deterrent: Any player hitting it with a sword or axe must pass a DC 11 Con Save or take 3 acid damage. At Level 1, two successful hits could actually kill the attacker before they kill the serpent.
 * Grapple Fail: If a player tries to pull it out of the water to negate its Water-Dependent AC bonus, they are almost guaranteed to take the acid sting.
3. The Boiling Rotation
The serpent plays a "Hit and Run" game using its 40 ft. Swim Speed.
 * Turn 1: Breach, Salt Spray (Recharge 5–6), and submerge.
 * Turn 2: While the party is blinded, use Boiling Bite with Advantage.
 * Turn 3: If Salt Spray hasn't recharged, use the Boiling Bite and then use its movement to swim to the deep end or under a ledge.

Synergy with Hotshoe NPCs
 * Milton’s Salt Quest: Milton (the Shopkeeper) might desperately need the "Salt-Gems" from the serpent's stomach to adjust the soil acidity for his Orchard of Hope. He’ll offer a bounty, but warn the players: "Don't let it spit in your eyes, or you'll be tasting the mountain for a week!"
 * Kip's Squeeze: Kip "The Cricket" might know of narrow underwater "Squeeze" tunnels that lead into the serpent's lair, allowing a brave (or foolish) player to bypass the boiling surface.
 * The Xorn Rivalry: If the players find themselves overwhelmed by a serpent, a Xorn might suddenly burst from the lakebed. The Xorn doesn't want to save the players—it just wants the "Salt-Gems." This creates a three-way battle where the players can potentially slip away while the elemental and the serpent tear into each other.
Roleplay Flavor: The Steam-Hiss
 * The Sound: When it breaches, it doesn't splash; it sounds like a hot iron being dunked in a bucket—a violent, steaming hiss.
 * Visuals: Its scales are encrusted with white salt crystals that flake off when it moves, looking like "underwater snow." Its throat glows a dull, boiling red just before it uses Salt Spray.
 * The "Brittle" Fear: If the serpent is forced onto land, describe its scales as audibly cracking. It becomes frantic and desperate, trying to find any source of moisture—even if it's just a player's water skin.
