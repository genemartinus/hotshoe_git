### Ash-Beak Phoenix (Fledgling)

- Small elemental neutral
- **Armor Class** 13
- **Hit Points** 14 (4d4 + 4)
- **Speed** 10 ft., fly 50 ft.
---

| **STR** | **DEX** | **CON** | **INT** | **WIS** | **CHA** |
| ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- |
| 6 (-2)  | 16 (+3) | 12 (+1) | 4 (-3)  | 12 (+1) | 10 (+0) |

---

- **Damage Immunities** Fire    
- **Senses** Passive Perception 11
- **Challenge** 1/4 (50 XP)
---
**Ash-Dive.** If the phoenix flies at least 20 feet straight toward a target and then hits it with a beak attack, the target takes an extra 2 (1d4) fire damage and must succeed on a DC 11 Constitution save or be blinded by a puff of ash until the start of its next turn.

### **Actions**

**Beak.** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. _Hit:_ 5 (1d4 + 3) piercing damage.

The Ash-Beak Phoenix (Fledgling) is the perfect "Aerial Skirmisher" for Mt. Conniption. It isn't a tanky bird of prey; it’s a living thermal vent that relies on speed and the blinding properties of the local ash to survive.
At CR 1/4, a small flock of these can easily overwhelm a Level 1 party if they stay in the air. Here is the combat strategy for these thermal fledglings.
Combat Strategy: The Cinder-Dive
1. The "Dive-Bomb" Loop
The Phoenix's greatest strength is its 50 ft. Fly Speed and its Ash-Dive trait. It should never start its turn within 5 feet of a player.
 * Tactical Movement: The Phoenix starts 30 feet up. On its turn, it dives 20+ feet straight at a target (triggering Ash-Dive), strikes, and then uses the remainder of its 50 ft. movement to fly back up and out of melee reach.
 * The Blindness Synergy: If the target fails the DC 11 Con Save, they are Blinded. This means they cannot take an Opportunity Attack against the Phoenix as it flies away, making its retreat perfectly safe.
2. Harassing the "Carbon-Based"
Since these are thermal elementals, they should target the players who look the most "flammable" or those relying on sight-based spells.
 * Targeting: They should swarm the party's backline (the Wizards or Sorcerers). If the Phoenix blinds the caster, the caster loses the ability to target most spells that require "a creature you can see."
 * Damage Immunity (Fire): If the party tries to use Burning Hands or Fire Bolt, the Phoenixes will fly right through the flames, unbothered, perhaps even using the heat to gain height.
3. Pack Tactics (Emergent Behavior)
Though they only have 4 Intelligence, they act with a "flock instinct."
 * The Chain Blind: If the first Phoenix blinds a player, the second and third Phoenixes can attack that player with Advantage.
 * The "Ash Cloud" Effect: If three or more Phoenixes use Ash-Dive in the same area, narratively describe the air becoming thick with grey soot, making it difficult for the players to breathe or see even if they passed their saves.
Tactical Summary Table
| Phase | Action | Movement | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| The Dive | Beak Attack | Move 20ft straight down. | Trigger Ash-Dive for extra fire damage and Blindness. |
| The Escape | None (N/A) | Fly 30ft up/away. | Avoid melee retaliation from non-reach weapons. |
| The Stall | Dodge | Hover at 40ft height. | Wait for Ash-Dive opportunity or for allies to distract. |
Synergy with Hotshoe NPCs
The Ash-Beak Phoenixes create interesting interactions with the residents of the hamlet:
 * Kaelen (Druid of Nerull): Kaelen might actually lure these birds toward "pruning" targets. Their ash-based blindness complements his Ash Cloud perfectly.
 * Milton (Shopkeeper): These birds are a nightmare for Milton's Orchard of Hope. He might pay the party a "Bounty in Apples" for every Ash-Beak wing they bring back, as their heat scorches his saplings.
 * Glour (Night Watch): During the night, the Phoenixes’ fire damage makes them glow. Glour can see them coming from 120 feet away and can alert the town before the "Fire-Rain" begins.
Roleplay Flavor: The Thermal Hunger
 * Visuals: Their feathers shouldn't look like typical phoenix fire; they should look like glowing embers trapped under a layer of grey silk. When they dive, they leave a "contrail" of hot ash in the air.
 * The Sound: Instead of a majestic cry, they might make a sound like a crackling campfire or a sudden intake of air into a furnace.
