## **The traveler's advisory**

Welcome to Hotshoe! If you’re reading this, you’ve likely ignored the warnings at the mountain pass and decided to see the "majestic" Mt Conniption volcano for yourself and bask in Hotshoe's famous hospitality.  While the views are breathtaking (literally, the sulfur will sear your lungs), the local wildlife is significantly less hospitable than **Dimble** at _The Thirsty Pebble_.

Before you step off the cobbles of the plaza, familiarize yourself with this guide to the wasteland’s most "enthusiastic" residents.

---

## 🌋 The Traveler’s Guide to Not Being Eaten

### 1. The Ash Spore Husk

Don't be fooled by what looks like a dried-out, hollowed-out corpse leaning against a basalt rock.

- **The Danger:** This is a fungal parasite that has completely hollowed out a previous traveler. When it senses the vibration of a footfall or the warmth of a breath, it ruptures.
    
- **The Effect:** It releases a cloud of fine, grey spores. If inhaled, these spores begin to root in your moist lung tissue, using your body heat to germinate.
    
- **Survival Tip:** If you see a "statue" made of ash that looks like it's "breathing" a fine mist, hold your breath and walk upwind. Immediately.
    

### 2. The Dust Mephit

Small, winged, and remarkably irritable, these elemental nuisances are made of grit and bad intentions.

- **The Danger:** They aren't strong enough to kill you outright, but they are masters of the "Stinging Cloud." They fly around your head, beating their wings to create a localized dust storm.
    
- **The Effect:** You’ll be blinded and coughing while they pick-pocket your shiny belongings—or lead you straight into a Sulfur-Skulker den.
    
- **Survival Tip:** A wet rag over the face is your best friend. They hate moisture; a well-aimed splash from your waterskin will turn them into harmless mud.
    

### 3. The Lesser Fire Snake

These shimmering, orange-red serpents thrive in the actual lava flows.

- **The Danger:** They are intensely hot to the touch—literally molten on the inside. They don't just bite; they cauterize.
    
- **The Effect:** Their bite leaves a "glass-burn." The heat is so intense it turns the minerals in your blood into tiny obsidian shards near the wound.
    
- **Survival Tip:** They are attracted to metal. If you hear a high-pitched hissing, drop any iron tools and move away. They’d rather melt your shovel than bite your leg.
    

### 4. The L'zoir

A creature of pure, jagged geometry. The L'zoir is a crystalline predator that looks like a cluster of floating obsidian needles.

- **The Danger:** They are nearly invisible against the shimmering heat waves of the volcano's thermal energy. They move by snapping their crystal "limbs" together, creating a sound like breaking glass.
    
- **The Effect:** They are "Heat-Vampires." They don't want your meat; they want your thermal energy. If one latches onto you, you will experience sudden frostbite.
    
- **Survival Tip:** You have a chance to hide from the L'zoir if there is a heat source nearby that is hotter and larger than you.
    

### 5. The Scald Scale Serpent

A massive, thick-bodied constrictor that lives in the boiling mud pits.

- **The Danger:** Its scales are constantly coated in a layer of boiling-hot slime.
    
- **The Effect:** If it wraps around you, it doesn't just crush your ribs; it boils your skin off. The "Scald-Squeeze" is a messy way to go.
    
- **Survival Tip:** Watch the mud. If you see bubbles that aren't rhythmic, it's not a gas vent—it's the Serpent's breath.
    

### 6. The Xorn Youngling

While adult Xorns are content to eat gems deep underground, the Younglings are surface-dwelling scavengers with a very specific appetite.

- **The Danger:** These three-armed, three-legged rock-beings think your "magical" items or high-mineral gear (like **Durn's** old tools) look like a five-course meal.
    
- **The Effect:** They will relentlessly pursue anyone carrying high-grade ore or enchanted trinkets, "swimming" through the earth and popping up beneath your feet like a hungry, stony tripod.
    
- **Survival Tip:** Toss them a handful of raw iron ore or a cheap gemstone. They have the attention span of a toddler and will stop to eat the "treat" while you make your escape.

🌋 Official Hotshoe Travel Advisory: Mt. Conniption & Beyond
Welcome to the Kantzai Frontier. While the thermal vistas of Mt. Conniption are unparalleled, the local ecology has adapted to the heat in ways that are... let's say, unpleasant for the average carbon-based traveler. Stay on the cobbles, keep your waterskin full, and mind the following locals.
1. The Ash-Spore Husk
Appearance: Indistinguishable from a desiccated, ash-covered corpse or a mound of grey fungus.
 * The Hazard: These are fungal parasites that respond to the vibrations of your footsteps. If disturbed, they rupture, releasing a fine, superheated mist of spores.
 * Safety Protocol: If a "statue" or mound appears to be "breathing" a fine grey haze, do not approach. Move upwind immediately to avoid inhalation.
2. The Dust Mephit
Appearance: Small, winged elemental nuisances that look like living soot.
 * The Hazard: While physically weak, they are masters of localized dust storms. They specialize in blinding travelers to facilitate theft or lead them into more dangerous predatory dens.
 * Safety Protocol: Keep a damp cloth over your nose and mouth. These creatures have a noted aversion to moisture; a simple splash of water is often enough to deter their interest.
3. The Lesser Fire Snake
Appearance: Shimmering, orange-red serpents found near active lava flows and heat vents.
 * The Hazard: Their internal temperature is high enough to cauterize on contact. They are "heat-seekers" by nature and are particularly drawn to the thermal conductivity of metal.
 * Safety Protocol: If you hear a sharp, metallic hissing, be prepared to distance yourself from iron tools or heavy packs. They are often more interested in melting your gear than hunting you.

🌊 Official Hotshoe Travel Advisory: The Boiling Depths & Subterranean Shifts
Traveling beyond the village plaza brings you into contact with the "Thermal-Cyborg" life forms that define the Mt. Conniption ecosystem. Whether you're crossing the Grey Wash or exploring the mineral-rich tunnels, these residents require your full attention.
4. The Scald-Scale Serpent
Habitat: The boiling saltwater lakes and black-sand beaches of the lower slopes.
 * The Hazard: These serpents are encrusted with caustic salt crystals that "sting" on contact. They are ambush predators that strike from beneath the steam, utilizing a pressurized spray of brine to disorient their prey.
 * Safety Protocol: Watch for "breaches" in the steam. If the water's surface begins to hiss violently, retreat at least fifteen feet from the shoreline. Their hide becomes brittle when dry; forcing them onto the hot ash flats is your best defense.
5. The L’zoir (Ash-Wing)
Habitat: High-altitude swarms near heat vents and "Glowstone" hives.
 * The Hazard: These are "Thermal Vampires" that consume heat rather than flesh. They ignore cold-blooded creatures but will swarm any source of warmth—including campfires and travelers. Their presence causes a rapid drop in local air temperature.
 * Safety Protocol: If you feel an unnatural chill in the volcanic heat, douse your lanterns and bank your fires. They are physically disoriented by sudden cold; carrying "Cooling Salts" from the apothecary is highly recommended.
6. The Xorn Youngling
Habitat: Subterranean mineral veins and the foundations of stone structures.
 * The Hazard: These tri-pedal elementals possess a "Treasure Sense" that allows them to scent precious metals and gems through solid rock. They are not traditionally predatory, but they are extremely persistent when hungry and can "glide" through stone to bypass locked doors.
 * Safety Protocol: If you hear a rhythmic grinding sound beneath your feet, check your coin purse. Travelers have found that "paying a toll" of raw ore or common silver often satisfies a Youngling enough to allow safe passage.
Warden’s Note: If you encounter a hive of L'zoir, do not attempt to harvest the central Glowstone without official clearance from Stefania. Such actions can destabilize the local thermal cycle and lead to "unauthorized" environmental shifts.
