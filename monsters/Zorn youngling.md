**Earth Glide.** The xorn can burrow through nonmagical, unworked earth and stone. While doing so, the xorn doesn't disturb the material it moves through.
**Treasure Sense.** The xorn can pinpoint, by scent, the location of precious metals and stones, such as coins and gems, within 30 feet.

### **Actions**

**Multiattack.** The xorn makes two claw attacks and one bite attack.
**Claw.** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. _Hit:_ `dice: 1d4 + 2`slashing damage.
**Bite.** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. _Hit:_ `dice: 2d4+2` piercing damage.

The Xorn Youngling is the undisputed "Heavyweight" of the Hotshoe starters. With 33 HP, an AC of 16, and Nonmagical Resistance, it is a literal brick wall for a Level 1 party. However, because it is a neutral scavenger driven by hunger rather than malice, it functions more like a "Hazardous Encounter" than a traditional villain.
Here is the combat strategy for this gemstone-hungry elemental.
Combat Strategy: The Geologic Juggernaut
1. The "Treasure-First" Targeting
The Xorn doesn't care about your Paladin’s HP; it cares about the gold in their pouch.
 * The Scent of Wealth: Using Treasure Sense, the Xorn will bypass the "poor" Wizard to focus entirely on whoever is carrying the most coins or gems.
 * The "Pay-to-Win" Mechanic: A clever party can avoid this fight entirely by throwing 10–20 GP in the opposite direction. If they don't, the Xorn will use Multiattack (Claw/Claw/Bite) to try and "crack open" the player like a piggy bank.
2. The "Earth Glide" Guerrilla
The Xorn should never stay on the surface if it feels threatened.
 * Vertical Ambush: It can burrow 20 ft. into the ground without leaving a hole. It should pop up directly underneath the person carrying the party's loot, strike, and then sink 5 feet back into the stone.
 * Tremorsense: Since most of the Hotshoe monsters (like the Fire Snake or Ash-Spore Husk) are grounded, the Xorn knows exactly where everyone is, even while it's submerged in the rock.
3. The Multiattack Pressure
At CR 1, this is one of the few creatures in your list that can make three attacks per turn.
 * Action Economy: It has a +4 to hit. Against a Level 1 character with 10–12 HP, one successful Multiattack sequence (avg. 20 damage) is an instant knockout.
 * Tactical Tip: If the Xorn is "playing with its food," it might use its first two claws to Grapple a player's backpack or belt, then use the Bite on the precious metals inside.

Synergy with Hotshoe NPCs
The Xorn’s presence creates unique social and mechanical hooks:
 * Dimble Pingun (The Pebble): Dimble is terrified of Xorns. His Fermenter’s Stone is a magical "pebble" that likely smells like a five-course meal to a Youngling. If a Xorn enters the Thirsty Pebble, it’s a social catastrophe.
 * Durn (The Mason): Durn has Mason’s Tools and spends his life in the mines. He might be the only one who can "speak" to the Xorn in Terran, negotiating a "toll" of raw obsidian to keep it from eating the party's gear.
 * The Serpent Rivalry: As mentioned in your lore, Xorns hunt Scald-Scale Serpents for their stomach gems. If the party is being chased by a serpent, they might lead it toward a Xorn’s burrow to start a monster-vs-monster brawl.
Roleplay Flavor: The Grinding Maw
 * The Sound: They don't growl; they sound like two tectonic plates rubbing together—a deep, rhythmic grind.
 * Visuals: Describe its three eyes as looking like dull garnets and its skin as having the texture of cooling basalt. When it eats, its mouth (on top of its head) opens like a jagged stone iris.
 * The "Hungry" Negotiator: In the middle of combat, it might stop attacking if a player holds up a diamond or a handful of silver. It tilts its head, its three arms twitching in anticipation.
