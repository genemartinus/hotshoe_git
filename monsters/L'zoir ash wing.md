### L’zoir (Ash Wing) lesser

_Small Elemental, Neutral_

---

- **Armor Class** 14 (Natural Armor)
- **Hit Points** 9 (2d6 + 2)
- **Speed** 10 ft., fly 50 ft.
- **Size**: Small
- **Type**: Elemental
- Neutral

| STR    | DEX     | CON     | INT    | WIS     | CHA    |
| ------ | ------- | ------- | ------ | ------- | ------ |
| 6 (-2) | 16 (+3) | 12 (+1) | 5 (-3) | 12 (+1) | 7 (-2) |

- **Damage Vulnerabilities** Cold
- **Damage Immunities** Fire, Poison
- **Condition Immunities** Poisoned
- **Senses** Blindsight 60 ft. (Heat-sense), Passive Perception 11
- **Languages** —    
- **Challenge** 1/4 (50 XP)
    
- **Heat Thirst.** The L’zoir can sense any heat source (creatures or fires) within 100 feet. It has advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks to locate such sources.
- **Heat Aura.** A creature that starts its turn within 5 feet of the L’zoir must succeed on a DC 10 Constitution saving throw or take 1 fire damage as the L'zoir leaches the warmth from the air.
- **Hive Mind.** The L’zoir is immune to the Charmed and Frightened conditions while it is within 1 mile of its hive or a Warrior L’zoir.
### Actions

- **Multiattack.** The L’zoir makes two Stinger attacks.
- **Stinger.** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. _Hit:_ 5 (1d4 + 1) piercing damage. If both attacks hit the same target on a single turn, the target’s Strength score is reduced by 1. The target dies if this reduces its Strength to 0. Otherwise, the reduction lasts until the target finishes a short or long rest.
### Lore & Tactics

- **The Hunt for Heat:** L’zoir do not eat food; they consume thermal energy. They will ignore a cold lizard to swarm a warm-blooded Paladin or a campfire.
- **Glowstones:** L'zoir hives center around "Glowstones"—massive gems that store the heat the insects collect. Removing a Glowstone often causes the entire hive to perish instantly.
- **Weakness to Cold:** While they are immune to fire, cold effects are physically painful and disorienting to them. In many Planescape campaigns, "Cold Iron" or ice magic is the only way to drive a swarm away from a ship's boiler or a camp.

CREDIT
Based on the lore from _Mimir.net_ (a Planescape fan resource), the **L’zoir** (or "Ash Wings") are heat-thirsting elemental insects native to the Quasimental Plane of Ash.
https://mimir.net/creatures/bestiary/l-zoir/

The L’zoir (Ash Wing) is a terrifying addition to the Hotshoe ecosystem. While other monsters in the region focus on blinding or burning, the L’zoir are "Thermal Vampires." They don't just hurt you; they drain the very heat (and Strength) from your body.
With 9 HP and a 50 ft. Fly Speed, they are fragile but hyper-mobile. Their Heat-sense Blindsight means hiding in the ash won't work against them.
Combat Strategy: The Cold Drain
1. The "Strength Sap" Focus
The L’zoir’s Multiattack is its most dangerous feature. At +5 to hit, it is very likely to land both stings on a low-AC character.
 * Targeting the "Warmest": Because of Heat Thirst, they will bypass the cold, armored Fighter to swarm the high-energy Sorcerer or the warm-blooded Cleric.
 * The Strength Drain: If a target's Strength hits 0, they die. For a Level 1 Wizard with 8 Strength, it only takes a few lucky rounds from a small swarm to be fatal.
2. The "Heat Aura" Passive
The Heat Aura (DC 10 Con) is a "tax" for standing near them.
 * The "Leach" Effect: Even if the L’zoir misses its stings, just being within 5 feet of it drains 1 fire damage. In a swarm of 3 or 4 L’zoir, a player could take 3–4 damage just by starting their turn, which is devastating for a 1st-level character.
3. The Hive Mind Bunker
While within a mile of their hive or a Warrior, they are Immune to Charmed and Frightened.
 * Morale: They will not flee. They will fight until they are "extinguished." This makes them much more dangerous than a typical beast, as they will ignore injuries to continue the "harvest."

Synergy with Hotshoe NPCs
The L’zoir change the way the townsfolk live:
 * Dimble Pingun (Barkeep): Dimble likely keeps his tavern's fire low or shielded, as the "Thirsty Pebble" would be a beacon for these insects. He might use Prestidigitation specifically to chill the exterior of the building to keep them away.
 * Millicent Preston: She is likely fascinated by their Heat-sense Blindsight. She might offer a high price for an intact L’zoir wing-membrane to craft "Thermal Sight" goggles for the party.
 * Orion & Kip: They probably carry "Cold Stones" or vials of cooling salts specifically to disrupt the L’zoir's senses if they get swarmed while scavenging.
Logical & Moral Implications: The Glowstone
 * The Ethical Choice: If the party finds a hive, they can "win" the encounter instantly by destroying the Glowstone. However, doing so kills the entire hive (likely hundreds of L’zoir). A Druid (like Kaelen) might argue that the L’zoir are a vital part of the "Final Pruning" of the volcano, creating a moral dilemma for the players.
Roleplay Flavor: The Chilled Air
 * Visuals: Their wings shouldn't buzz like a bee; they should shimmer like heat-haze on a highway. When they get close, the air around the player should visibly frost or feel unnaturally cold as the L'zoir leaches the warmth.
 * The Weakness: If a player uses a cold-based spell (like Ray of Frost), describe the L’zoir as physically shattering or becoming "brittle," falling to the ground like frozen glass.
