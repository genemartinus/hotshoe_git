### Ash-Spore Husk

_Medium Undead (Fungus), Neutral Evil_

---

- Medium, undead fungus neutral evil (thanks to Nerull?)
- **Armor Class** 11 (Natural Armor)
- **Hit Points** 10 (2d6 + 4)
- **Speed** 20 ft.

| **STR** | **DEX** | **CON** | **INT** | **WIS** | **CHA** |
| ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- |
| 12 (+1) | 8 (-1)  | 14 (+2) | 3 (-4)  | 6 (-2)  | 5 (-3)  |

- **Damage Immunities** Poison
- **Condition Immunities** Poisoned
- **Senses** Darkvision 60 ft., Passive Perception 8
- **Challenge** 1/4 (50 XP)

**False Appearance.** While the husk remains motionless, it is indistinguishable from a normal ash-covered corpse or a mound of grey fungus.

**Smoldering Spores.** When the husk takes damage, a cloud of hot, grey ash puffs out in a 5-foot radius. Any creature in that area must succeed on a **DC 10 Constitution saving throw** or be **Blinded** until the end of its next turn.

**Undead Fortitude.** If damage reduces the husk to 0 hit points, it must make a Constitution saving throw with a DC of 5 + the damage taken, unless the damage is fire or from a critical hit. On a success, the husk drops to 1 hit point instead.

### Actions

**Sooty Slam.** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +3 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target.  Hit: `dice: 1d4+1` bludgeoning damage plus 2 (1d4) fire damage.

**Choking Cough (Recharge 6).** The husk exhales a puff of thick, black smoke in a **10-foot cone**. Each creature in that area must make a **DC 10 Constitution saving throw**, taking `dice: 1d6` poison damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one.

**Traits**

- **False Appearance:** While the husk remains motionless, it is indistinguishable from a normal ash-covered corpse or a mound of grey fungus.
- **Smoldering Spores:** When the husk takes damage, a cloud of hot, grey ash puffs out in a 5-foot radius. Any creature in that area must succeed on a **DC 11 Constitution saving throw** or be **Blinded** until the end of its next turn as the ash gets in its eyes.

- **Choking Cough (Recharge 5–6):** The husk exhales a puff of thick, black smoke in a 15-foot cone. Each creature in that area must make a **DC 12 Constitution saving throw**, taking 7 (2d6) poison damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one.

**Combat Tactics for the DM**

- **The "Pop" Factor:** Use the **Smoldering Spores** to create chaos. If the Fighter hits a Husk, they might go blind, giving the Husk (or Kaelen) advantage on the next turn.
- **Environmental Synergy:** If the volcano tremors during the fight, you can have a "vent" open up nearby, dealing 1d6 fire damage to anyone standing on it, further complicating the battlefield.


The Ash-Spore Husk is the perfect "Meat Shield" for the Oakhaven Cemetery. While the Ash-Beak Phoenix dominates the air, these husks control the ground by punishing anyone brave (or foolish) enough to stand in melee range. Their low speed of 20 ft. makes them easy to kite, but their False Appearance and Smoldering Spores make them a terrifying surprise in a crowded graveyard.
Here is the combat strategy for these fungal-undead sentries.
Combat Strategy: The Smoldering Wall
1. The "Ashen Trap" (Ambush)
The Husk should almost never start a fight standing up. Because of False Appearance, they should be placed in "mounds" throughout the cemetery.
 * The Trigger: Wait for a player to step within 5 feet or try to "loot" a corpse.
 * The Surprise: The Husk stands up and uses its Sooty Slam. Since it was likely hidden, the DM might grant it a Surprise Round, allowing it to land a hit before the player can react.
2. Punishing the Melee (Smoldering Spores)
The Husk’s most dangerous feature is triggered when it takes damage.
 * The "Blinding Puff": Every time a Fighter or Paladin hits the Husk, they risk being Blinded (DC 10 Con).
 * Tactical Loop: If the player is blinded, the Husk gains Advantage on its next Sooty Slam. More importantly, the player now has Disadvantage on their next attack. This creates a "death spiral" where the player keeps missing because they are hitting the very thing that blinds them.
3. The "Choking Cough" Reset
With a Recharge 6, the Choking Cough is a rare but powerful area-of-effect tool.
 * Crowd Control: The Husk should use this when two or more players are clustered together.
 * The Poison/Fire Split: Between the poison damage from the cough and the fire damage from the slam, the Husk hits multiple damage types, testing the party's resistances early.
Tactical Summary Table

|---|---|---|---|
| Passive | False Appearance | High | Gain a surprise round by looking like a fungus mound. |
| Reactive | Smoldering Spores | Automatic | Blind anyone who dares to strike it in melee. |

Synergy with Hotshoe NPCs
The Husks are likely the "crops" that Kaelen the Harvester is so interested in.
 * The Harvester's Guard: Kaelen can stand behind a line of 2 or 3 Ash-Spore Husks. While the players struggle to get past the blinding spores, Kaelen snipes them with Chill Touch or Ray of Frost.
 * Jube Curnow’s Nightmare: As the cemetery protector, Jube likely hates these things. Her Thaumaturgy clap might be used to try and "scare" players away from a mound she knows is actually a Husk.
 * Undead Fortitude: Since they are hard to kill (unless using fire), they can keep a party busy for 3–4 rounds, long enough for Glour to spot the commotion and intervene.
Roleplay Flavor: The Choking Grave
 * The Sound: Instead of a typical zombie groan, the Husk should emit a dry, raspy wheeze, like a bellows blowing through hot ash.
 * Visuals: Describe the grey fungus as pulsing slightly with a dull orange glow deep within the "ribcage" area, hinting at the thermal nature of the Mt. Conniption life forms.
 * The Aftermath: Even if the players kill it, the "Undead Fortitude" means it might just twitch back to life. Use this to keep the tension high in the cemetery—just because it stopped moving doesn't mean it's dead.
