To the residents of Hotshoe, the story of the "Jade-Glint" (the green poisonous obsidian) isn't just history—it’s a ghost story that still has teeth. Depending on who the players talk to, they get a very different "flavor" of the tragedy.
Here is how the survivors and storytellers of the hamlet describe the rise and fall of the Jade-Glint Mines.
🟢 The Discovery: "The Serpent's Rib"
As told by Yingman (The Scavenger-Scholar):
> "It wasn't a slow find. The mountain cracked like a dry bone during the 'Great Shudder' six years back. A fissure opened in the deep dark, exposing a vein that didn't reflect torchlight—it drank it and spat back a sickly, emerald glow. The Dwarves called it the 'Serpent's Rib.'
> They found that if you knapped it using Ash Yam sap, the glass didn't just sharpen; it pulsed. It wasn't 'tainted' to them; it was alive. They started pulling it out by the ton, thinking they’d found a way to forge blades that could kill a giant with a single scratch. But obsidian is glass, and glass remembers the heat that made it. The mountain didn't like its ribs being picked."
> 
⛏️ The Mining: "The Poison-Steam"
As told by Obur (The Watchman/Survivor):
> "I remember the smell first. Not just sulfur—something sweet, like rotting Cinder Berries. We were working the lower drifts when the green dust started settling. It didn't burn the skin; it made your veins turn black under the surface.
> The 'Green Cast' obsidian was outrageously sharp, aye. You could shave a hair in the dark with it. But the more we mined, the more the Ash-Spore Husks started appearing in the shafts. It was like the green glass was calling them. We were making the finest +1 Poison blades the Frontier had ever seen, but the price was a hacking cough that tasted like copper and bile. We thought we were rich. We were just feeding the mountain's fever."
> 
🌋 The Destruction: "The Breath of Nerull"
As told by Glour (The Stoic Watchman):
> "Kaelen calls it a blessing from Nerull. I call it a burial. When the second tremor hit, it wasn't lava that came for the mines—it was a pressurized wall of green-tinted ash and poison gas. It hit the main shaft like a piston.
> I saw the forge-fires turn emerald green before the ceiling came down. Most of my kin are still down there, preserved in that 'Eternal Ash.' The mines weren't just destroyed; they were sealed. The mountain took back the Jade-Glint and left us with nothing but these soot-stained memories and a few broken shards. If you see a green flicker in the wasteland, don't go toward it. That’s the mountain looking for another 'miner' to preserve."
> 
📜 Mechanical Hook: The "Jade-Glint" Shard
If the players manage to scavenge a piece of this "tainted" obsidian from the ruins:
 * The Weapon: A dagger or sword knapped from this glass deals its normal damage plus 1 Poison damage.
 * The Risk: Every time the player rolls a Natural 1 on an attack with a Jade-Glint weapon, they accidentally nick themselves, taking the 1 poison damage and becoming Poisoned until the end of their next turn.
🎭 The Standoff:
 * Kaelen wants the players to find the "Motherlode" of Jade-Glint to prove it’s a gift from Nerull to rot the world.
 * Obur & Glour want the mines left alone. They fear that reopening the "Serpent's Rib" will release the pressurized poison gas and turn Hotshoe into a mass grave.

🗺️ The "Mirror-Map" of the Jade-Glint Vent
The etching shows a cross-section of Mt. Conniption’s western flank, leading away from the village and toward the Dwarven Cemetery.
1. The Entry Point: "The Chimney"
Located behind a cluster of Needle Grass that has grown unusually high (nearly 4 feet tall), there is a natural steam vent known as The Chimney.
 * The Tell: Unlike the other vents that puff white steam, this one intermittently exhales a faint, lime-green haze.
 * The Hazard: The air here is thin. Characters must succeed on a DC 11 Constitution save every 10 minutes or suffer one level of exhaustion from the "Sweet-Rot" fumes.
2. The Descent: "The Ash-Choke Squeeze"
The vent drops 40 feet into a vertical shaft.
 * The Squeeze: Halfway down, the shaft narrows. Kip "The Cricket" is the only one who has ever made it through, but a Small creature (or a Medium creature with a successful DC 13 Dexterity/Acrobatics check) can slide past the obsidian jaggeds.
 * The Loot: Tiny fragments of Jade-Glint are embedded in the walls here. A player can spend 10 minutes to pry out enough for 1 Jade-Glint Arrowhead (+1 poison damage).
3. The Destination: "The Emerald Drift"
The bottom of the shaft opens into the ceiling of Level 1 of the Old Mines.
 * The Sight: The floor is covered in a thick, velvety layer of Green Ash.
 * The Guardian: This is where the first Ash-Spore Husks of the "Jade Variety" reside. They don't just blind you; their spores carry the Jade-Glint poison.
📜 Yingman’s Scrawled Note (Tucked behind the mirror)
> "The dwarves didn't die from the cave-in. They died because they stopped coughing and started breathing the glass. If you go down there, take a filter-mask soaked in Ash Yam Sap. The sap binds to the green dust before it can bind to your blood. And for Nerull’s sake, don't bring a torch. The green gas in the Drift is... 'reactive' to open flames."
>
