Here is how the Ash & Water Crisis manifests as a series of social and mechanical divides for your players to navigate.
💧 The Resource War: Carbon vs. Thermal
The village is split between those trying to preserve the "Old World" (Carbon) and those fascinated by the "New World" (Thermal).
1. The Entrepreneurs’ Struggle
 * Milton (The Orchardist): He is the most desperate. His apples need clean, non-alkaline water. The ash turns the rain into a caustic lye that "sears" his saplings. He is looking for a way to "filter" the ash before it hits the soil.
 * Dimble (The Barkeep): He has the Fermenter’s Stone, but even magic needs a base. If the water supply is too ash-heavy, his beer tastes like sulfur and grit. He needs the players to find a "clean" spring—or steal one from the L'zoir.
 * Millicent (The Apothecary): She sees the water problem as a chemical opportunity. She wants the "Boiling Brine" from the Scald-Scale Serpent’s lake to see if its mineral content can stabilize her potions.
 * Phanzis (The Scribe): He is the wild card. He needs Clean Water for his inks, but he is secretly theorizing that Thermal Ash could be processed into a "Conducive Ink" that allows spells to "burn" longer.
🪦 The Theological Split: Jube vs. Kaelen
The cemeteries are the ground zero for Hotshoe’s nightmares. The fact that the dead no longer rot normally has created a terrifying rift between the town’s spiritual leaders.
The Conflict: The "Eternal Ash"
 * Jube Curnow (The Guardian): She views the Ash-Spore Husks and the lack of decay as an abomination—a "stalling" of the natural cycle. She believes the ancestors are being "trapped" in their own soot-covered skins, unable to move on. She wants the players to "Prune" the husks to release the spirits.
 * Kaelen (The Harvester): He sees the "Ash Fungus" as the next stage of evolution. To him, the fact that the dead are becoming "Thermal Mounds" is beautiful. He believes the volcano is "upgrading" the village. He wants the players to protect the fungal blooms and bring him samples of "living" ash.
> The Nightmare Hook: If the ash fall continues, will the residents of Hotshoe eventually stop "dying" and simply become part of the thermal landscape while still conscious?

🛠️ DM Engagement Hook: The "Town Meeting"
When the players arrive, have a public argument breaking out in the plaza:
 * Milton is shouting at Kaelen because the "Death-Fungus" is spreading toward his orchard.
 * Jube is ringing the cemetery bell to ward off Dust Mephits.
 * Dimble is trying to sell "Discount Ash-Ale" to calm everyone down.
The players are immediately asked to choose a side: Do they help Milton "scrub" the ash (Carbon survival), or do they help Kaelen "cultivate" the change (Thermal evolution)?
