### Description
The lake appeared after the eruption and no one is sure when the serpent arrived or how.  It is a hot salt lake and home of a [[scald-scale serpent]] that may be engaged in a fight to the death with a [[L'zoir ash wing]] or [[Zorn youngling]].

### **Encounters**

**First visit:** The lake is peaceful and the first sighting of the [[scald-scale serpent]] will be when it is attacked by a [[L'zoir ash wing]] or [[Zorn youngling]].  A monster clash presents an opportunity to observe the local wildlife.  This is a trial run at combat where players can observe the mechanics of how it plays out and the DM narrates or role plays the action.

**Subsequent visits:**  Monster encounters at the lake are at the DM discretion.  Being foolish will always attract attention.

### Perception checks
- **Evidence of the Scald-scale serpent's presence in the lake**.  Success means the players will not suffer a stealth attack.  It might also provide some further evidence of likely biology, cause/effect and behavior patterns the players should work into their expectations and strategic response to the uncertain.
	- Ripples on the surface that indicate something big.
	- Serpent feces on the shore
	- Dead bodies of defeated enemies
	- Serpent scales from the last shedding.
- **Once the players know about the serpent they can actively look for it themselves.** If they do not or forget to say so then they might be surprised by it.

# **Kaelen the Harvester, Druid of Nerull**

#### **Statistics**
**Race**  Human (Variant)   **Class:** Druid   **Alignment:** Neutral Evil
- **Armor Class:** 14 (Hide Armor, Shield)
- **Hit Points:** 13 (2d8+4)
- **Speed:** 30 ft.
- **Ability Scores:** STR 10 (+0), DEX 12 (+1), CON 14 (+2), INT 10 (+0), WIS 15 (+2), CHA 8 (-1)
- **Saving Throws:** Wis +4
- **Skills:** Medicine +4, Nature +2, Perception +4
- **Senses:** Passive Perception 14
- **Languages:** Common, Druidic
- **Challenge:** 1/2 (100 XP) | **Proficiency Bonus:** +2
#### **Special Trait**
**Feat: Magic Initiate (Wizard)**   This allows him to tap into Nerull’s arcane deathly power without being a multiclassed Wizard.
- **Gleaner’s Patience:** Kaelen has advantage on saving throws against being charmed or frightened. He is too focused on the inevitable end to be swayed by mortal emotions.
- **- **Fungal Affinity:** Kaelen can move through difficult terrain created by non-magical plants or fungi without spending extra movement
#### **Combat & Actions**

- **The Gleaner (Sickle):** Melee Weapon Attack: **+2 to hit**, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit:_ **1d4 + 2** slashing damage.
- **Ash Cloud (1/Day):** Kaelen shakes his soot-stained robes. A 10-foot radius of grey ash spreads from him, lasting for 1 minute. The area is heavily obscured. Kaelen can see through this ash perfectly.
#### Spellcasting
- **Chill Touch** (Cantrip): A ghostly, skeletal hand touches the target. **+2 to hit**, 20 ft range. **disadvantage 1d6  necrotic damage**,
- **Ray of Frost (Cantrip):** The cold of the grave slows enemies by 10 ft.
- **Cantrips:** _Primal Savagery_ (his teeth/fingernails sharpen into acidic rot), _Guidance_ (Nerull whispers a cold truth).
#### **Bonus actions**
**Prune the Weak:** Kaelen targets a creature within 30 feet that is below its hit point maximum. His next attack against that creature deals an additional 2 ($1d4$) necrotic damage
#### **Reactions**
**Vessel of Soot:** When Kaelen takes damage from a melee attack, he can use his reaction to emit a puff of volcanic ash. The attacker must succeed on a DC 12 Constitution saving throw or be **Blinded** until the end of its next turn.
#### **Roleplaying Notes**
- **Personality:** He speaks in metaphors of the "long winter" and the "final pruning." He is not angry; he is simply certain.
- **Visual:** He is covered in a fine layer of grey volcanic dust that never seems to wash off. His sickle is chipped but razor-sharp.
- He likely follows the party or hangs around the edges of a village, watching the elderly or the sick with an unsettling amount of patience. He isn't necessarily violent—he just knows that _everyone_ eventually belongs to Nerull, and he’s happy to wait for his turn to "harvest."
- This setting is a perfect pressure cooker for a Druid of Nerull. The volcano represents nature’s most violent form of "harvest," while the cemetery represents the quiet aftermath.
#### **Specific storyline**
In the Oakhaven cemetery, Kaelen is seen meticulously scraping "Death-Glass" fungi off the headstones. He claims the village's fear of the volcano is misplaced—the mountain isn't the threat; the things growing in the ash are.
#### **Logical and moral implications**
- **The Fragile Oracle:** At CR 1/2, Kaelen is easily dispatched by a coordinated party. However, his death might stop the "pruning" process, allowing the fungal-necrotic spores to infect the village's water supply.
- **Mercy or Execution:** Kaelen doesn't beg for his life. If defeated, he simply remarks that he is "early for the harvest" and waits for the party to finish the job.
#### **Reward/treasure/favor**
- **The Favor:** Kaelen can show the party how to coat their weapons in volcanic ash, granting them +1 necrotic damage for their next successful hit.
- **The Loot:** A chipped obsidian sickle (worth 10 gp), a pouch of "Death-Glass" spores, and a locket filled with volcanic ash.
#### **The hook**
###### **Volcanic unrest:** 
The village sits in the shadow of a grumbling volcano. For year, an intermittent fine, grey ash has been falling like snow; sometimes light and others quite heavy. The villagers are terrified of an eruption, but over time the local cemetery has developed a more immediate, localized problem: the bodies aren't staying buried, but they aren't "undead" (zombies) in the traditional sense.  Someone needs to check on the [[Dwarven cemetery]].

###### **Nerull's touch:** 
The volcanic ash has become magically infused with brimstone and necrotic energy from a deep pocket of gas and steam touched by Nerull released by recent tremors. This ash has a perverse fertilizing quality according to certain [[Kaelen|druids of Nerull]]. Instead of ghosts or zombies, the corpses in the cemetery are being "overgrown" by charcoal-colored, heat-resistant luminescent  fungi that use the bones as a trellis and source of decay.

#### **Kaelen’s Role:**

The players find **Kaelen** in the cemetery during an ash-storm. He isn't fighting the fungal horrors; he is **pruning** them with his sickle.

- **Kaelen’s Perspective:** He believes the volcano is Nerull’s way of "clearing the fields." He is tending to the "Ash-Spore" corpses, claiming they are a rare crop that only blooms once a century.
- **The Twist:** Kaelen is actually preventing the village priest from "blessing" the graves. He claims the holy water will cause a chemical reaction with the volcanic ash that will trigger a localized explosion or a toxic gas cloud.
- **Moral Ambiguity:** Is Kaelen a creepy lunatic, or is his "death-wisdom" actually saving the town from a volcanic gas leak?
- **Environmental Hazard:** Combat in the cemetery can include **Low Visibility** (ash) and **Difficult Terrain** (shifting soot and grasping fungal limbs).
- **The Reward:** If the players help Kaelen "harvest" the cemetery, he might grant them a shard of **Volcanic Obsidian** that can be used as a spellcasting focus or forged into a +1 Sickle.

An encounter with Jube

**Scene:** The Oakhaven Cemetery at dusk. A thick, grey layer of volcanic ash blankets the headstones like fallen snow. **Jube Curnow** is struggling to push a heavy wooden cart filled with fresh limestone and burial shrouds, her splint armor clanking loudly with every step. **Kaelen the Harvester** is crouched over a particularly ancient grave, his obsidian sickle shaving off glowing, charcoal-colored fungi.

**Narrative** The silence of the graveyard is shattered by the rhythmic _clank-shirr-clank_ of Jube’s mismatched armor. She stops, huffing, and wipes a smudge of soot from her forehead, leaving a streak of white limestone paste behind. She catches sight of Kaelen’s hunched form and freezes, her hand instinctively flying to the holy symbol she usually forgets is pinned to her shoulder.

**Jube** (Speaking telepathically, her voice echoing inside Kaelen’s mind with a disorganized, frantic energy) _“Excuse me! Sir? Mr. Harvester? You can’t be... scraping that. I have a very specific list of funeral rites from the Temple, and 'shaving the headstones' is definitely not on page four. Or was it page six? Regardless, it's disrespectful to the—wait, is that fungus glowing?”_

**Kaelen** (He doesn't look up, his voice a dry, papery rasp that scrapes against the air) "Respect is for the living, little cleric. The dead have no use for your limestone or your stuttering prayers. They are becoming a garden. A dark, ripening crop."

**Jube** (Speaking aloud now, her voice flustered and high-pitched) "A garden? These are _ancestors_, not turnips! I’m the appointed undertaker, and I’m supposed to keep this place 'dignified and quiet.' You are making it... crunchy. And smelly. If the Elder sees this, she’ll send me another letter, and I’ve already lost the letter-opener!"

**Kaelen**(He stands slowly, his height looming over the small halfling. He holds up a shard of the fungal 'Death-Glass') "Your Temple seeks to preserve the husk. Nerull seeks the essence. This ash is a blessing from the mountain’s throat. If I do not 'prune' these graves, the rot will stop being a passenger and start being a predator. Would you prefer a quiet cemetery, or one that breathes back at you?"

**Jube**  (She blinks, looking between her cart of shrouds and his wicked sickle. Her **Disorganized** trait kicks in as she starts rummaging through a pouch) "Breathes? Oh dear. I don't think I have a spell for 'Lung-Prevention.' I have _Burning Hands_, but that seems like it would just make the ash hotter, right? And _Cure Wounds_... can you heal a grave? Is a grave a person?"

**Kaelen** (He steps closer, his amber eyes tracking a vein of necrotic energy in the soot) "Step aside, Jube Curnow. Go back to your clanking and your ledgers. I will handle the harvest. You can handle the decorations. Just stay away from the north corner—the earth there is... hungry today."

**Jube** (Telepathically, with a hint of genuine worry) _“Hungry? Like... for snacks? Because I have some dried apple rings in my splint-thigh-pocket, but they’re a bit dusty. Please don't let the cemetery eat the neighbors, Mr. Kaelen. I’m already behind on the paperwork as it is!”_

**Narrative** Kaelen ignores her, returning to his crouch with military precision. Jube stands there for a moment, looking down at her disorganized cart, then shrugs and begins to clank toward the south gate, muttering about where she left her favorite shovel.

**Another take at Jube and Kaelen**
**Scene:** The entrance to the Hotshoe Cemetery. The midday sun is filtered through a haze of volcanic ash, giving everything a sickly, sepia tone. **Jube Curnow** is standing next to her cart, tangling her fingers in a set of rusted garden shears that look far too large for her. **Kaelen the Harvester** is nearby, silently sharpening his obsidian sickle with a piece of volcanic pumice.

**Narrative** The sound of Jube’s armor clanking stops abruptly as she reaches a particularly overgrown patch of thorny, ash-stained briars near the gate. She looks at the shears, then at the wall of thorns, then at Kaelen. A look of desperate inspiration crosses her face.

**Jube**  (Telepathically, her voice echoing in Kaelen’s mind like a scattered pile of falling books) _“Mr. Kaelen! Sir! I’ve had a thought. A professional proposal! You see, the Temple—or was it the Mayor? Someone with a very fancy hat—said the landscaping has become ‘aggressive.’ And my shears... well, they’re more of a suggestion than a tool at this point.”_

**Kaelen** (He doesn't stop the rhythmic _shrr-shrr_ of his sharpening. His voice is a low, graveyard rasp) “Nature does not suggest, Cleric. It reclaims. The briars are merely tasting the iron in the soil. They are... eager.”

**Jube** (Speaking aloud now, waving a silver piece she found at the bottom of a pocket filled with lint) “Right! Eager! Exactly! So, since you’re already ‘harvesting’ the glowy-bits, what if I officially hired you? As a... Senior Vegetation Management Consultant? You have that lovely sickle, and you’re so... precise. If you could just ‘prune’ the grass and the bushes so I can actually find the headstones? I keep tripping over the Grimsby family, and they were very private people.”

**Kaelen** (He stops sharpening and looks at the silver piece with cold, amber eyes) “You wish for me to use the Gleaner—a tool consecrated to the final transition—to trim... decorative shrubbery?”

**Jube** (Nodding vigorously, her mismatched pauldrons squeaking) “Yes! Exactly! It’s all part of the ‘Cycle,’ right? Death, rebirth, and... keeping the weeds from swallowing the epitaphs. I’ll even throw in a jar of Millicent’s ‘Invigorating Tonic.’ She says it tastes like copper and regret, but it keeps you awake for three days! Perfect for night-shifts!”

**Kaelen**(A long silence passes. He looks at the briars, then back to Jube. He reaches out a gaunt, soot-stained hand and takes the coin) “The green world is choking on the grey. It needs to be bled. I will 'trim' your cemetery, Jube Curnow. But understand... once I start the harvest, I do not stop until the earth is bare. Do not ask me to spare the flowers. Nerull does not care for petals.”

**Jube** (Beaming, though she looks slightly terrified) “Oh, that’s fine! The flowers were mostly dead anyway. Just... try not to sickle my boots. I only have the one pair, and the left one already has a hole where a stray _Burning Hands_ went a bit sideways. We have a deal!”

**Narrative** Kaelen stands, his height making him look like a shadow cast by the volcano itself. He swings the sickle in a low, terrifyingly efficient arc, shearing through a thicket of briars like they were made of silk. Jube squeaks, hops back three feet with a loud _clank_, and starts frantically looking for her clipboard to "log the progress."

## Millicent and Kaelen

**Scene**: The interior of Millicent’s Apothecary, 'Preston’s Remedies & Milling Arts.' The air is thick with the scent of dried lavender, sharp sulfur, and ground charcoal. Millicent Preston (6'0", gaunt, in embroidered leathers and a feathered tan hat) stands aggressively behind her polished wooden counter, which is stacked with glass vials and stone mortars. She is staring intently at a low stack of charcoal-colored, heat-resistant fungi on a slate slab. ### Special Traits

- **Ventral Squeeze:** Kip can move through a space as narrow as 1 foot wide without squeezing.
- **Ash-Lung Intuition:** Kip has **Advantage** on Wisdom (Perception) checks to detect shifts in air pressure or the scent of upcoming gas leaks from the volcano.
- **Scavenger’s Luck:** Once per long rest, if Kip fails a Dexterity saving throw against an environmental hazard (like a steam vent or falling rock), he can reroll the die.
### Combat & Actions

- **Chipped Glass Shard:** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. _Hit:_ 5 (1d4+3) piercing damage.
- **Soot-Cloud (Recharge 5–6):** Kip shakes out his heavy, ash-laden cloak. All creatures in a 5-foot radius must succeed on a DC 11 Constitution saving throw or be **Blinded** until the start of Kip’s next turn.
### Spellcasting

Kip has no formal training, but his experience among volcanic ley lines has granted him some valuable on-the-job abilities.

- **At Will:** _Guidance_ (Flavor: Kip’s frantic whispering helps an ally focus).
- **1/Day:** _Expeditious Retreat_ (Flavor: A desperate burst of adrenaline).    
### Bonus Actions

- **Nimble Escape:** Kip can take the **Disengage** or **Hide** action as a bonus action on each of his turns.
- **Whistle Alarm:** Kip lets out a piercing, bird-like whistle. All allies within 60 feet who can hear him have **Advantage** on their next Initiative roll if combat hasn't started yet.    
### Reactions
- **Duck and Cover:** When Kip is targeted by a ranged attack, he can quickly drop **Prone**, imposing disadvantage on the attack roll, provided there is a piece of rubble or a crate within 5 feet to hide behind.
### Roleplaying Notes
- **Backstory** Kip is a wiry, soot-covered teenager with a permanent cough and eyes that never stop darting. He is small enough to crawl into the cooling gas vents where the most pristine obsidian "needles" form—places where a grown man would be roasted alive. He views Orion as a surrogate big brother, tethered to him by a mix of trauma and the hope of a meal.
- **Personality:** Fidgety, observant, and fiercely loyal to Orion. He speaks in short, breathless sentences. He is terrified of the volcano but more terrified of being alone.
- **Visuals:** Extremely thin with oversized boots and a leather cap pulled low. His skin is permanently stained grey, and he often has a rag tied over his mouth.
### Specific Storyline: The Vent's Secret

Kip recently found a "pocket" inside a steaming vent that contains not just obsidian, but a set of ancient Dwarven surveyor’s tools. He hasn't told Orion yet because he’s afraid Orion will make him go back in deeper to find the body they likely belonged to.
### Logical and Moral Implications

- **Child Labor:** Using Kip to squeeze into lethal gas vents is objectively horrific, yet in Hotshoe, it is one of the few ways the group survives.
- **The Burden of Protection:** If Orion dies, Kip has no standing in the village and would likely be driven out or exploited by someone far less "kind" than Orion.
- **Ecological Toll:** Kip is developing "Ash-Lung." Every day he spends scavenging for Milton’s shop is a day off the end of his life.
### Reward/Treasure/Favor

- **The Favor:** If the party gives Kip a _Necklace of Adaptation_ or a simple _Filter Mask_, he will give them the "Map of the Vents" he’s been scratching into a piece of slate, revealing safe paths through the eruption zone.
- **The Loot:** A small pouch of "Needle Obsidian" (worth 15 gp), a half-eaten ash yam, and a tarnished silver locket containing a picture of a woman who looks nothing like him.

#### **Special Traits**
- **Dwarven Resilience:** Obur has advantage on saving throws against poison.
- **Stonecunning:** Whenever Obur makes an Intelligence (History) check related to the origin of stonework, he adds double his proficiency bonus (+4) to the check.
- **Stealth Disadvantage:** Obur has disadvantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks while wearing his Chain Mail.
- **Action Surge (1/Short or Long Rest):** Obur can push himself beyond his normal limits. On his turn, he can take one additional action.
#### **Combat & Actions**
- **Multiattack:** Obur makes two attacks: one with his Mace and one Shield Bash, or one attack with his Javelin.
- **Mace:** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: `dice: 1d6+2` bludgeoning damage.
- **Shield Bash:** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: `dice: 1d6+2`bludgeoning damage. If the target is a Medium or smaller creature, it must succeed on a DC 12 Strength saving throw or be knocked prone.
- **Javelin:** _Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack:_ +4 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 30/120 ft., one target. Hit: `dice: 1d6+2`piercing damage.
#### **Spellcasting**.
Obur is a pure martial fighter and does not possess spellcasting abilities, though his "optimism" often feels like a magical aura to his allies.
#### **Bonus actions**
**Encouraging Word:** As a bonus action, Obur can shout a word of encouragement to an ally within 30 feet. That ally gains a +2 bonus to their next attack roll or saving throw made before the start of Obur's next turn.
#### **Reactions**
- **Second Wind (1/Short or Long Rest):** Obur can draw on a well of stamina to protect himself. He regains `dice: 1d10+2` hit points.
- **Intercept:** When a creature adjacent to Obur makes an attack roll, Obur can attempt to block it. He makes an attack roll with his Mace; if his result is higher than the attacker's roll, the attack misses.
#### **Roleplaying Notes**
- **Personality Trait (Optimistic):** Obur always believes the best of people and situations. Even in the middle of a goblin raid, he’s likely to remark on how lovely the weather is for a scrap.
- **Demeanor:** Warm, loud, and approachable. He is the face of the hamlet's watch and spends as much time petting local dogs as he does checking gate locks.
#### **Specific storyline**
He is employed as the day watchman of Hotstone and answers to [[Stefania]] the warden.  His nighttime relief is [[Glour]] who is better suited to the darkness.
Obur is **a fighter hiding a dark secret**. Despite his cheerful exterior, he fled a distant mountain hold after a catastrophic failure in the dwarven settlement (see the [[Dwarven cemetery]]) and mines that he blames on himself. He treats the hamlet as his second chance at redemption, which is why he is so fiercely protective of its people—he cannot afford to fail a village the way he feels he failed his clan.
#### **Logical and moral implications**
- **The Burden of the Smile:** Obur’s optimism is a mask. If the party pierces his high Deception (+4), they might find a dwarf who is terrified of his past catching up to him.
- **The Shield of the Weak:** Because of his Lawful Good nature, Obur will always prioritize the safety of commoners over the capture of a criminal. A clever thief might use a distraction to force Obur to choose between a chase and saving a falling child.
#### **Reward/treasure/favor**
- **The Favor:** Obur is a master of "the local grapevine." Befriending him gives the party advantage on checks to gather rumors or find hidden locations within the hamlet.
- **The Treasure:** If the party helps Obur face his "dark secret," he may gift them his _Shield of the Sentinel_ or a masterwork _Mace_ engraved with the Stonequarry crest.

#### **AI encounter**
**Character:** Obur (The Optimistic Watchman) **Setting:** The bustling village well during the breakfast hour. **Context:** The party has just arrived in the hamlet and is looking for information or a place to stay.
**The Script:**
- **Narrative:** A boisterous laugh rings out over the sound of clanking buckets. A stout dwarf in polished chain mail is helping an elderly villager haul water. He looks up, wiping sweat from his brow, and beams a toothy grin at you.
- **Obur:** "Ho there, travelers! Fine morning for a walk, isn't it? The birds are singing, the Orcs are staying in their holes, and the ale at the taphouse is cold. What more could a soul ask for?"
- **Interaction:** If the party asks about trouble in town, Obur’s smile falters for just a fraction of a second before returning.
- **Obur:** "Trouble? In our little slice of heaven? Nonsense! We had a fox in a chicken coop Tuesday, but [[Glour]] scared it off. Don't you worry your heads. You’re safe on my watch. Say, you look like the capable sort—keep an eye out for any 'unusual' stonework while you're exploring, would you? Old habit of mine."

## **The traveler's advisory**

Welcome to Hotshoe! If you’re reading this, you’ve likely ignored the warnings at the mountain pass and decided to see the "majestic" Mt Conniption volcano for yourself and bask in Hotshoe's famous hospitality.  While the views are breathtaking (literally, the sulfur will sear your lungs), the local wildlife is significantly less hospitable than **Dimble** at _The Thirsty Pebble_.

Before you step off the cobbles of the plaza, familiarize yourself with this guide to the wasteland’s most "enthusiastic" residents.

---

## 🌋 The Traveler’s Guide to Not Being Eaten

### 1. The Ash Spore Husk

Don't be fooled by what looks like a dried-out, hollowed-out corpse leaning against a basalt rock.

- **The Danger:** This is a fungal parasite that has completely hollowed out a previous traveler. When it senses the vibration of a footfall or the warmth of a breath, it ruptures.
    
- **The Effect:** It releases a cloud of fine, grey spores. If inhaled, these spores begin to root in your moist lung tissue, using your body heat to germinate.
    
- **Survival Tip:** If you see a "statue" made of ash that looks like it's "breathing" a fine mist, hold your breath and walk upwind. Immediately.
    

### 2. The Dust Mephit

Small, winged, and remarkably irritable, these elemental nuisances are made of grit and bad intentions.

- **The Danger:** They aren't strong enough to kill you outright, but they are masters of the "Stinging Cloud." They fly around your head, beating their wings to create a localized dust storm.
    
- **The Effect:** You’ll be blinded and coughing while they pick-pocket your shiny belongings—or lead you straight into a Sulfur-Skulker den.
    
- **Survival Tip:** A wet rag over the face is your best friend. They hate moisture; a well-aimed splash from your waterskin will turn them into harmless mud.
    

### 3. The Lesser Fire Snake

These shimmering, orange-red serpents thrive in the actual lava flows.

- **The Danger:** They are intensely hot to the touch—literally molten on the inside. They don't just bite; they cauterize.
    
- **The Effect:** Their bite leaves a "glass-burn." The heat is so intense it turns the minerals in your blood into tiny obsidian shards near the wound.
    
- **Survival Tip:** They are attracted to metal. If you hear a high-pitched hissing, drop any iron tools and move away. They’d rather melt your shovel than bite your leg.
    

### 4. The L'zoir

A creature of pure, jagged geometry. The L'zoir is a crystalline predator that looks like a cluster of floating obsidian needles.

- **The Danger:** They are nearly invisible against the shimmering heat waves of the "Smoking Throat." They move by snapping their crystal "limbs" together, creating a sound like breaking glass.
    
- **The Effect:** They are "Heat-Vampires." They don't want your meat; they want your thermal energy. If one latches onto you, you will experience sudden, lethal hypothermia in 100°C weather.
    
- **Survival Tip:** If you feel a sudden, unnatural chill, start a fire. The L'zoir will go for the campfire and leave your body heat alone.
    

### 5. The Scald Scale Serpent

A massive, thick-bodied constrictor that lives in the boiling mud pits.

- **The Danger:** Its scales are constantly coated in a layer of boiling-hot slime.
    
- **The Effect:** If it wraps around you, it doesn't just crush your ribs; it boils your skin off. The "Scald-Squeeze" is a messy way to go.
    
- **Survival Tip:** Watch the mud. If you see bubbles that aren't rhythmic, it's not a gas vent—it's the Serpent's breath.
    

### 6. The Xorn Youngling

While adult Xorns are content to eat gems deep underground, the Younglings are surface-dwelling scavengers with a very specific appetite.

- **The Danger:** These three-armed, three-legged rock-beings think your "magical" items or high-mineral gear (like **Durn's** old tools) look like a five-course meal.
    
- **The Effect:** They will relentlessly pursue anyone carrying high-grade ore or enchanted trinkets, "swimming" through the earth and popping up beneath your feet like a hungry, stony tripod.
    
- **Survival Tip:** Toss them a handful of raw iron ore or a cheap gemstone. They have the attention span of a toddler and will stop to eat the "treat" while you make your escape.

**Here are the most rare yet prominent threats beyond Hotshoe's well traveled trails.**
The volcanic wasteland surrounding Hotshoe is a graveyard for traditional biology. To survive the caustic air and the searing heat of Mt Conniption, the creatures here have evolved thermal life form adaptations that often depend upon the very minerals and ash they walk upon into their anatomy.

### 1. The Ash-Mantle ( _Testudo Cinis_ )

Commonly mistaken for a pile of cooling lava, this ambush predator is the bane of careless travelers in the volcanic wastes.

- **Appearance:** A large, tortoise-like creature with a shell made of fused basalt and obsidian. It remains perfectly still in the ash fields, venting steam from small pores in its back to mimic a thermal vent.
    
- **Behavior:** When a scavenger walks over it, the Mantle snaps its massive, beak-like jaws. Its saliva is highly acidic, designed to melt through even Durn's **[[Hot yam fireproof boot polish]]**.
    
- **Harvest:** Its shell is highly prized by Millicent for making alchemical catalysts.
- ![[ash mantle.png|395]]


### 2. The Sulfur-Skulker ( _Vulpis Mephitis_ )

A lean, six-legged canine with translucent, yellow-tinted skin.

- **Appearance:** You rarely see a Skulker; you smell it first. They exude a concentrated sulfur gas that creates a yellow haze around them. They have no eyes, instead sensing the "vibrations" of heat signatures.
    
- **Behavior:** They hunt in packs, surrounding their prey and "gas-choking" them. Once the victim faints from lack of oxygen, the Skulkers move in.
    
- **The "Kip" Strategy:** Kip has learned that if you carry a satchel of crushed dried lavender and mint, it "confuses" their scent-receptors, making you invisible to them.
- **Appearance**  The Sulfur-Skulker is a lean, six-legged canine predator that thrives in the yellow-hazed sulfur pools of the volcanic wasteland. Its translucent skin is yellow-tinted and constantly exudes a toxic sulfur gas. Because it hunts by sensing vibrations and heat signatures rather than by sight, it is completely eyeless. The Skulkers use the yellow fog created by their own gases to ambush prey, surrounding victims and choking them before they can even react.
        
### 3. Glass-Winged Harrier ( _Accipiter Vitrum_ )

The apex aerial predator of the Vane slopes.

- **Appearance:** A massive bird of prey whose feathers are coated in a layer of natural volcanic glass. In the sunlight, they look like a flying kaleidoscope.
- **Behavior:** They don't use talons to kill; they use **friction**. They dive at high speeds, and the glass edges of their wings act like flying razors, slicing through prey before it even hears the screech.
- The **Glass-Winged Harrier** (_Accipiter Vitrum_) is the apex aerial predator of the Vane slopes, a master of high-altitude thermal currents and lethal velocity. It is a biological marvel that has evolved to incorporate the volcano’s most dangerous byproduct—obsidian—directly into its physiology.
- **Detailed Appearance**
	- **External Anatomy:**
		- **Vitrificated Plumage:** The Harrier’s most striking feature is its feathers. Each feather is coated in a micro-thin layer of natural volcanic glass. This doesn't just provide protection from the heat; it makes the bird a living kaleidoscope. In mid-flight, the Harrier can tilt its wings to reflect sunlight, momentarily blinding prey below with a "solar flash."
		- **The Razor-Edge:** The primary flight feathers on the wingtips are not soft. They are rigid, serrated blades of obsidian. The Harrier does not kill with its beak; it performs "fly-by" slashes, using its momentum to slice through leather and flesh like a hot wire through wax.
		- **Nictitating Membrane (The "Blast-Shield"):** To survive the high-speed dives through ash-choked air, the Harrier has a secondary, transparent eyelid made of a flexible, horn-like material. This allows it to keep its eyes open even during a 200 mph descent through a grit-storm.
	- **Internal Structure: The "Pneumatic Frame"**
		- **Carbonized Bone Matrix:** To support the weight of its glass-coated feathers, the Harrier’s bones are reinforced with carbon filaments. They are incredibly light but have the structural integrity of industrial struts.
		- **Thermal Reservoirs:** The bird has specialized air sacs along its chest. These sacs don't just help with breathing; they act as "heat batteries." The Harrier can store the ambient heat of a thermal vent and release it slowly to keep its muscles supple during the cold, high-altitude glides above the cloud layer.
		- **Magnetite Cranial Core:** Inside the Harrier’s skull is a small cluster of magnetite crystals. This acts as a biological compass, allowing the bird to navigate the "magnetic fog" of the volcano that often leaves human travelers (and even **Millicent’s** instruments) spinning in circles.
    **Predatory Mechanics**
		- **The Thermal Climb:** The Harrier circles the "Smoking Throat," riding the rising heat until it is nearly invisible to those below.
		- **The Kinetic Dive:** Folding its wings into a tight wedge, it enters a near-vertical drop. The glass feathers vibrate at a high frequency, creating a distinctive "whistling scream" that is often the last thing a victim hears.
		- **The Friction Kill:** Rather than grabbing prey, it flares its wings at the last second, using the serrated obsidian edges to deliver a massive kinetic blow.

### 4. The Obsidian Wraith ( _Spiritus Obsidianus_ )

A semi-incorporeal entity believed to be the "ghosts" of the mountain.

- **Appearance:** They look like heat-shimmer made manifest—dark, twisting shadows that appear to be made of floating obsidian shards.  The **Obsidian Wraith** (_Spiritus Obsidianus_) is perhaps the most misunderstood entity in the Hotshoe wasteland. While the superstitious see them as the ghosts of fallen miners, **Millicent Preston’s** alchemical journals describe them as "high-density magnetic particulates suspended in a localized gravitational pocket."  They are less a biological creature and more a sentient atmospheric phenomenon.   
- **Behavior:** They don't attack physically but "drain" the resolve of anyone they pass through, leaving the victim feeling cold and hopeless amidst the flames.

To a traveler, this feels like a "spectral chill," but it is actually the Wraith's magnetic field pulling the heat out of the victim's body to power its own movement.
	- **The "Lead-Pulse":** The Wraith emits a rhythmic electromagnetic pulse. In humans, this interferes with the electrical signals of the nervous system. Victims report a sudden sense of "hopelessness," heavy limbs, and a metallic taste in the mouth—symptoms often mistaken for a haunting, but actually caused by **localized EM interference.*

# Monster Statistics

A monster’s statistics, sometimes referred to as its **stat block**, provide the essential information that you need to run the monster. 

## Size 
A monster can be Tiny, Small, Medium, Large, Huge, or Gargantuan. The Size Categories table shows how much space a creature of a particular size controls in combat. See the *Player’s Handbook* for more information on creature size and space. 

**Table- Size Categories**

| ---------- | ---------------------- | ------------------- |
| Tiny       | 2½ by 2½ ft.           | Imp, sprite         |
| Small      | 5 by 5 ft.             | Giant rat, goblin   |
| Medium     | 5 by 5 ft.             | Orc, werewolf       |
| Large      | 10 by 10 ft.           | Hippogriff, ogre    |
| Huge       | 15 by 15 ft.           | Fire giant, treant  |
| Gargantuan | 20 by 20 ft. or larger | Kraken, purple worm |
|            |                        |                     |

> **Modifying Creatures**
> Despite the versatile collection of monsters in this book, you might be at a loss when it comes to finding the perfect creature for part of an adventure. Feel free to tweak an existing creature to make it into something more useful for you, perhaps by borrowing a trait or two from a different monster or by using a **variant** or **template**, such as the ones in this book. Keep in mind that modifying a monster, including when you apply a template to it, might change its challenge rating

## Type

A monster's type speaks to its fundamental nature. Certain spells, magic items, class features, and other effects in the game interact in special ways with creatures of a particular type. For example, an *arrow of dragon slaying* deals extra damage not only to dragons but also other creatures of the dragon type, such as dragon turtles and wyverns.

The game includes the following monster types, which have no rules of their own.

**Aberrations** are utterly alien beings. Many of them have innate magical abilities drawn from the creature's alien mind rather than the mystical forces of the world. The quintessential aberrations are aboleths, beholders, mind flayers, and slaadi.

**Beasts** are nonhumanoid creatures that are a natural part of the fantasy ecology. Some of them have magical powers, but most are unintelligent and lack any society or language. Beasts include all varieties of ordinary animals, dinosaurs, and giant versions of animals.

**Celestials** are creatures native to the Upper Planes. Many of them are the servants of deities, employed as messengers or agents in the mortal realm and throughout the planes. Celestials are good by nature, so the exceptional celestial who strays from a good alignment is a horrifying rarity. Celestials include angels, couatls, and pegasi.

**Constructs** are made, not born. Some are programmed by their creators to follow a simple set of instructions, while others are imbued with sentience and capable of independent thought. Golems are the iconic constructs. Many creatures native to the outer plane of Mechanus, such as modrons, are constructs shaped from the raw material of the plane by the will of more powerful creatures.

**Dragons** are large reptilian creatures of ancient origin and tremendous power. True dragons, including the good metallic dragons and the evil chromatic dragons, are highly intelligent and have innate magic. Also in this category are creatures distantly related to true dragons, but less powerful, less intelligent, and less magical, such as wyverns and pseudodragons.

**Elementals** are creatures native to the elemental planes. Some creatures of this type are little more than animate masses of their respective elements, including the creatures simply called elementals. Others have biological forms infused with elemental energy. The races of genies, including djinn and efreet, form the most important civilizations on the elemental planes. Other elemental creatures include azers, invisible stalkers, and water weirds.

**Fey** are magical creatures closely tied to the forces of nature. They dwell in twilight groves and misty forests. In some worlds, they are closely tied to the Feywild, also called the Plane of Faerie. Some are also found in the Outer Planes, particularly the planes of Arborea and the Beastlands. Fey include dryads, pixies, and satyrs.

**Fiends** are creatures of wickedness that are native to the Lower Planes. A few are the servants of deities, but many more labor under the leadership of archdevils and demon princes. Evil priests and mages sometimes summon fiends to the material world to do their bidding. If an evil celestial is a rarity, a good fiend is almost inconceivable. Fiends include demons, devils, hell hounds, rakshasas, and yugoloths.

**Giants** tower over humans and their kind. They are humanlike in shape, though some have multiple heads (ettins) or deformities (fomorians). The six varieties of true giant are hill giants, stone giants, frost giants, fire giants, cloud giants, and storm giants. Besides these, creatures such as ogres and trolls are giants.

**Humanoids** are the main peoples of a fantasy gaming world, both civilized and savage, including humans and a tremendous variety of other species. They have language and culture, few if any innate magical abilities (though most humanoids can learn spellcasting), and a bipedal form. The most common humanoid races are the ones most suitable as player characters: humans, dwarves, elves, and halflings. Almost as numerous but far more savage and brutal, and almost uniformly evil, are the races of goblinoids (goblins, hobgoblins, and bugbears), orcs, gnolls, lizardfolk, and kobolds.

**Monstrosities** are monsters in the strictest sense-frightening creatures that are not ordinary, not truly natural, and almost never benign. Some are the results of magical experimentation gone awry (such as owlbears), and others are the product of terrible curses (including minotaurs and yuan-ti). They defy categorization, and in some sense serve as a catch-all category for creatures that don't fit into any other type.

**Oozes** are gelatinous creatures that rarely have a fixed shape. They are mostly subterranean, dwelling in caves and dungeons and feeding on refuse, carrion, or creatures unlucky enough to get in their way. Black puddings and gelatinous cubes are among the most recognizable oozes.

**Plants** in this context are vegetable creatures, not ordinary flora. Most of them are ambulatory, and some are carnivorous. The quintessential plants are the shambling mound and the treant. Fungal creatures such as the gas spore and the myconid also fall into this category.

**Undead** are once-living creatures brought to a horrifying state of undeath through the practice of necromantic magic or some unholy curse. Undead include walking corpses, such as vampires and zombies, as well as bodiless spirits, such as ghosts and specters.

### Tags

A monster might have one or more tags appended to its type, in parentheses. For example, an orc has the *humanoid (orc)* type. The parenthetical tags provide additional categorization for certain creatures. The tags have no rules of their own, but something in the game, such as a magic item, might refer to them. For instance, a spear that is especially effective at fighting demons would work against any monster that has the demon tag.

## Alignment

A monster's alignment provides a clue to its disposition and how it behaves in a roleplaying or combat situation. For example, a chaotic evil monster might be difficult to reason with and might attack characters on sight, whereas a neutral monster might be willing to negotiate. See the *Player's Handbook* for descriptions of the different alignments.

The alignment specified in a monster's stat block is the default. Feel free to depart from it and change a monster's alignment to suit the needs of your campaign. If you want a good-aligned green dragon or an evil storm giant, there's nothing stopping you.

Some creatures can have **any alignment**. In other words, you choose the monster's alignment. Some monster's alignment entry indicates a tendency or aversion toward law, chaos, good, or evil. For example, a berserker can be any chaotic alignment (chaotic good, chaotic neutral, or chaotic evil), as befits its wild nature.

Many creatures of low intelligence have no comprehension of law or chaos, good or evil. They don't make moral or ethical choices, but rather act on instinct. These creatures are **unaligned**, which means they don't have an alignment.

## Armor Class

A monster that wears armor or carries a shield has an Armor Class (AC) that takes its armor, shield, and Dexterity into account. Otherwise, a monster's AC is based on its Dexterity modifier and natural armor, if any. If a monster has natural armor, wears armor, or carries a shield, this is noted in parentheses after its AC value.

## Hit Points

A monster usually dies or is destroyed when it drops to 0 hit points. For more on hit points, see the *Player's Handbook*.

A monster's hit points are presented both as a die expression and as an average number. For example, a monster with 2d8 hit points has 9 hit points on average (2 × 4½).

A monster's size determines the die used to calculate its hit points, as shown in the Hit Dice by Size table.

## Speed

A monster's speed tells you how far it can move on its turn. For more information on speed, see the *Player's Handbook*.

All creatures have a walking speed, simply called the monster's speed. Creatures that have no form of ground-based locomotion have a walking speed of 0 feet.

Some creatures have one or more of the following additional movement modes.

### Burrow

A monster that has a burrowing speed can use that speed to move through sand, earth, mud, or ice. A monster can't burrow through solid rock unless it has a special trait that allows it to do so.

### Climb

A monster that has a climbing speed can use all or part of its movement to move on vertical surfaces. The monster doesn't need to spend extra movement to climb.

### Fly

A monster that has a flying speed can use all or part of its movement to fly. Some monsters have the ability to **hover**, which makes them hard to knock out of the air (as explained in the rules on flying in the *Player's Handbook*). Such a monster stops hovering when it dies.

### Swim

A monster that has a swimming speed doesn't need to spend extra movement to swim.

## Ability Scores

Every monster has six ability scores (Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma) and corresponding modifiers. For more information on ability scores and how they're used in play, see the *Player's Handbook*.

## Skills

The Skills entry is reserved for monsters that are proficient in one or more skills. For example, a monster that is very perceptive and stealthy might have bonuses to Wisdom (Perception) and Dexterity (Stealth) checks.

A skill bonus is the sum of a monster's relevant ability modifier and its proficiency bonus, which is determined by the monster's challenge rating (as shown in the Proficiency Bonus by Challenge Rating table). Other modifiers might apply. For instance, a monster might have a larger-than-expected bonus (usually double its proficiency bonus) to account for its heightened expertise.

***Armor, Weapon, and Tool Proficiencies***

*Assume that a creature is proficient with its armor, weapons, and tools. If you swap them out, you decide whether the creature is proficient with its new equipment.*

*For example, a hill giant typically wears hide armor and wields a greatclub. You could equip a hill giant with chain mail and a greataxe instead, and assume the giant is proficient with both, one or the other, or neither.*

*See the Player's Handbook for rules on using armor or weapons without proficiency.*

## Vulnerabilities, Resistances, and Immunities

Some creatures have vulnerability, resistance, or immunity to certain types of damage. Particular creatures are even resistant or immune to damage from nonmagical attacks (a magical attack is an attack delivered by a spell, a magic item, or another magical source). In addition, some creatures are immune to certain conditions.

## Senses

The Senses entry notes a monster's passive Wisdom (Perception) score, as well as any special senses the monster might have. Special senses are described below.

### Blindsight

A monster with blindsight can perceive its surroundings without relying on sight, within a specific radius.

Creatures without eyes, such as grimlocks and gray oozes, typically have this special sense, as do creatures with echolocation or heightened senses, such as bats and true dragons.

If a monster is naturally blind, it has a parenthetical note to this effect, indicating that the radius of its blindsight defines the maximum range of its perception.

### Darkvision

A monster with darkvision can see in the dark within a specific radius. The monster can see in dim light within the radius as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. The monster can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray. Many creatures that live underground have this special sense.

### Tremorsense

A monster with tremorsense can detect and pinpoint the origin of vibrations within a specific radius, provided that the monster and the source of the vibrations are in contact with the same ground or substance. Tremorsense can't be used to detect flying or incorporeal creatures. Many burrowing creatures, such as ankhegs and umber hulks, have this special sense.

### Truesight

A monster with truesight can, out to a specific range, see in normal and magical darkness, see invisible creatures and objects, automatically detect visual illusions and succeed on saving throws against them, and perceive the original form of a shapechanger or a creature that is transformed by magic. Furthermore, the monster can see into the Ethereal Plane within the same range.

## Languages

The languages that a monster can speak are listed in alphabetical order. Sometimes a monster can understand a language but can't speak it, and this is noted in its entry. A "-" indicates that a creature neither speaks nor understands any language.

### Telepathy

Telepathy is a magical ability that allows a monster to communicate mentally with another creature within a specified range. The contacted creature doesn't need to share a language with the monster to communicate in this way with it, but it must be able to understand at least one language. A creature without telepathy can receive and respond to telepathic messages but can't initiate or terminate a telepathic conversation.

A telepathic monster doesn't need to see a contacted creature and can end the telepathic contact at any time. The contact is broken as soon as the two creatures are no longer within range of each other or if the telepathic monster contacts a different creature within range. A telepathic monster can initiate or terminate a telepathic conversation without using an action, but while the monster is incapacitated, it can't initiate telepathic contact, and any current contact is terminated.

A creature within the area of an *antimagic field* or in any other location where magic doesn't function can't send or receive telepathic messages.

## Challenge

A monster's **Challenge rating** tells you how great a threat the monster is. An appropriately equipped and well-rested party of four adventurers should be able to defeat a monster that has a challenge rating equal to its level without suffering any deaths. For example, a party of four 3rd-level characters should find a monster with a challenge rating of 3 to be a worthy challenge, but not a deadly one.

Monsters that are significantly weaker than 1st- level characters have a challenge rating lower than 1. Monsters with a challenge rating of 0 are insignificant except in large numbers; those with no effective attacks are worth no experience points, while those that have attacks are worth 10 XP each.

Some monsters present a greater challenge than even a typical 20th-level party can handle. These monsters have a challenge rating of 21 or higher and are specifically designed to test player skill.

### Experience Points

The number of experience points (XP) a monster is worth is based on its challenge rating. Typically, XP is awarded for defeating the monster, although the GM may also award XP for neutralizing the threat posed by the monster in some other manner.

Unless something tells you otherwise, a monster summoned by a spell or other magical ability is worth the XP noted in its stat block.

## Special Traits

Special traits (which appear after a monster's challenge rating but before any actions or reactions) are characteristics that are likely to be relevant in a combat encounter and that require some explanation.

### Innate Spellcasting

A monster with the innate ability to cast spells has the Innate Spellcasting special trait. Unless noted otherwise, an innate spell of 1st level or higher is always cast at its lowest possible level and can't be cast at a higher level. If a monster has a cantrip where its level matters and no level is given, use the monster's challenge rating.

An innate spell can have special rules or restrictions. For example, a drow mage can innately cast the *levitate* spell, but the spell has a "self only" restriction, which means that the spell affects only the drow mage.

A monster's innate spells can't be swapped out with other spells. If a monster's innate spells don't require attack rolls, no attack bonus is given for them.

### Spellcasting

A monster with the Spellcasting special trait has a spellcaster level and spell slots, which it uses to cast its spells of 1st level and higher (as explained in the *Player's Handbook*). The spellcaster level is also used for any cantrips included in the feature.

The monster has a list of spells known or prepared from a specific class. The list might also include spells from a feature in that class, such as the Divine Domain feature of the cleric or the Druid Circle feature of the druid. The monster is considered a member of that class when attuning to or using a magic item that requires membership in the class or access to its spell list.

A monster can cast a spell from its list at a higher level if it has the spell slot to do so. For example, a drow mage with the 3rd-level *lightning bolt* spell can cast it as a 5th-level spell by using one of its 5th-level greater or lesser threat than suggested by its challenge rating.

### Psionics

A monster that casts spells using only the power of its mind has the psionics tag added to its Spellcasting or Innate Spellcasting special trait. This tag carries no special rules of its own, but other parts of the game might refer to it. A monster that has this tag typically doesn't require any components to cast its spells.

## Actions

When a monster takes its action, it can choose from the options in the Actions section of its stat block or use one of the actions available to all creatures, such as the Dash or Hide action, as described in the *Player's Handbook*.

### Melee and Ranged Attacks

The most common actions that a monster will take in combat are melee and ranged attacks. These can be spell attacks or weapon attacks, where the "weapon" might be a manufactured item or a natural weapon, such as a claw or tail spike. For more information on different kinds of attacks, see the *Player's Handbook*.

***Creature vs Target***. The target of a melee or ranged attack is usually either one creature or one target, the difference being that a "target" can be a creature or an object.

***Hit***. Any damage dealt or other effects that occur as a result of an attack hitting a target are described after the "Hit" notation. You have the option of taking average damage or rolling the damage; for this reason, both the average damage and the die expression are presented.

***Miss***. If an attack has an effect that occurs on a miss, that information is presented after the "Miss:" notation.

***Grapple Rules for Monsters***

*Many monsters have special attacks that allow them to quickly grapple prey. When a monster hits with such an attack, it doesn't need to make an additional ability check to determine whether the grapple succeeds, unless the attack says otherwise.*

*A creature grappled by the monster can use its action to try to escape. To do so, it must succeed on a Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check against the escape DC in the monster's stat block. If no escape DC is given, assume the DC is 10+the monster's Strength (Athletics) modifier.*

### Multiattack

A creature that can make multiple attacks on its turn has the Multiattack action. A creature can't use Multiattack when making an opportunity attack, which must be a single melee attack.

### Ammunition

A monster carries enough ammunition to make its ranged attacks. You can assume that a monster has 2d4 pieces of ammunition for a thrown weapon attack, and 2d10 pieces of ammunition for a projectile weapon such as a bow or crossbow.

## Reactions

If a monster can do something special with its reaction, that information is contained here. If a creature has no special reaction, this section is absent.

## Limited Usage

Some special abilities have restrictions on the number of times they can be used.

***X/Day***. The notation "X/Day" means a special ability can be used X number of times and that a monster must finish a long rest to regain expended uses. For example, "1/Day" means a special ability can be used once and that the monster must finish a long rest to use it again.

***Recharge X-Y***. The notation "Recharge X-Y" means a monster can use a special ability once and that the ability then has a random chance of recharging during each subsequent round of combat. At the start of each of the monster's turns, roll a d6. If the roll is one of the numbers in the recharge notation, the monster regains the use of the special ability. The ability also recharges when the monster finishes a short or long rest.

For example, "Recharge 5-6" means a monster can use the special ability once. Then, at the start of the monster's turn, it regains the use of that ability if it rolls a 5 or 6 on a d6.

***Recharge after a Short or Long Rest***. This notation means that a monster can use a special ability once and then must finish a short or long rest to use it again.

## Equipment

A stat block rarely refers to equipment, other than armor or weapons used by a monster. A creature that customarily wears clothes, such as a humanoid, is assumed to be dressed appropriately.

You can equip monsters with additional gear and trinkets however you like, and you decide how much of a monster's equipment is recoverable after the creature is slain and whether any of that equipment is still usable. A battered suit of armor made for a monster is rarely usable by someone else, for instance.

If a spellcasting monster needs material components to cast its spells, assume that it has the material components it needs to cast the spells in its stat block.

# Legendary Creatures

A legendary creature can do things that ordinary creatures can't. It can take special actions outside its turn, and it might exert magical influence for miles around.

If a creature assumes the form of a legendary creature, such as through a spell, it doesn't gain that form's legendary actions, lair actions, or regional effects.

## Legendary Actions

A legendary creature can take a certain number of special actions-called legendary actions-outside its turn. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. A creature regains its spent legendary actions at the start of its turn. It can forgo using them, and it can't use them while incapacitated or otherwise unable to take actions. If surprised, it can't use them until after its first turn in the combat.

## A Legendary Creature's Lair

A legendary creature might have a section describing its lair and the special effects it can create while there, either by act of will or simply by being present. Such a section applies only to a legendary creature that spends a great deal of time in its lair.

### Lair Actions

If a legendary creature has lair actions, it can use them to harness the ambient magic in its lair. On initiative count 20 (losing all initiative ties), it can use one of its lair action options. It can't do so while incapacitated or otherwise unable to take actions. If surprised, it can't use one until after its first turn in the combat.

### Regional Effects

The mere presence of a legendary creature can have strange and wondrous effects on its environment, as noted in this section. Regional effects end abruptly or dissipate over time when the legendary creature dies.

### Botanical Classification: _Dioscorea Cinis_ (The Ash Yam)

The **Ash Yam** is a geological anomaly as much as a biological one. It is a lithophilic tuber that thrives exclusively in the high-acidity, mineral-dense silts of active volcanic regions. While most flora withers under the touch of Mount Vane’s breath, the Ash Yam uses the heat to accelerate its unique subterranean fermentation.
### Physical Characteristics

- **Vining Structure:** Above ground, the Ash Yam presents as a series of creeping, heart-shaped leaves. The foliage is a deep, bruised emerald with jagged silver veins. These leaves are covered in microscopic, glass-like hairs (trichomes) that trap falling volcanic ash, absorbing nitrogen directly from the debris.
    
- **The Tuber (The "Heart"):** The edible portion is a cylindrical root that can grow up to 18 inches in length.
    
    - **Periderm (Skin):** The outer layer is obsidian-black, rough, and scorched in appearance. It is incredibly tough, requiring a sharp blade to penetrate.
        
    - **Pulp (Flesh):** The interior is a dense, marbled starch. It ranges from a pale lavender to a deep, royal violet. When cut, it bleeds a thick, milky sap that smells faintly of toasted sugar and sulfur.
        
- **Root System:** The plant does not seek water; it seeks heat. Its secondary roots wrap around subterranean basalt formations, drawing warmth to keep the tuber in a constant state of "slow-cook" while still in the earth.
    
---
### Lifecycle & Ecology

- **The "Fire-Bloom":** The Ash Yam only flowers during periods of high seismic activity. It produces a singular, soot-grey blossom that opens at night, pollinated by ash-moths.
    
- **Thermo-bio-chemical growth:** The thermo-bio-chemical processes in the roots that reach down to the heat provide the energy resources for growth similar to sunshine and photosynthesis.  Ash-yam has green leaves as a back-up redundant source of energy and production of the other chemical processes for growth.  
    
- **Relationship with the Volcano:** The plant acts as a heat sink. In areas where Ash Yams are densely packed, the ground temperature is noticeably cooler, as the roots absorb the thermal energy to drive biological growth.
---
### Known Varieties in Hotshoe

| Variety         | Appearance                    | Culinary Profile                                       |
| --------------- | ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Soot-Skin**   | Pure black skin, white flesh. | Very starchy; best for heavy breads.                   |
| **Vane-Violet** | Marbled purple flesh.         | Sweetest variety; Dimble's "official" choice for beer. |
| **Ember-Vein**  | Red-streaked interior.        | Spicy and slightly metallic; considered a delicacy.    |
### Alchemical & Arcane Properties

> "The Ash Yam is a bridge. It eats the death of the mountain and turns it into the life of the village. It is the only thing in Hotshoe that enjoys being buried." — **Kaelen the Harvester**

- **Heat relief medicine:** Consuming the raw sap provides a temporary (1-hour) resistance to environmental heat, though it causes mild hallucinations of "fire-spirits."

- **The Process:**  Sharp crystalline obsidian dust clumps when mixed with ink meant to withhold the rigors of magic craft.  Millicent is refining Ash Yam extracts as a binding agent that will be durable and adhere reliably to all objects for magic crafts.

- Gloves and aprons made from fabrics impregnated with the paste provides incredible heat resistance. It’s the secret behind why her gear never looks singed,.

##  Hotshoe Ash Yam Bread

_Yields: 1 dense loaf | Prep time: 20 mins | Bake time: 45 mins_

### Ingredients

- **2 cups Ash Yam puree:** (Boiled until soft, peeled, and mashed—should be a deep violet color).
    
- **3 cups Coarse Flour:** (Barley or Oat flour works best to match the rustic texture).
    
- **1/4 cup cinderberry syrup:**
    
- **1 tsp Salt:** from up by the vents.
    
- **1 tbsp Yeast:** (Activated in lukewarm water).
    
- **1/4 cup Melted Fat:** (Lard or vegetable oil).
    
- **Optional:** A pinch of dried lavender or rosemary to mask the faint scent of sulfur.
    
---
### Instructions

1. **Prepare the Tubers:** Scrub the obsidian-black skins of the ash yams thoroughly. Boil them whole until a knife slides through the center. Peel while warm—the skin should slip off like charred paper—and mash until no lumps remain.
    
2. **Activate the Rise:** In a small bowl, combine your yeast with a splash of warm water and a teaspoon of the honey. Let it sit until it foams (about 10 minutes).
    
3. **Mix the Dough:** In a large wooden trough or bowl, combine the yam puree, the remaining honey, salt, and melted fat. Gradually fold in the flour and the yeast mixture.
    
    > **Note:** Ash yam dough is much stickier than standard dough. Do not be tempted to add too much flour, or you’ll end up with a brick.
    
4. **Knead with Intent:** Knead the purple mass for about 8 minutes. It should be elastic but still slightly tacky to the touch.
    
5. **First Rise:** Place in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth (to keep the ash out!), and let it rise in a warm spot for 1 hour. It won’t double in size like wheat bread, but it will puff significantly.
    
6. **The Bake:** Preheat your oven (or Dutch oven over a bed of coals) to **190°C (375°F)**. Shape the dough into a round boule and score the top with a sharp obsidian flake.
    
7. **Final Fire:** Bake for 45–50 minutes. The crust should be dark and hard, sounding hollow when tapped on the bottom.
    

---

### Serving Suggestions

- **The "Dimble" Special:** Serve warm with a thick slab of salted butter and a mug of _The Thirsty Pebble’s_ soot-stout.
    
- **The "Kaelen" Method:** Slice thin and toast over an open flame until the edges char, then spread some cinderberry jam.
    

### Logical and Moral Implications

Because Ash Yams grow in volcanic soil, they absorb high concentrations of minerals. Eating this bread daily is said to strengthen the bones, but long-term consumption results in **"Grey-Tongue,"** a permanent darkening of the mouth tissues—a tell-tale sign of a true Hotshoe local.

--- FILE: Ash yam fireproof boot polish.md ---
![[ash_yam_boot_polish.jpg|389]]

This boot polish creates a thermal barrier that reflects heat and resists the caustic nature of volcanic ash and other hot surfaces. Similar to asbestos. 

### Explanation

In the treacherous, heat-scarred terrain of Hotshoe, a pair of boots isn't just footwear—it's life insurance. Standard leather shreds, cracks and curls walking on the sharp cinders, vents of steam or gas and recently cooled lava. [[Orion]]'s crew of [[Durn]] and [[Kip]] have found a way to "fire proofing" their leather gear with extract from Ash Yam roots.

_Yields: 1 small tin (enough for 3 coatings) | Cure time: 24 hours_

### Ingredients

- **1/2 cup Ash Yam Roots:** Deeper dug are best quality.  Dried and ground into a fine powder.  Note: [[Millicent]] is the expert in this technique.
    
- **3 tbsp Ash Yam Sap:** Freshly squeezed out of from the yam flesh. 
    
- **2 tbsp Rendered Tallow or Beeswax:** (The base to keep the leather supple).
    
- **1 tsp Finely Crushed Magnetite:** (Commonly found in the volcanic sand, this helps the polish "grip" the leather fibers).
    
- **1 drop Lavender Oil:** (Optional; purely to keep your boots from smelling like a sulfur pit).
    
---

### Tools

- **Mortar and Pestle:** To grind the skins to an impalpable dust.
- **Small Iron Pot:** For melting the wax over a low flame.
- **Steel-grass Brush:** For deep application.
    

---

### Instructions

1. **The Carbon Base:** Take the black, scorched skins of the [[Ash yam]]. Roast them over a flame until they are bone-dry, then grind them in the mortar until they are as fine as soot.
2. **The Binding:** Melt your tallow or wax in the iron pot. Once liquid, slowly whisk in the milky Ash Yam sap. The mixture will begin to thicken and turn a deep, bruised purple.
3. **The Integration:** Fold in the powdered skins and the magnetite sand. Stir constantly for five minutes over very low heat. The polish should transform into a shimmering, ink-black paste.
4. **The Application:** * Clean your boots of all grit.
    - Apply the polish while it is still warm (but not scalding) using the stiff brush.
    - Work it into the seams and the welt—this is where the heat usually kills a boot first.
5. **The Heat-Set:** Once coated, hold the boot near (but not _in_) a heat source for ten minutes. This "melts" the sap into the pores of the leather, creating a permanent bond.
6. **Buffing:** Once cool, buff with a rough rag. The result should be a dull, obsidian-like sheen.

---

### Performance Notes

- **Heat Resistance:** Boots treated with this polish allow the wearer to stand on cooling lava crust (approx. **500°C**) for up to 30 seconds without the soles melting or the leather scorching.
- **Waterproofing:** The sap creates a natural latex-like barrier, making the boots entirely waterproof—essential for the acidic steam bogs at the mountain's base.
- **The "Kip" Advantage:** Because the polish contains magnetite, a wearer with high sensitivity (or a compass) can actually "feel" the pull of the mountain’s magnetic North through their feet.

### Moral & Logical Implications

This recipe is a closely guarded secret of the scavengers. **Millicent** has her own ways of crafting the protective fabric clothing for alchemy work with hot materials.  

So far, no one has thought of making yarn or thread by stripping the skin off of the ash yam roots.

Durn dipped a stiff brush into a pot of ash yam polish, the thick, purple-black paste shimmering like oil on water. Kip sat across from him, watching with wide eyes, his own smaller boots already stripped of their laces.

--- FILE: Thermal based life forms.md ---
Hotshoe is the perfect storm of opportunities because of the tensions present in the landscape between the carbon-based life system and a new one based on thermal energy.  The thermal energy life system is rooted in an excess of thermal energy and emergence of 'plants' with catalysts that can replace or augment photosynthesis and animal that can feed on.  The same new thermal energy life force is evident in the animals/monsters that can feed upon them.  This uproar in life chemistry is rife with opportunity to secure potential new resources for the magical ink industry.

## **The Hotshoe hook**
#### **Volcanic unrest:** 
The village sits in the shadow of a grumbling volcano. For year, an intermittent fine, grey ash has been falling like snow; sometimes light and others quite heavy. The villagers are terrified of an eruption, but over time the local cemetery has developed a more immediate, localized problem: the bodies aren't staying buried, but they aren't "undead" (zombies) in the traditional sense.  Someone needs to check on the [[Dwarven cemetery]].

#### **Nerull's touch:** 
The volcanic ash has become magically infused with brimstone and necrotic energy from a deep pocket of gas and steam touched by Nerull released by recent tremors. This ash has a perverse fertilizing quality according to certain [[Kaelen|druids of Nerull]]. Instead of ghosts or zombies, the corpses in the cemetery are being "overgrown" by charcoal-colored, heat-resistant luminescent  fungi that use the bones as a trellis and source of decay.

In the shadow of Mount Conniption are large areas where the soil is less a source of life and more a chemical challenge. To grow in these areas of Hotshoe ecosystem, a plant must either process heavy metals, withstand boiling groundwater, or—in some cases—supplement its diet with these materials and local wildlife.

The following botanical guide covers the essential flora that defines the landscape outside the hamlet's walls.

## 🌿 The Hotshoe Botanical Guide

### 1. Cinder-Berry Bush (_Rubus Pyrophilus_)

**Category:** Fruit-Bearing Bush

- **Appearance:** A low, sprawling shrub with waxy, silver-grey leaves that reflect the harsh summer sun. The berries themselves are a deep, translucent orange, resembling small glowing coals.
- **Physiology:** The Cinder-Berry uses an alternative to photosynthesis with a heat differential  root system.  Hot/cold oscillation along the root system drives a chemical reaction that creates the sugar for the berries.
- **Culinary Use:** When raw, the berries taste of smoke and citrus with metallic overtones. Cooked into a jam (a specialty of **Kaelen’s**).
- **Danger:** The thorns are coated in a mild irritant that causes a sensation of intense heat, often tricking a harvester into thinking they’ve been burned by a stray ember.
- Cinder berries can be used to [[Cinder berry jam]]; [[Kaelen]] is working on a recipe and will reward the players if they help him harvest more berries.
    ![[cinder_berry.png|344]]

---
### 2. Needle Grass (_Agrostis Ferrum_)

**Category:** Ground Cover

- **Appearance:** This is not the soft green grass of the lowlands. Needle grass grows in tight, metallic-blue clumps that look more like wire wool than vegetation.
    
- **Physiology:** It is a "Hyper-Accumulator." It pulls iron and magnetite from the ash and stores it in its blades. This makes the grass incredibly tough and nearly fireproof and protects the root system underneath.
    
- **The Scavenger’s Curse:** If you fall onto a patch of Needle grass without thick leather trousers, the "blades" will leave microscopic metallic splinters in your skin that are notoriously difficult to remove.
    
- **Utility:** Needle grass can be used as a scouring pad for cleaning oxidized surfaces or sharpening dull blades and making hot yam boot polish.
	- Needle grass roots can be grown in food slurry such as ash yam mash or cinder berry juice as a hyper-accumulator to extract the metals.  Needles produced in the process can be separated through magnetic filtration.  [[Dimble]] has discovered this process and [[Millicent]] is desperately curious to find out how.  [[Kaelen]] would also like to use it for making a better cinder berry jam with less metallic overtones.
![[needle_grass.png|323]]

### 3. Vicegrip Bamboo (_Phyllostachys Vorax_)

**Category:** Carnivorous Bamboo

- **Appearance:** Tall, pale stalks that grow nearby hot vents. Unlike normal bamboo, the "nodes" of this plant are flexible, muscular sphincters.
- **The Trap:** The hollow stalks are filled with a sweet-smelling, highly pressurized pheromone gas. When a creature (like a **Dust Mephit** or a small rodent) brushes against the sensory hairs on the stalk, the plant "snaps" a joint closed, trapping the limb or body.  
	**Small prey digestion:** Once trapped, the plant slowly fills the chamber with a digestive acid. The "hissing" sound often heard in the bamboo groves is the sound of the plant venting excess gas during the digestion process.- Small prey digestion 
	**Large prey digestion** Large animals are also at risk if they blunder into a vicegrip patch.  The stalks flex forcefully to cling and pin the victim while spraying it with streams of digestive acid.  Those that escape (safety check on dexterity to dodge out of the way and failing that strength to break free) suffer `dice: 1d4`acid attack.  Continue strength saves until successful escape.  Companions can offer their help although they might also become victims.
-

### 4. Prismatic Willow (_Salix Prismatica_)

**Category:** Shade Tree

- **Appearance:** A massive, weeping tree that grows best in groves. Its long, drooping branches are covered in leaves that are literally made of thin reflective panes of volcanic mica.  The ground underneath the tree is covered and insulated by a layer of reflective leaves that help protect the tree.
- **The Canopy:** On a bright summer day, the Prismatic Willow provides the only safe shade in the wasteland. The glass leaves reflect 90% of the radiant heat and can create a a thermal pocket between the canopy and leaf litter layer that can become cool enough to produce condensation in the winter when air temperatures can be cooler at night.
- **The Danger:** The "Willow-Wind." When the wind picks up, the glass leaves clatter together, creating a beautiful chiming sound. However, the wind also causes tiny mica "dust" to shed from the leaves. Without a face mask, sitting under a Willow can lead to shortness of breath, chronic lung damage and even suffocation.  
    ![[prismatic_willow.png|347]]
