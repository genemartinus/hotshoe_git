The "interrupted decay process" is a brilliant mechanical hook. If things don't rot, the Dwarven Cemetery (where Jube and Kaelen hang out) becomes a much weirder, more preserved place than a typical graveyard.

Ash as a global problem.
 * The Ash Property: If the ash prevents decay, it might also act as a Preservative or a Conductive Layer for the thermal cyborgs.
 * Gameplay Tip: Perhaps the players need to "clean" their gear at The Thirsty Pebble or have Millicent craft a specific solvent, or they risk their carbon-based gear becoming "Thermal-Locked."
2. The Quest Givers (Your "Contact List")
Your NPCs are perfectly tiered for Level 1 players:
 * The Social Hub: Dimble Pingun is the perfect "First Contact." His quest for the Fermenter’s Stone and his "soft info" makes the tavern the natural starting point.
 * The Professional Hook: Stefania (Warden) and Obur (Day Watch) provide the "Official" quests—likely clearing out the emerging thermal-based pests from the hamlet’s perimeter.
 * The Moral Gray Zone: Orion, Durn, and Kip (The Black Gold Team) represent the "High Risk, High Reward" side of Mt. Conniption. They want the players to help them scavenge in the Grey Wash.
 * The Mystery: Kaelen (Druid of Nerull) and Millicent (Apothecary) deal with the "unusual properties" of the ash. They are the ones who will ask the players to investigate why the decay process has stopped.
3. Mechanical Synergy (The "Hotshoe" Combat Meta)
I’ve noticed a consistent theme in your stat blocks: Fragility vs. High Output.
 * Most of your NPCs (Kip, Glour, Phanzis, Dimble) have under 10 HP.
 * This teaches players early on that in Hotshoe, positioning is everything. If the players protect the NPCs, the NPCs provide massive support (like Orion’s 95 damage or Jube’s Disciple of Life healing). If the players let the NPCs get hit, they die instantly.

🌋 Welcome to Hotshoe: A Traveler’s Prelude
Perched precariously on the ashen shoulders of Mt. Conniption, the hamlet of Hotshoe is not your typical frontier outpost. Here, the air doesn't just carry the scent of pine; it carries the weight of a world rewriting its own biology. You have arrived at the edge of the Kantzai Frontier, where the rules of life—and death—are shifting under the heat.
The Thermal Shift
In Hotshoe, "flesh and blood" is an aging concept. You will encounter Thermal-based life forms: creatures that breathe steam, bleed molten glass, and hunt for heat rather than hunger. Some are eerie cyborgs—ancient carbon-based functions augmented by the volcano’s raw power. To survive here, you must learn to watch the temperature as closely as you watch your back.
The Ash that Remembers
Something is amiss beyond the heat. The constant ash fall from the peak has begun to exhibit strange, preservative properties. In the local cemeteries and wasteland thickets, the usual process of decay has stalled. Here, what falls stays—and sometimes, it finds a way to stand back up.
Life in the "Thirsty" Hamlet
Despite the hazards, the hospitality of Hotshoe remains (mostly) warm.
 * The Social Heart: Share a mug of "Pebble Ale" with Dimble Pingun at The Thirsty Pebble, but don't ask too many questions about why he never buys hops.
 * The Law: Look to Warden Stefania or the watchmen Obur and Glour to keep the perimeter—though they can’t be everywhere at once.
 * The Opportunists: You might find yourself "on the job" with the Black Gold scavenging crew, diving into the Grey Wash for treasures that shouldn't exist.
A Warning to the Green
As a newcomer, remember: Positioning is survival. The threats of Mt. Conniption—from the blinding Ash-Beak Phoenix to the heat-drinking L’zoir—are fragile but lethal. They don't fight fair, and the environment won't help you.
Things are heating up on the mountain. Whether you’re here to solve the mystery of the "Eternal Ash" or simply to make your fortune in the mines, one thing is certain: Hotshoe never leaves a traveler unchanged.

From the vantage point of the Cemetery Trail, the Hotshoe Valley unfolds as a study in violent geological contrast, where the "Whiteout" glare of the eruption zone meets the dark, industrious heart of the settlement. At this elevation, the thin air is saturated with the "chiming" sound of the Prismatic Willow grove and the heavy, metallic scent of the Cinder-Berry bushes.
The Eruption Zone and the Emerald Drift
To the north, the valley floor is dominated by the Emerald Drift, a jagged expanse of black obsidian veined with translucent green glass.
 * The Jade-Glint Vents: Intermittent plumes of toxic green gas rise from the drift, marking the locations of active "Jade-Glint" vents.
 * The Obsidian Mines: The dark, gaping maws of the obsidian mines are visible along the valley walls, where Lesser Jade-Husks—miners overtaken by crystalline growth—are known to wander the shallower shafts.
 * Needle Grass Meadows: Surrounding the drift are vast, shimmering meadows of Needle Grass, their iron-rich blades glinting like a field of static electricity.
The Hamlet of Hotshoe
Below the eruption zone lies the living heart of the valley, a cluster of low-slung buildings designed to survive the mountain's "indigestion."
 * The Kick Ash Market: From this height, the colorful stalls of the market appear as a small, bustling hub of trade for Iron-Sow leather and Ash Yam provisions.
 * The Soft-Grave Perimeter: On the outskirts of the hamlet, the Secondary Cemetery (the "Soft-Grave") is visible as a patch of darker, misted soil where Kaelen maintains his secret fungal harvests beneath the silver-grey leaves of the Cinder-Berry bushes.
 * The Thirsty Pebble: The central tavern stands out with its heavy, salt-crusted masonry, serving as the primary social anchor for the valley's residents.
The Atmospheric Horizon
The sky over the valley is rarely clear; instead, it is filled with a shifting haze of Mica Dust and volcanic ash.
 * The Heat Haze: Daytime visibility is constantly distorted by rising thermal currents, making the distant structures of the valley shimmer and dance.
 * The Shadowfell Influence: To the far edge of the valley, the air takes on a "colder" quality, hinting at the hidden domains of dread and ancient shrines to Nerull that the "Star Reapers" believe are necessary to unite the planes.
