## Hara.  Gnome grenade

Scaling **Hara** down to Level 1 makes her the party’s "Unstoppable Anchor." As a Deep Gnome (Svirfneblin), she is naturally suited for the rocky, ash-strewn tunnels of **Mt. Conniption**. While she loses her multiattack and advanced shield maneuvers, she remains a powerhouse of physical defense.

**Second Wind**. On their turn, they can use a Bonus Action to heal themselves and still use their Action to attack with a sword or war pick.

## **Combat Strategy: The Gnome Grenade**

Hara is designed to be the "Anvil." She stands in the gap, soaks up damage, and ensures the squishier members of the party (like **Emerick** or **Assunta**) stay standing.

## **The "Human Shield" (Interception)**

This is Hara’s most critical Level 1 skill.

- **The Move:** Stand within 5 feet of an ally (preferably **Emerick** or **Assunta**).
    
- **The Logic:** If they get hit, use your **Reaction** to reduce the damage. At Level 1, reducing damage by $1d10+2$ often means an ally takes **zero** damage from a hit that would have otherwise knocked them out.
    

## **Stone Camouflage**

Even though Hara wears heavy armor and has Stealth disadvantage, her Deep Gnome heritage gives her **Advantage** in rocky terrain.

- **The Move:** When in caves or mountain passes, use a normal roll for Stealth (Advantage and Disadvantage cancel out).
    
- **The Logic:** This allows Hara to actually participate in ambushes rather than clanking loudly and giving the party away.
    

## **War Pick Grind**

Hara has a solid +2 Strength. She is reliable.

- **The Move:** Engage the toughest enemy in melee. With 16 AC, she is very hard to hit for CR 1/4 creatures.
    
- **The Logic:** By keeping the "boss" occupied, she frees up **Brevil** to get around for a Sneak Attack or **Harsent** to use his dual-wielding.
    

## **Tactical Advice: Second Wind Management

- **The Strategy:** Use **Second Wind** as soon as you drop below 6 HP. Because you are the party's primary tank, if you go down, the "backline" will follow quickly. You are the only thing keeping the party from being overrun.

#### Combat strategy
- Hara is built as a **Tank/Defender**. While her Hit Points ($13$) are a bit lean for a front-liner, her abilities allow her to mitigate damage for herself and her allies.
- **The "Shield Wall" Strategy**
	- This is Hara’s most powerful defensive tool. In D&D 5e, "Resistance" (halving damage) is usually reserved for Barbarians, but Hara can trigger it as an action.
	- **When to use it:** Use this when you are surrounded or facing a "boss" enemy that hits hard.
	- **The Bonus:** Since you still get to make a **Shield Bash** while using Shield Wall, you aren't sacrificing your entire turn just to stay alive.    
- **Intercept: The Ultimate Bodyguard**
	- Your **Intercept** reaction is incredibly strong. Most Fighters use their reaction for an "Attack of Opportunity," but you can literally swat an enemy's weapon out of the air.
	- **Pro Tip:** Stay adjacent to your party's "squishy" members (like a Wizard or Sorcerer). If an enemy tries to hit them, use Intercept to cancel the attack entirely.
- **The Multiattack Combo**
	- Hara's offensive routine is very consistent. On a standard turn, you should be looking to use your **War pick** followed by a **Shield Bash**.
	- **Average Damage:** If both hit, you’re dealing roughly $11$ ($6+5$) damage per turn, which is excellent for a Challenge 1 character.

Hara is the "Grit" that holds the party together.  Hara is the one standing in the literal fire, shield raised, taking the brunt of the enemy's attention. As a Svirfneblin, she has a natural affinity for the deep earth.  Here is how "The Gnome Grenade" anchors the party in the face of adversity.

The Svirfneblin Edge: Stone Camouflage
Hara’s Stone Camouflage is a massive tactical advantage here.
 * The "Living Boulder": In the Dwarven Cemetery or the Jade-Glint drifts, Hara can remain perfectly still among the basalt rubble. Predators likely mistake her for a simple outcropping of rock until she erupts with a Shield Bash.
 * Darkvision (120 ft): In darknss where visibility is a death sentence, Hara sees twice as far as others. She is the party's primary nighttime scout.

The "Shield Wall" vs. Elemental Hazards
Hara’s Shield Wall is a lifesaver in an environment full of environmental damage.
 * Hara can use her reaction/action to halve incoming damage. She can effectively "muffle" an explosion with her shield, protecting the fragile players behind her.
 * The "Multiattack" Pressure: At Level 1, having a War Pick and a Shield Bash (via Multiattack or Action Surge) makes her a whirlwind of kinetic energy. She can crack an enemy with the pick and then shove it away with her shield.

⚔️ Combat Strategy: 
Hara is an "Anvil" to support teammates' strikes:
 * The Hold: Hara moves to confront the enemy using Shield Wall to become an immovable object.
 * The Surge: When the enemies are bunched up, she uses Action Surge to Shield Bash a priority target prone, giving others Advantage for their Sneak Attacks.
 * The Animal Handling: Hara's Animal Handling (+3) might allow her to "wrangle" a creature so others can dispense with it.
