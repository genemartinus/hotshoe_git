### Bonus action:
Looking toward this:  At Level 2 Brevil will get **Cunning Action**, allowing her to Dash, Disengage, or Hide as a Bonus Action every single turn.

### **Combat Strategy: The Underworld Enforcer**

Brevil doesn't fight fair. She uses her unusual Strength to manipulate the battlefield so her allies can land their big hits.

#### **The "Grapple & Shank" (Strength Utility)**
Brevil has a solid Strength score and **Athletics Expertise**.
- **The Move:** Use your action to **Grapple** a target (Athletics +4). Once they are grappled, their speed is 0.
- **The Logic:** This pins an enemy in place so allies within reach can attack and the target cannot fight back until they break free.

#### **The "Dirty Opener" (Surprise Rounds)**
Brevil is the spirit of an assassin, even without the "Assassinate" trait yet.
- **The Move:** Use **Stealth** to get close. If you have advantage (from hiding), your Shortsword attack triggers **Sneak Attack**.
- **The Logic:**  a $1d6 + 2$ (sword) + $1d6$ (sneak attack) + potential poison is a massive hit.    

#### **The "Poison harasser" (Blowgun Utility)**
Brevil uses her blowgun to "soften" targets.
- **The Move:** If the frontline is too dangerous, stay back and use the Blowgun.
- **The Logic:** Even though the damage is low, forcing a **Constitution Save** every turn can wear down an enemy’s resolve or break a caster's concentration.

#### **Tactical Advice:
- **The Strategy:** Stay near an ally. If they are engaged with an enemy, you get your **Sneak Attack** automatically without needing advantage.

#### Combat strategies

- ***The Ambush***
	- With her **Assassinate** trait and **Stealth +4**, Brevil is deadliest in the first few seconds of a fight.
	- **The Play:** Use the area features to **Hide** (Cunning Action). Wait for a target to get close, then strike with the **Blowgun** from the shadows.
	- **The Math:** Because of Assassinate, if she goes before her target, she has Advantage. If she hits a surprised target, it’s a **Critical Hit**, doubling her weapon dice, poison dice, and sneak attack dice for a massive opening burst.
- ***The Strength Shove (Cunning Grappler)***
	- Because her **STR (15)** is much higher than her **DEX (12)**, she can win most Athletics contests against other Humanoids.
	- **The Play:** Use an attack to **Shove** an enemy prone. Since they are now prone, Brevil gains **Advantage** on her Shortsword attacks within 5 feet.
	- **The Math:** Advantage automatically triggers her **Sneak Attack** (that extra 2d6+3), even if she doesn't have an ally nearby. This is the most reliable way for a Strength-based Rogue to deal high damage solo.
- ***Tactical "Hit and Run"***
	- Brevil has low AC (13) and relatively low HP (16). She cannot afford to stand in the middle of a group of adventurers.
	- **The Play:** 1. Move in and strike with the **Shortsword** (adding that +2 Strength to the hit). 2. Use her **Bonus Action** for **Cunning Action: Disengage**. 3. Move her full 30 ft. speed away, preferably behind one of those stone pillars or ruined walls in the garden to gain **Total Cover**.
	- **Why it works:** It forces enemies to use their full movement and their Action just to get to her, preventing them from attacking her effectively.
- ***Poison Kiting***
	- The **1d10 poison** on her shortsword is her best "crowd control" tool.
	- **The Play:** If an enemy looks like a "Tank" (high HP, low mobility), hit them with the shortsword and then run.
	- **The Math:** If the poison lingers or if you're using homebrew "Poisoned" condition rules, it weakens them significantly. Use the **Blowgun** to finish them off from 25 feet away while staying out of their reach.

Brevil’s Shortsword already deals poison damage, suggesting she has a supply of toxins. The nature and specificity of those toxins are unknown and therefore limited.  She needs a better arsenal to combat a variety of enemy resistances.  Maybe services of an alchemist.

Her poison laced Blowgun is her most tactical tool.
 * Brevil is the only party member who can safely neutralize opponents from 25 feet away without igniting combustibles in the vicinity.

Brevil’s Tactical Advantage
 * Assassinate: If she surprises a Husk, her critical hit with a blunt instrument (like a sap or the pommel of her sword) can "Stun" the elemental-undead long enough to be wrapped in a Soot-Damp Rag.
 * Bane: Casting Bane on the enemy makes it much harder for them to hit her as she disengages.

Gets an edge using these items

- Needle Grass Mask protects from noxious gasses.
