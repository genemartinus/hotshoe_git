
At Level 1, **Reactions** are often the most overlooked part of the action economy. A Reaction allows a character to respond to a specific trigger—even when it isn't their turn.

In the **Hotshoe** setting, these reactions are the difference between a character being "Glass" or a "Cannon."

## **Strategic Breakdown for the Players**

- **The "Wall" Duo (Hara & Vem):** These two should always stand next to each other or a squishy ally. **Hara** reduces the damage if a hit connects, while **Vem** makes it harder for the hit to connect in the first place. You only get **one** reaction per round, so coordinate who "defends" whom!
    
- **The "Reach" Advantage (Assunta):** Because your whip has a 10-foot reach, an enemy triggers an Opportunity Attack when they move from 10 feet to 15 feet away. This is great for stopping enemies from reaching **Emerick**.
    
- **The "Emergency" Button (Emerick):** Since you only have 6 HP, your Reaction is almost always reserved for the _Shield_ spell. Do not use your reaction for an Opportunity Attack (punching someone) unless you are 100% sure you won't be attacked later that round.
    
- **The "Grappler" (Brevil):** At Level 1, Brevil's best "reaction" is actually preventing the enemy from moving at all. By swapping her Opportunity Attack for a Grapple, she keeps the enemy pinned in the **Jade-Glint** or **Ash-Cloud** hazards.
    

## **Important Reaction Rules:**

1. **Once per Round:** Once you use your reaction, you cannot use another one until the start of your next turn.
    
2. **Reset:** You "get your reaction back" at the beginning of your own turn.
    
3. **Proximity:** Most reactions (except Emerick's _Shield_) require you to be able to see the target or be within a specific distance of the event.
