## **Assunta (Level 1 Rogue)**

_Assunta’s "Backflip" and low health make her perfect as a Level 1 Rogue, using Cunning Action and Sneak Attack to stay relevant without needing a high AC._

- **AC:** 12 (Leather)
- **HP:** 8 ($1d8 + 0$)
- **Speed:** 35 ft.

| **STR** | **DEX** | **CON** | **INT** | **WIS** | **CHA** |
| ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- |
| 8 (-1)  | 14 (+2) | 10 (+0) | 12 (+1) | 14 (+2) | 12(+1)  |
- **Skills:** Acrobatics +4, Investigation +3, Insight +4, Stealth +4.
- **Saving Throws:** DEX +4, INT +3.
- **Damage Resistances** Psychic
- **Condition Immunities** Sleep
- **Senses** Passive Perception 12
- **Languages:** Common, Deep speech
 
#### Abilities:

- **Sneak Attack:** Once per turn, deal an extra $1d6$ damage to a creature you hit with an advantage or if an ally is within 5 ft of it.
- **Expertise (Acrobatics/Insight):** Doubled proficiency in these skills.
        
#### **Actions:**
- **Whip:** +4 to hit, reach 10 ft. **Hit:** 4 ($1d4 + 2$) slashing.
- **Dagger.** +2 to hit, reach 5 ft or range 20/60 ft, one target. _Hit:_ 3 (1d4 + 1) piercing damage.
- **Sling:** +4 to hit, range 30/120. **Hit:** 4 ($1d4 + 2$) bludgeoning.

#### Reaction:
**Opportunity Attack**An enemy moves out of your 10ft reach (Whip). (without disengaging) Make one melee attack with your Whip (+4 to hit).

#### Combat strategy

**Role:** Battlefield Disruptor / Sneak Attacker 
Assunta has low HP (8) and has to rely on "finesse" to play.  She relies entirely on **Sneak Attack** to be effective in combat.
- **The "Whip & Ally" Combo:** You only get **Sneak Attack** if an ally (on your side) is within 5 feet of your target. Use the **10-foot reach** of your whip to strike the enemy your ally is fighting.
- **The "Safety Gap":** By standing 10 feet away, you are out of the enemy's standard melee reach. Hit them with the whip, then use your 35-foot movement to back away even further.
- **Acrobatic Defense:** If you are forced into a tight spot, use your **Acrobatics Expertise (+4)** to "Disengage" or "Hide" as your main action (until you get Cunning Action at Level 2).
- **Tactical Advice:** Use your **Investigation (+3)** during combat to look for environmental hazards—like a loose pile of volcanic ash or a steaming vent—that you can use to your advantage.

Assunta is a fascinating "support-skirmisher." With 13 HP and an AC of 11, she is the definition of a glass cannon—or rather, a "glass disruptor." She isn't designed to trade blows; she is designed to waste the enemy's actions while staying just out of reach.

Her strategy centers on **Action Economy** and **Battlefield Control.**

---

**The "Whip & Trip" (Range Management)**
Assunta’s **Whip** has a 10-foot reach. This is her most important tool because it allows her to hit enemies without entering their 5-foot "threat zone."
- **The Move:** Stay exactly 10 feet away and strike with the Whip and then the Dagger (if the enemy closed in) or the Sling (if they are further out).
- **The Logic:** By staying at 10 feet, she forces melee enemies to use their movement to reach her, triggering potential reactions from her allies.

**The "Backflip" Bait (Defensive Mastery)**
Assunta’s **Backflip** reaction is her life insurance useing it to **nullify** the enemy's entire turn.
- **The Move:** Position Assunta near a heavy hitter. When they attack, use the **Backflip** reaction.
- **The Logic:** Since her Acrobatics is $+2$, it’s a gamble, but if she wins the contest, the attack misses entirely. Because she has **35 ft. speed**, she can then move to a safer spot on her next turn.

**The "Nova" Surge (Action Surge)**
Assunta is one of the few CR 1 creatures with **Action Surge**. This allows her to double her output in a single critical moment.
- **The Move:** Use Action Surge to perform two attacks in one turn.
- **The Logic:** While her damage is low ($1d4 + 1$), hitting two times in one turn forces two separate concentration checks on an enemy spellcaster or finishes off a weakened foe.
    
**The "Second Wind" Reset**
With only 13 HP, a single lucky hit can down her.
- **The Move:** Do not wait until you are at 1 HP. If Assunta takes _any_ damage (even 4 or 5 points), use **Second Wind** as a bonus action.
- **The Logic:** Regaining **$1d10 + 3$** health will likely put her back at full HP instantly. This makes her "effective health" much higher than it appears on paper.
    

---

**The "Deep Speech" Psychological Edge**
Since Assunta knows **Deep Speech** and has **Psychic Resistance**, she is mentally hardened. In combat, she should be played as a distracting presence—shouting jarring, alien commands in Deep Speech to unnervingly calm her enemies while she flips and lashes out with her whip.
