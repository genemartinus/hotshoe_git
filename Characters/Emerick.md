# Emerick. Level 1 Sorcerer.  Mentalist


Emerick steps up as a "Telepathic Controller" and can wield his thoughts with **+4 Charisma**  that makes it incredibly difficult for enemies to resist.

### **Combat Strategy: The Psychic Scalpel**

Emerick should be played as a "force multiplier." He doesn't just deal damage; he sets up the rest of the party to succeed by breaking enemy defenses.

#### **The "Mind Sliver" Setup (Essential Combo)**
This is Emerick’s most important move. _Mind Sliver_ deals $1d6$ psychic damage and forces the target to subtract $1d4$ from their next saving throw.
- **The Move:** Target an enemy with _Mind Sliver_.
- **The Logic:** On your next turn (or an ally's turn), that enemy will likely fail their save against a bigger spell like _Dissonant Whispers_ or an effect from **Thomasine** or **Assunta**. You are essentially "softening" their brain.
    
#### **The "Dissonant Whispers" Peel**
Because Emerick has very low HP, he needs to stay away from melee.
- **The Move:** If an enemy gets close to you or  an ally cast _Dissonant Whispers_.
- **The Logic:** On a failed save, the enemy takes psychic damage and **must immediately use its reaction to run away**. This prevents them from attacking you and potentially allows **Harsent** or **Brevil** to take an Opportunity Attack as the enemy flees.
    
#### **The "Shield" Panic Button**
Emerick’s AC is only 12. He will get hit.
- **The Move:** Keep one of your two spell slots specifically for the **Shield** reaction.
- **The Logic:** Boosting your AC to 17 for a full round is the only way you survive a Level 1 ambush. If you use both slots on offensive spells, you are defenseless.
    
#### **Tactical Advice: Social Combat**
With a **+6 to Intimidation and Persuasion**, Emerick should be the "Face" of the party.
- **The Strategy:** Use _Minor Illusion_ or _Telepathy_ to create distractions or plant thoughts in guards' heads before a fight even starts. You can often "win" an encounter before a single rapier is drawn.

### **Combat Strategy**

- Emerick relies on positioning and reaction-based defense to survive.
	- **The "One-Two Punch":** Using **Mind Sliver** as an action is great, but since he has **Quickened Spell**, he can cast a powerful save-based spell like _Dissonant Whispers_ or _Arms of Hadar_ as a bonus action. The target would then have to subtract $1d4$ from their saving throw, making his spells much harder to resist.
	- **Safety First:** With **Misty Step** as a bonus action and **Shield** as a reaction, Emerick is slippery. If an enemy gets too close, he can blink away; if they swing at him, he can bump his AC to a respectable 17 instantly.
	- **Crowd Control:** _Calm Emotions_ and _Dissonant Whispers_ are excellent for peeling enemies off allies or stopping a fight before it starts.

**The Telepathic Advantage**
Emerick’s Telepathy (30 ft) and Deep Speech proficiency are vital here.
 * Emerick can "touch" the minds of enemies and anticipate what will come next..

The "Mind Sliver" vs. Other Resistances
Emerick’s Psychic Damage bypasses almost most other damage resistances.
 * The Shield/Misty Step Loop:  Emerick is the only one who can reposition without triggering traps. He can "blink" past the hazards that would shred everyone else's armor.

Combat Strategy: The "Ash-Cloud" Peel
Emerick is the party's best "Safety Valve" when things go wrong in the Emerald Drift:
 * The Peel: If Brevil gets surrounded by Jade-Husks, Emerick uses Dissonant Whispers. The Husks are forced to run away, triggering Opportunity Attacks from the rest of the party while keeping Emerick safely at range.
 * The "Mind-Sliver" Debuff: By hitting a Scald-Scale Serpent with Mind Sliver, he lowers its saves, making it almost certain to fail against Brevil’s poisons or his own Arms of Hadar.

📉 **Emerick's weakness**
 * The Concentration Trap:  In an environment that requires constant Constitution Saving Throws against hardships, he will likely lose his spells every time he fails a saving throw.
