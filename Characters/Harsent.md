## **Harsent (Level 1 Fighter)**

_To scale Harsent, we reduce his massive +3 Constitution and slightly lower his Dexterity to fit a standard "starting array" (Point Buy/Standard Array)._

- **Medium Humanoid (male Human), Chaotic Neutral**
- **AC:** 14 (Padded)
- **HP:** 12 ($1d10 + 2$)
- **Speed:** 30 ft.

| **STR** | **DEX** | **CON** | **INT** | **WIS** | **CHA** |
| ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- |
| 10 (+0) | 16 (+3) | 14 (+2) | 9 (-1)  | 9 (-1)  | 9(-1)   |

- **Skills:** Acrobatics +5, Intimidation +1.
- **Saving Throws:** STR +2, CON +4.
    
- **Abilities:**
    
    - **Second Wind:** (1/Short Rest) Regain $1d10 + 1$ HP.
        
    - **Fighting Style (Two-Weapon Fighting):** When you engage in two-weapon fighting, you can add your ability modifier to the damage of the second attack.

- **Actions:**
    
    - **Scimitar:** +5 to hit, reach 5 ft. **Hit:** 6 ($1d6 + 3$) slashing.
    - **Shortsword (Off-hand):** +5 to hit, reach 5 ft. **Hit:** 6 ($1d6 + 3$) piercing.

#### Bonus action:
**Second Wind**. On their turn, they can use a Bonus Action to heal themselves and still use their Action to attack with a sword or war pick.  
**Harsent** attacks with a Scimitar in his main hand (Action), the rules allow him to use a **Bonus Action** to attack with a Shortsword in his off-hand. This is why dual-wielders feel so busy at Level 1!

#### Combat strategy

**Role:** Frontline Striker / High-Damage "Nova"

Harsent is your primary damage dealer. With a $+5$ to hit and two weapons, he can potentially put down a standard CR 1/4 goblin in a single turn.
- **The "Dual-Wield" Loop:** Always use your **Bonus Action** to make an off-hand attack with your shortsword. Since you have the **Two-Weapon Fighting Style**, you add your full $+3$ DEX to that second hit.
- **The "Second Wind" Safety Net:** You only have 12 HP. Do not wait until you are at 1 HP to use **Second Wind**. If you take 5 or more damage from a single hit, use your Bonus Action to heal immediately. It’s better to "waste" a few points of healing than to be knocked unconscious before your next turn.
- **Tactical Advice:** You have **Stealth Disadvantage** from your padded armor. Let others scout ahead; you should be the second person through the door, ready to engage the biggest threat.

level 3 Combat strategy

To maximize Harsent’s high Dexterity ($17$) and Constitution ($16$), his combat style should be a "Skirmisher’s Waltz." He is built to be a high-mobility harasser who is difficult to pin down but hits remarkably hard for a CR 1 creature.

**The "Kiting" Opener (The Blowgun)**
Harsent should never start in melee. With a speed of **35 ft.** and a Blowgun, he is faster than most standard humanoids.
- **The Move:** Stay at the 25-foot range. Fire the Blowgun as your main action.
- **The Logic:** You are targeting the enemy's concentration or squishy backline. Since Harsent has **Stealth Disadvantage** from his padded armor, don't bother hiding; just stay out of reach.

**The "Aggressive Mid-Game" (Rapier + Scimitar)**
Once the frontline is engaged, Harsent should move in and attack with Rapier or Scimitar strike in one action.
- **The Move:** Use your **Action Surge** early. In a single turn, Harsent can potentially make **two melee attacks** (Action: Rapier or Scimitar + Action Surge: Rapier or Scimitar).
- **The Logic:** With a $+5$ to hit, he is very likely to land these and dismiss a lower-level enemy quickly.

**Mastering the "Backflip" Defense**
Harsent’s **Backflip** reaction is his most powerful defensive tool, especially since he has **3 charges** per short rest.
- **The Strategy:** Do not use Backflip on the first weak attack that comes your way. Save it for the "heavy hitters" or when an enemy rolls a natural 15-18.
- **The Combo:** Use **Cambering Strike** as a bonus action, rafter using a Backflip grant you **Advantage** on your next scimitar swing. This creates a loop: _Defend acrobatically $\rightarrow$ Counter-attack with precision._

**Survival & Reset (Second Wind)**
Because Harsent has a massive $+3$ Constitution modifier, he is surprisingly well built for a skirmisher.
- **The Move:** If Harsent drops below 10 HP, use **Second Wind** as a bonus action immediately.
- **The Logic:** This regains an average of **8-9 HP**, which is nearly 40% of his total health. Use this _before_ you are forced to retreat, keeping you in the "pocket" of the fight longer.
