#### **Special Traits**
_**Nature Affinity.**_ Thomasine's experience gained living in the wilderness has given her the following abilities:  
- She can spend 1 minute to sense whether one of the following types of creatures are present within 2 miles of her: aberrations, celestials, dragons, elementals, fey, fiends and undead. This feature doesn't reveal the creatures' location or number.
- **Stone-Sleepless.** Thomasine does not require sleep and cannot be forced to sleep by magical means. To gain the benefits of a long rest, she must spend at least 4 hours standing perfectly still in contact with stone or earth.
- **Vulnerability Seeker.** Thomasine adds her Proficiency Bonus to damage rolls against creatures she has tracked for more than one hour (already included in her actions).
#### **Combat & Actions**
- _**Rapier.**_ _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +4 to hit, reach 5 ft, one target. _Hit:_  `dice: 1d12+2` piercing damage.
- _**Blowgun.**_ _Ranged Weapon Attack:_ +5 to hit, range 25/100 ft, one target. _Hit:_ `dice: 1d6+4` piercing damage.
#### **Spellcasting**.
- Thomasine casts one of the following spells, using Wisdom as the spellcasting ability (spell save DC 12, +4 to hit with spell attacks):
- 3/day each: _alarm_
#### **Bonus actions**
- _**Ranger Tricks.**_ Thomasine casts _Hunter's Mark_ or _Hail of Thorns_ as if using a spell slot of 1st level.
#### **Reactions**
- _**Horde Breaker.**_ Once on each of her turns when Thomasine makes a ranged attack, she can make another attack with the same weapon against a different creature that is within 5 feet of the original target and within range of her weapon.
#### **Roleplaying Notes**
Thomasine should be played as **cold and efficient.** She doesn't taunt; she breathes rhythmically and waits for the perfect shot. Because of her **Stone-Sleepless** trait, she often sets up her "blind" (her sniping position) hours in advance, standing perfectly still like a statue until the target appears.
#### **Specific storyline**
Thomasine was once a member of the _Deepgate Watch_, a specialized unit tasked with guarding the thin veil between the Material Plane and the Elemental Plane of Earth. During a catastrophic cave-in, she was trapped in a pocket of "Living Stone" for three years. She didn't age, she didn't eat, and she lost the ability to sleep. Now, she wanders the surface world as a freelance "Exterminator of the Unnatural," treating her hunts with the cold, mechanical precision of a clockmaker.
#### **Logical and moral implications**
- **The Burden of Sensation:** Because she cannot sleep, she is hyper-aware. She views the "dreaming world" as vulnerable and slightly pathetic. She is Lawful Neutral because she prioritizes the _Order of the Natural World_ over individual lives.
- **Mercy is Math:** She will not hesitate to sacrifice a civilian if it ensures the destruction of an aberration that could kill hundreds. To her, life is a ledger that must remain balanced.
- **Quietude:** She finds the sounds of cities physically painful and prefers the rhythmic "hum" of the earth.

#### Combat strategy

**Role:** Long-Range Sniper / Scout

Thomasine is the ultimate "early warning system." Her job is to ensure the party is never surprised by the mountain's extra-planar threats.
- **The "Favored Enemy" Edge:** Before entering any cave or ruined mine, ask the DM to use your **Favored Enemy** trait to look for tracks or signs of Elementals or Aberrations. Knowledge is your best weapon.
- **The High-Ground Sniper:** You have 30 feet of movement and a 100-foot range on your Blowgun. Use your movement to climb a rock or find cover _every single turn_.
- **The "Rapier Pivot":** If a fast enemy reaches you, don't panic. Your Rapier deals $1d8+3$ damage—at Level 1, that is enough to stop most attackers in their tracks. Use it to fend them off, then move back to range.
- **Tactical Advice:** You are the most "stable" character. Stay 20 feet behind Harsent and focus your fire on enemy casters or archers.

Thomasine has a very specific niche: **Extra-Planar Execution.** She isn't a tank and cannot afford to fight fair.  Her strategy centers on her massive $+7$ damage modifier and her ability to detect and mark targets from a distance.

**Defensive prelude**
Thomasine’s greatest strength is her **Nature Affinity**. She should never be surprised by an elemental or undead.
- **The Move:** Spend 1 minute before entering any dungeon or clearing to sense the presence of her favored enemies within 2 miles.
- **The Logic:** If she senses a target, she spends the next hour tracking it. This triggers her **Vulnerability Seeker** trait, adding her Proficiency Bonus ($+2$) to her flat damage, bringing her rapier/blowgun damage to that terrifying **+7**.

**The "Hidden Needle" (Blowgun & Hunter's Mark)**
Thomasine is a ranged specialist first. Her **Blowgun** has a $+7$ to hit, which is incredibly high for her level.
- **The Move:** Start the encounter at maximum range (up to 100 ft).
- **Action:** Fire the Blowgun ($1d4 + 7$ damage).
- **Bonus Action:** Cast _Hunter’s Mark_ (+ $1d6$ damage).
- **The Logic:** On a hit, she deals an average of **13 damage** from 100 feet away. For most Level 1-2 players, this is nearly an instant knockdown.

**The "Crowd Control" Burst (Hail of Thorns)**
Apply her bonus actions to combat a group of enemies. 
- **The Move:** Use **Ranger Tricks** to cast _Hail of Thorns_ as a Bonus Action, then fire the Blowgun.
- **The Logic:** This turns her single-target needle into an AOE (Area of Effect) burst. If the enemies are standing within 5 feet of each other, she uses her **Horde Breaker** reaction to immediately fire a _second_ blowgun shot at a different target.
    
**The "Desperation Parry" (Rapier & Speed)**
If an enemy closes the gap, Thomasine uses her high Dexterity ($16$) to punish them before retreating.
- **The Move:** Use the **Rapier** ($1d8 + 7$ damage).
- **The Logic:** She hits harder with the rapier than most fighters do with a greatsword. Because she has a **Passive Perception of 16**, she is difficult to flank.
- **The Escape:** After a strike, she should use her movement to get behind cover or up a cliffside. She is a survivalist; she does not stand her ground—she resets the encounter.
