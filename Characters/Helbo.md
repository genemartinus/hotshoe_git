# Helbo Bluefoot.  Bard.  Secret weapon 

Scaling **Helbo Bluefoot** down to Level 1 makes her the party's essential "Field Medic and Lore-Keeper." As a Hill Dwarf with a focus on survival and stonework, she is perfectly suited for the treacherous, ash-covered slopes of **Mt. Conniption**.

At Level 1, she loses her advanced _Blindness/Deafness_ and _Silence_ spells, but her ability to bolster her allies' spirits remains her "Secret Weapon."

---

- **Rapier:** +5 to hit, reach 5 ft. **Hit:** 7 ($1d8 + 3$) piercing damage.

### Bonus action:
Casts **Healing Word**. This is a Bonus Action, meaning she can revive a downed teammate and _still_ use her main action to attack with her rapier or cast a Cantrip like _Message_.

## **Combat Strategy: The Secret Weapon**

Helbo doesn't just play music; she manages the "rhythm" of the battlefield. She is surprisingly dangerous with her rapier but most effective when she is keeping the party's momentum alive.

## **The "Safety Net" (Healing Word)**

This is Helbo's most important tactical tool at Level 1.
- **The Move:** Do not use _Healing Word_ just because an ally is slightly hurt. Save it for when someone (likely **Assunta** or **Emerick**) drops to 0 HP.
- **The Logic:** Since it is a **Bonus Action**, Helbo can cast it and still use her Action to attack with her Rapier or use the _Message_ cantrip to coordinate the team.
    

## **The Nature & History**

Helbo’s knowledge of the mountain is a combat asset.
- **The Move:** Before the party goes underground, use **Stonecunning** to identify structural weaknesses.
- **The Logic:** You might find a weakness to avoid danger or find a way to use as an advantage.
    

## **The "Thunderclap" Deterrent**
If Helbo is surrounded by small, weak enemies (like ash-rats or goblins), she has a loud solution.
- **The Move:** Use the _Thunderclap_ cantrip.
- **The Logic:** Every creature within 5 feet must make a Constitution save or take $1d6$ thunder damage. It’s a great way to clear space if the "backline" gets swamped.
    

## **Tactical Advice: Bardic Inspiration Timing**

- **The Strategy:** Give your **Bardic Inspiration** to allies before they make their big moves
- **The Logic:** At Level 1, a single missed attack can end a fight. Adding a $d6$ to Harsent's attack roll ensures his dual-wielding actually connects.

#### Combat strategy
- **The "Support Sniper" (Opening Moves)**
	- Because of her **+3 Dexterity** and **+5 Stealth**, Helbo should always try to start combat hidden or at a distance.
		- **Round 1:** Cast **Silence** or **Blindness/Deafness** (if the enemy looks like they have low Constitution). Even with a DC 10, these can shut down a mage or a boss early.
		- **Bonus Action:** Use **Bardic Inspiration** on the party’s heaviest hitter (the Fighter or Paladin) to ensure their first big swing connects.
- **The "Frontline Support"**
- Helbo is a "Middle-Weight" combatant who ensures no one stays down:
 * The Inspiration: She stays 15 feet behind a PC tank, giving  the "extra edge" needed to land a Shield Bash on the enemy
 * The Nature Check: Before the fight starts, she uses a bonus action to identify a monster's weakness and craft how to take the advantage.
 * The Survivalist: If the party gets lost, Helbo’s Survival (+4) is the only thing that will lead them back to know where they are safe.
- **Managing the Mid-Field**
	- Helbo is surprisingly sturdy for a Bard with **19 HP**. She should stay close enough to help, but never be the one taking the most hits.
		- **If an enemy gets close:** Don't just swing the rapier. Use **Thunderclap**. It forces a Constitution save ($DC 10$) on all creatures within 5 feet. It’s great for creating breathing room if she gets swarmed.
		- **The Reaction Trap:** Save your reaction for **Cutting Words**. If an enemy rolls just high enough to hit an ally, use Cutting Words to subtract $1d6$ from their attack roll. It’s her most effective way to "heal" by preventing damage before it happens.
- **The "Help from Nowhere" Strategy**
	- Since Helbo has **Unseen Servant** and **Mage Hand**, she can influence the map without moving.
		- **Tactical Interaction:** Use your _Unseen Servant_ or _Mage Hand_ to drop ball bearings, pour out oil flasks, or interact with the environment (closing doors, pulling levers) while you stay safely behind cover.
		- **Message:** Use your at-will _Message_ cantrip to coordinate with your frontline allies silently, telling them exactly when you’re about to drop a _Silence_ spell so they can move out of the zone.
- **Emergency Management**
	- When things go south, Helbo is a primary medic.
		- **Healing Word:** Since it's a **Bonus Action**, she can cast this to revive a downed ally and still use her Action to **Dash**, **Disengage**, or attack with her **Rapier** (+5 to hit).
		- **Animal Friendship:** In wilderness encounters, don't waste time fighting guard dogs or wolves. Cast this early to take a beast out of the fight entirely for 24 hours.
