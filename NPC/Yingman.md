# Yingman Pickhand, Hotshoe administrator and bard manipulator extraordinaire 

#### **Statistics**

**Personality Trait** Serene
**Armor Class** 15 (leather)
**Hit Points** 105 (14d8 + 42)
**Speed**25 ft

| **STR** | **DEX** | **CON** | **INT** | **WIS** | **CHA** |
| ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- |
| 15 (+2) | 18 (+4) | 16 (+3) | 20 (+5) | 8 (-1)  | 17 (+3) |
**Skills** Acrobatics +7, Arcana +8, Deception +9, Investigation +8, Performance +9, Persuasion +6
**Damage Resistances** poison
**Senses** Dark vision 60 ft, passive Perception 9
**Languages**Common, Dwarvish
**Challenge** 8 ( 3,900 XP)
**Proficiency Bonus**+3
#### **Special Traits**
- _**Recorder.**_ Yimgan uses his Recorder as a spellcasting focus for his bard spells.
- _**Dwarven Resilience.**_ Yimgan has advantage on saving throws against poison.
- _**Stonecunning.**_ Yimgan adds double his proficiency bonus to Intelligence (History) checks related to the origin of stonework.
- _**Bureaucrancy.**_ Yimgan can help allies or friends fill out documents or paperwork, and he always knows the right organization or person to ask when he wants paperwork to be dealt with as a priority.
#### **Combat & Actions**

- _**Dagger.** Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack:_ +7 to hit, reach 5 ft or range 20/60 ft, one target. _Hit:_ 6 (1d4 + 4) piercing damage plus 24 (7d6) psychic damage. Yimgan's attacks are magically toxic to a creature's mind.
- _**Song of Terror (1/Short or Long Rest).**_ Yimgan plays his Recorder to seed paranoia and fear into the minds of 3 creatures of his choice within 60 feet, who must succeed on a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw or be frightened of Yimgan or another creature of his choice for 1 round.
- _**Mantle of Whispers: Use Shadow.**_ Yimgan consumes the shadow of a dead humanoid acquired with his Mantle of Whispers: Capture Shadow, taking the form of the humanoid. While in disguise, he gains access to all information that the humanoid would freely share with a casual acquaintance, including general details of its background and personal life but not secrets. Another creature can see through this disguise by succeeding on a Wisdom (Insight) check contested by Yimgan's Charisma (Deception) check. Yimgan gains a +5 bonus to his check.

#### **Spellcasting**.

- Yimgan casts one of the following spells, using Charisma as the spellcasting ability (spell save DC 14, +6 to hit with spell attacks):
- At will: light, mage hand, message
- 4/day each: longstrider, silent image, unseen servant
- 3/day each: ice storm, locate creature, dimension door, feign death, speak with dead, detect thoughts, magic mouth
- 2/day each: wall of force, seeming, mass cure wounds
- 1/day each: magnificent mansion, programmed illusion
#### **Bonus actions**

Bardic Inspiration (3/Short or Long Rest). Through stirring words or playing his Recorder, Yimgan inspires a creature within 60 feet of him who can hear him. Once within the next 10 minutes, the creature can roll a d10 and add the number rolled to one ability check, attack roll, or saving throw it makes. 
#### **Reactions**

Mantle of Whispers: Capture Shadow (1/Short or Long Rest). When a humanoid dies within 30 feet of Yimgan, he can magically capture its shadow.

Shadow Lore (1/Short or Long Rest). When a humanoid within 60 feet of Yimgan attacks him or chooses him as the target of a spell, he can magically whisper a phrase that only that humanoid can hear. The humanoid must make a DC 14 Wisdom saving throw. On a failed saving throw, the humanoid interprets the whispers as a description of its most mortifying secret. The attack or spell automatically fails and the humanoid won't attack or do harm to Yimgan until the end of its next turn, for fear of him revealing its secret. When the effect ends, the humanoid has no understanding of why it held Yimgan in such fear.

#### **Reward/treasure/favor**

Based on Yingman Pickhand’s stat block, he isn’t a frontline bruiser—he is a high-INT "Bard Manipulator" who thrives on psychological warfare and positioning. With 105 HP and 15 AC, he is durable for a Bard, but his true power lies in that massive 7d6 psychic damage rider on his dagger.
Here is a combat strategy designed to make Yingman a terrifying, elusive encounter.
Combat Strategy: The Whispering Bureaucrat
1. The Opening Gambit: Psychological Lockdown
Yingman should never start a fight in his true form if he can help it. Use Mantle of Whispers to appear as a trusted ally or a harmless clerk.
 * Surprise Round: If Yingman is disguised, he should get close to the party's squishiest caster or concentration-user.
 * Action: Use Song of Terror immediately. Target the party’s heavy hitters (Fighter/Barbarian).
 * The Goal: Force the "Frightened" condition so they cannot move closer to him, effectively peeling for himself without needing guards.
2. Mid-Game: The "Toxic" Skirmisher
Yingman’s dagger is his most lethal tool. 1d4 + 4 piercing plus 7d6 psychic damage averages about 31 damage per hit.
 * Stay Mobile: With a 25 ft speed, he’s slow. Use Acrobatics (+7) to Disengage or weave through difficult terrain.
 * Targeting: Focus on targets with low Wisdom or those maintaining Concentration. Psychic damage is excellent for forcing high-DC Concentration checks.
 * Ranged Pressure: Don't forget the dagger has a 20/60 ft range. If the frontline is too dangerous, he can throw it, though he'll need a way to retrieve it or carry multiple "magically toxic" backups.
3. Defensive Maneuvers
Despite his "Serene" trait, Yingman is an administrator—he values efficiency and survival.
 * Leverage Intelligence: With a 20 INT, Yingman identifies the party's "engine." He won't waste turns hitting the high-AC Paladin; he will go for the Bard or Wizard to eliminate their utility.
 * Dwarven Resilience: Use his poison resistance to his advantage. If the fight takes place in a cloud of Stinking Cloud or near poisonous traps, Yingman can stand his ground while the party suffers.
Tactical Summary Table
| Phase | Action | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Round 1 | Song of Terror | CC the melee threats to keep them at bay. |
| Round 2 | Dagger (Melee/Thrown) | Delete a low-HP caster or disrupt concentration. |
| Reaction | Opportunity Attack | Always take it. That 7d6 psychic damage applies to OAs. |
| Emergency | Mantle of Whispers | If losing, use a captured shadow to vanish into a crowd. |
Roleplay Flavor in Combat
 * The "Paperwork" Taunt: Since he has the Bureaucracy trait, have him critiquing the party's "illegal entry" or "lack of proper adventuring permits" while he stabs them.
 * Serene Lethality: He shouldn't scream or grunt. He should remain perfectly calm, perhaps even humming a soft, discordant tune on his Recorder while the psychic damage wracks the players' brains.
