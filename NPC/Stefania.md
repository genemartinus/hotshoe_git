# Stefania Brunschweiler. Warden of Hotshoe

#### **Statistics**
- Medium humanoid, female 
- **Class:** Ranger (Level 1)
- **Armor Class:** 13 (Leather Armor)
- **Hit Points:** 11 (1d10+1) — _Adjusted to standard Level 1 Ranger HP._
- **Speed:** 30 ft.

| **STR** | **DEX** | **CON** | **INT** | **WIS** | **CHA** |
| ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- |
| 10 (+0) | 12 (+2) | 13 (+1) | 8 (-1)  | 11 (+0) | 11 (+0) |

- **Saving Throws:** Str +2, Dex +4 (Proficient)
- **Skills:** Deception +2, History +1, Insight +2, Perception +4, Stealth +4, Survival +2
- **Condition Immunities:** Magic cannot put her to sleep.
- **Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft., Passive Perception 14
- **Languages:** Common, Elvish, Aquan, Gnoll
- **Proficiency Bonus:** +2
#### **Special Traits**
- **Fey Ancestry.** Stefania has **advantage** on saving throws against being charmed.
- **Favored Enemy (Gnolls & Elementals).** Stefania has **advantage** on Wisdom (Survival) checks to track Gnolls or creatures from the Elemental Plane of Water, as well as on Intelligence checks to recall information about them.
- **Natural Explorer (Forest).** When making an Intelligence or Wisdom check related to the forest, Stefania’s proficiency bonus is doubled if she is using a skill she’s proficient in.
#### **Combat & Actions**
- **Multiattack (Special).** Stefania can use her action to make one attack with her Scimitar and one with her Dagger. _Note: Per Level 1 rules, she does not add her Ability Modifier to the damage of the second (off-hand) attack._
- **Scimitar.** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. _Hit:  `dice: 1d6 + 2` slashing damage.
- **Dagger.** _Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack:_ +4 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 20/60 ft., one target. _Hit:_ `dice: 1d4+2` piercing damage.
- **Sling.** _Ranged Weapon Attack:_ +4 to hit, range 30/120 ft., one target.  bludgeoning  _Hit:_ `dice: 1d4+2` damage.
#### **Spellcasting**.
Stefania does not possess traditional spellcasting, relying instead on her martial training and survival instincts to protect the village.
#### **Bonus actions**
- **Warden’s Focus:** Stefania can use a bonus action to mark a creature she can see within 30 feet. She has advantage on Wisdom (Perception) and Wisdom (Survival) checks made to track or locate that creature until the end of her next long rest.
#### **Reactions**
- **Skirmisher’s Retreat:** When an enemy ends its turn within 5 feet of Stefania, she can move up to half her speed as a reaction. This movement does not provoke attacks of opportunity.
#### **Roleplaying Notes**
- **Personality:** Alert and clipped. She treats the village perimeter like a holy site.
- **Demeanor:** She prefers the company of the trees to the people in the tavern. If she is in the village, she is usually there only to report to Yingman, check on Obur and Glour or buy supplies from Millicent.
- **Voice:** A quiet, steady alto that carries well in the open air.
#### **Specific storyline**
- Stefania is the primary **Warden Protector** of the village. She is the first line of defense against the Gnoll packs that roam the nearby plains and the strange, aquatic threats that occasionally emerge from the local river (explaining her fluency in Aquan and Gnoll). 
- She treats her role with grim seriousness, often skipping sleep—not that she needs much—to ensure the perimeter is secure.
- Stefania was once a scout for an Elven enclave but chose to stay behind and protect this human hamlet after a Gnoll raid took the lives of the previous wardens. 
- She speaks **Aquan** because she believes **something ancient and hungry is waking up** in the river that feeds the village crops.

#### **Logical and moral implications**
- **The High Stakes Defender:** At Level 1, her HP is still low enough that a lucky crit from a bandit could take her down. She is a protector who literally puts her life on the line every night.
- **Tactical Ruthlessness:** Because of her low Intelligence and high Survival, she values the "greater good" of the village over individual safety. She might suggest abandoning a lone straggler if it means keeping the village gates closed against a pack of Gnolls.
#### **Reward/treasure/favor**
- **The Favor:** Stefania can provide the party with a map of "The Blind Spots"—areas around the village where they can move with Advantage on Stealth checks.
- **The Loot:** A set of leather armor with a forest-green cloak, a scimitar with a wave-patterned blade, and 3 gold pieces.

Stefania is a Level 1 Ranger, which makes her a "glass cannon" in a D&D 5e combat scenario. With only 11 HP and 13 AC, she cannot afford to stand in the middle of a brawl. Her greatest asset is her Multiattack (Special), which is rare for a CR 1/4 or Level 1 character, giving her a higher damage output than most peers if she can get close.
Here is a strategy to keep the Warden of Hotshoe alive while maximizing her dual-wielding potential.
Combat Strategy: The Guerilla Warden
1. The Pre-Combat Edge: Natural Explorer
If the fight occurs in a Forest, Stefania should be the one initiating.
 * Ambush: Use her +4 Stealth to hide in foliage. At Level 1, a surprise round is the difference between winning and being downed in one hit.
 * Tracking: If the party is trying to flee, her +4 Perception and Survival (doubled in forests) mean they aren't getting away.
2. The "Sting and Slip" (Tactical Rotation)
Stefania should play like a skirmisher, not a tank.
 * Round 1 (Ranged): Start with the Sling (30/120 ft) or a thrown Dagger. Stay at max range to force the enemy to burn their movement to reach her.
 * Round 2 (The Commit): If an enemy is isolated or already damaged, move in. Use her Multiattack (Scimitar + Dagger).
   * Average Damage: 5 \text{ (Scimitar)} + 2 \text{ (Dagger off-hand)} = 7 damage.
 * Round 3 (The Retreat): If she takes even a single hit, she needs to use the Disengage action and move back toward cover. 11 HP can disappear from one lucky Longsword swing.
3. Exploiting Favored Enemies
If the party includes a Gnoll or Water Elemental (or someone summoned one):
 * Stefania should focus all her "Recall Information" checks on them to identify their weaknesses (like a Gnoll's Rampage or an Elemental's Freeze trait) and shout them to her allies.

Survival Tips for the DM
 * The "Glass" Problem: Since she is only Level 1, Stefania is very susceptible to "Magic Missile" (which can't miss) or a Sleep spell (though she is immune!).
 * Dual Wielding Note: Ensure you remember that her second attack (Dagger) does not add her +2 Dex modifier to the damage roll, as per standard Two-Weapon Fighting rules, unless she has the Fighting Style (which she doesn't get until Level 2).
 * The "Warden" Flavor: Have her use the environment. In a forest, she might lure players into a thicket or across a stream where she has the advantage.
