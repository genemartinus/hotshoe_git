# **Glour Grayclub.  Night watchman

#### **Statistics**
- **Medium Humanoid (Duergar), Lawful Neutral**
- **Armor Class:** 13 (Studded Leather)
- **Hit Points:** 4 ($1d8$)
- **Speed:** 30 ft.
- **Ability Scores:** STR 16 (+3), DEX 12 (+1), CON 10 (+0), INT 10 (+0), WIS 15 (+2), CHA 11 (+0)
- **Saving Throws:** Dex +3, Con +2
- **Skills:** Acrobatics +3, Animal Handling +4, Athletics +5, Survival +4
- **Damage Resistances:** Poison
- **Condition Immunities:** Charmed, Paralyzed (via Duergar Resilience)
- **Senses:** Darkvision 120 ft., Passive Perception 12
- **Languages:** Common, Dwarvish, Undercommon
- **Challenge:** 1/4 (50 XP) | **Proficiency Bonus:** +2
#### **Special Traits**
- **Duergar Resilience:** Glour has advantage on saving throws against illusions and against being charmed or paralyzed.
- **Sunlight Sensitivity:** While in sunlight, Glour has disadvantage on attack rolls and Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight.
- **Stonecunning:** Glour adds double his proficiency bonus to Intelligence (History) checks related to the origin of stonework.
#### **Combat & Actions**
- **Multiattack:** Glour makes two attacks: one with his Dagger and one with his Whip, or one attack with his Sling.
- **Dagger:** _Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack:_ +5 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 20/60 ft., one target. _Hit:_ 5 ($1d4 + 3$) piercing damage.
- **Whip:** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +5 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. _Hit:_ 5 ($1d4 + 3$) slashing damage.    
- **Sling:** _Ranged Weapon Attack:_ +3 to hit, range 30/120 ft., one target. _Hit:_ 3 ($1d4 + 1$) bludgeoning damage.
#### **Spellcasting**.
- Glour does not possess traditional spellcasting, relying instead on his physical discipline and Duergar heritage.
#### **Bonus actions**
- **Tactical Positioning:** Glour can use a bonus action to evaluate the terrain, granting himself advantage on his next Athletics or Acrobatics check made before the end of his turn.
#### **Reactions**
- **Backflip (1/Short or Long Rest):** When a creature attacks Glour with a melee or ranged attack, he can make a Dexterity (Acrobatics) check. If the result is higher than the attack roll, the attack misses.
#### **Roleplaying Notes**
- **Personality Trait (Relentless):** Once Glour sets his mind on a task—whether it's catching a thief or finishing a patrol—he does not stop until it is complete. He is a man of few words and iron discipline.
- **Demeanor:** Cold and observant. He often lingers in the shadows of the hamlet's stone buildings to avoid the glare of the sun.
#### **Specific storyline**
- Glour hails from an **unusual family line**—a group of Duergar who left the Underdark generations ago to seek "The True Stone." He serves as the hamlet's keeper of the peace not out of a love for the law, but because the hamlet is built atop an ancient, buried ruin that his family is sworn to watch over. He suspects the "valuable heirloom" Phanzis carries might actually be a key to the vault below.
#### **Logical and moral implications**
- **The Outsider's Justice:** As a Duergar in a surface hamlet, Glour is often viewed with suspicion. His Lawful Neutral alignment means he follows the letter of the law strictly, which can lead to moral friction when "mercy" is requested by the villagers.
- **Surface Adaptation:** His Sunlight Sensitivity makes him a night-shift specialist. If the hamlet is attacked during the day, he is significantly less effective, forcing him to rely on others or fight from the shadows.
#### **Reward/treasure/favor**
- **The Favor:** Gaining Gloin's respect grants the party a reliable ally who knows every secret passage and hidden cellar in the hamlet. He can also identify the historical significance of any stone artifacts the party finds.
- **The Treasure:** If the party assists him in his watch, he may gift them a _Whip of Warning_ or a set of _Studded Leather Armor_ reinforced with Underdark materials.

Glour is a unique tactical challenge: he has the offensive output of a seasoned warrior (Multiattack at CR 1/4 is extremely rare) but the fragility of a glass ornament with only 4 HP. As the night watchman, he is designed to end a fight before the intruder even knows he's there.
Here is the combat strategy for Glour Grayclub, the "Sting of the Night."
Combat Strategy: The 10-Foot Shadow
1. The Nighttime Advantage
Glour should never fight during the day. His Sunlight Sensitivity makes him a liability in the sun, but his 120 ft. Darkvision makes him a god in the Hotshoe night.
 * The Long Range: Glour should initiate combat from 100 feet away using his Sling. He can see the party; they likely cannot see him.
 * Ambusher: Use his Athletics (+5) to climb onto rooftops or stone ledges. He can rain stones down, then move to a different ledge before the party can pinpoint his location.
2. The "Whip-and-Fade" Technique
In melee, Glour’s best friend is his Whip. With a 10-foot reach, he can hit enemies without ever entering their 5-foot "threat zone."
 * Multiattack Combo: On his turn, Glour uses his Whip to strike from 10 feet away, then throws a Dagger at a second target or the same one.
 * Zero-Contact Combat: Because he stays 10 feet away, most melee-focused players will have to use their movement to reach him on their turn. If they do, Glour uses Acrobatics (+3) to tumble away or over obstacles.
3. Duergar Resilience: The Anti-Control Tank
Despite his low health, Glour is mentally very tough.
 * Ignoring the Bard: Spells like Vicious Mockery, Charm Person, or Hold Person are significantly less effective against him due to Duergar Resilience.
 * Poison Resistance: He can safely fight in areas filled with volcanic gases or near Kaelen's toxic fungi where players might be poisoned or paralyzed.

Synergy: The Watchmen Switch
Glour is the perfect foil to Obur (the Day Watchman).
 * The Hand-off: While Obur is the "Shield" who stands in the light and talks to people, Glour is the "Sword" who cleans up what Obur misses at night.
 * The Tactic: If they fight together, Obur stands in the front to Intercept attacks, while Glour stands 10 feet behind him, using his Whip to strike over Obur’s shoulder. This protects Glour’s 4 HP while maximizing his two attacks per round.
Roleplay Flavor: The Quiet Professional
 * Minimalist Speech: Unlike Obur's loud optimism, Glour speaks in short, gruff sentences. Usually in Undercommon or Dwarvish to keep intruders confused.
 * The "One-Hit" Reality: Glour knows he is fragile. Describe him as being incredibly twitchy and alert—he doesn't "block" attacks; he barely evades them with a look of grim determination.
 * Night Senses: Have him describe things to the party that they can't see: "Three of 'em. Hidden in the soot-bushes. One's got a bow."
