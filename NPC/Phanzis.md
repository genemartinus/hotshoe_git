# **Phanzis Guos. Noble Sorcerer 
#### Statistics**
- **Medium Humanoid (Green Dragonborn), Neutral Good**
- **Armor Class:** 9
- **Hit Points:** 5 ($1d8 - 1$)
- **Speed:** 30 ft.

| **STR** | **DEX** | **CON** | **INT** | **WIS** | **CHA** |
| ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- |
| 19 (+4) | 9 (-1)  | 8 (-1)  | 11 (+0) | 11 (+0) | 14 (+2) |

- **Saving Throws:** Con +1, Cha +4
- **Skills:** Deception +4, Nature +2, Persuasion +4
- **Damage Resistances:** Poison, Psychic
- **Senses:** Passive Perception 10
- **Languages:** Common, Deep Speech, Draconic, Telepathy 30 ft.
- **Challenge:** 1/4 (50 XP) | **Proficiency Bonus:** +2
#### **Special Traits**
- **Draconic Ancestry (Green):** Phanzis has resistance to poison damage and a poison-based breath weapon.
- **Psychic Attunement:** Phanzis possesses innate telepathic abilities and resistance to psychic damage, hinting at a lineage touched by more than just draconic blood.
#### **Combat & Actions**
- **Mind Sliver:** _Ranged Spell Attack:_ +4 to hit, range 60 ft., one target. _Hit:_ `dice: 1d6+2` psychic damage. If hit, the target must subtract  `dice: 1d4`from the next saving throw it makes before the end of Phanzis's next turn.
- **Poison Breath (1/Short or Long Rest):** Phanzis exhales poison in a 15-foot cone. Each creature in that area must make a DC 9 Dexterity saving throw, taking `dice: 2d6` poison damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one.
#### **Spellcasting**.
Phanzis is a sorcerer (Spell Save DC 12, +4 to hit with spell attacks) with the following spells prepared:
- **At will:** _infestation, prestidigitation, light_
- **2/day each:** _charm person, dissonant whispers, arms of hadar_
#### **Bonus actions**
**Telepathic Communication:** Phanzis can use his bonus action to transmit a message telepathically to any creature he can see within 30 feet.
#### **Reactions**
**Shield:** When hit by an attack or targeted by _magic missile_, Phanzis gains a +5 bonus to AC until the start of his next turn, and takes no damage from _magic missile_.
#### **Roleplaying Notes**
- **Personality Trait (Noble):** Phanzis carries himself with an air of refined grace, despite his imposing physical strength. He is polite but clearly used to being the most important person in the room.
- **Demeanor:** He is generous and well-meaning, though perhaps slightly disconnected from the everyday struggles of those with less "valuable" backgrounds.
#### **Specific storyline**
Phanzis is currently protecting a **valuable family heirloom**—a shimmering, emerald-encrusted relic that supposedly anchors the magic in his bloodline. There is a quiet tension in his household; he suspects someone in the hamlet may be looking to claim it, perhaps even someone he considers a friend.
#### **Logical and moral implications**
- **The Fragile Titan:** Despite his massive 19 Strength, Phanzis's health is incredibly precarious (5 HP). This creates a moral dilemma for protectors: he is physically capable of lifting heavy rubble or holding a gate, but a single stray arrow could be fatal.
- **The Heirloom Burden:** If the heirloom is the source of his magic, Phanzis must weigh his safety against his duty to his ancestors. If the party helps him, are they protecting a noble soul or merely a piece of property?
#### **Reward/treasure/favor**
- **The Favor:** If the party earns Phanzis’s trust, he can provide access to high-society connections, specialized information on Aberrations (due to his knowledge of Deep Speech), or temporary use of his manor as a safe haven.
- **The Treasure:** Should the party be invited to his inner circle, he might grant them a _Potion of Poison Resistance_ or a fine piece of jewelry worth 50 gp.

Phanzis is the definition of a "Glass Cannon." With a massive 19 Strength but a meager 5 HP and an AC of 9, he is physically a titan but biologically a soap bubble. In combat, he is a liability that must be managed with extreme care.
Here is the combat strategy for Phanzis Guos, designed to reflect his noble grace and his terrifying fragility.
Combat Strategy: The Fragile Paragon
1. The "Golden Hour" (Pre-Combat)
Phanzis should never be caught in the open. As a Noble, he likely has guards or "low-born" associates to stand in front of him.
 * Telepathic Coordination: Use his Bonus Action Telepathy to direct allies silently. He acts as the "brain" of the operation, positioning others to body-block for him.
 * The Shield Buffer: He must keep his Reaction open for the Shield spell. Without it, a single commoner with a sling has a 50% chance of killing him instantly.
2. High-Efficiency Spellcasting
With only 5 HP, Phanzis cannot afford a long war of attrition. He must end the fight in 1–2 rounds.
 * Round 1: Dissonant Whispers or Arms of Hadar. * If an enemy gets within 5–10 feet, he uses Arms of Hadar. It forces a Strength save (which many dex-based assassins are bad at) and prevents them from taking reactions, allowing Phanzis to walk away safely.
   * If the enemy is at a distance, Dissonant Whispers forces them to run away, wasting their turn.
 * Round 2: Mind Sliver + Poison Breath.
   * Phanzis uses Mind Sliver on a high-threat target. If it hits, they subtract 1d4 from their next save.
   * On his next turn (or an ally's turn), he follows up with his Poison Breath. The 1d4 penalty makes it much harder for them to pass the DC 9 Dexterity save.
3. The "Last Resort" Strength
His 19 Strength is a narrative trap. While he could hit someone with a staff for significant damage, doing so puts him in melee range where he will almost certainly die.
 * Environmental Interaction: Instead of attacking, Phanzis should use his Strength to Shove enemies away or topple heavy statues/furniture to create difficult terrain between himself and the threat.

The Moral Dilemma: Protecting the Heirloom
In combat, Phanzis’s priority is the Emerald Relic.
 * If he drops to 2 HP or less, he will likely attempt to flee or surrender the relic to save his life, unless his "Noble" pride overrides his survival instinct.
 * The "Fragile Titan" Hook: If a player sees Phanzis struggling to hold up a collapsing ceiling (using his 19 STR) while an enemy archer aims at his chest, it creates a high-stakes moment: Do they kill the archer or help hold the ceiling?
Roleplay Flavor in Combat
 * Refined Retorts: Even while casting Infestation, he should look disgusted that he has to resort to "vermin" to defend himself.
 * The "One-Hit" Fear: Phanzis should flinch at every missed attack. He knows he is one bad roll away from meeting his ancestors.
