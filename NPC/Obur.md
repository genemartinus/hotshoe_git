# **Obur Glinttok.  Hamlet watchman

#### **Statistics**
- **Medium Humanoid (Hill Dwarf), Lawful Good**
- **Armor Class:** 18 (Chain Mail, Shield)
- **Hit Points:** 17 ($2d8 + 8$)
- **Speed:** 25 ft.
- **Ability Scores:** STR 14 (+2), DEX 11 (+0), CON 14 (+2), INT 8 (-1), WIS 15 (+2), CHA 14 (+2)
- **Saving Throws:** Str +4, Con +4
- **Skills:** Animal Handling +4, Deception +4, Perception +4
- **Damage Resistances:** Poison
- **Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft., Passive Perception 14
- **Languages:** Common, Dwarvish
- **Challenge:** 1/2 (100 XP) | **Proficiency Bonus:** +2
#### **Special Traits**
- **Dwarven Resilience:** Obur has advantage on saving throws against poison.
- **Stonecunning:** Whenever Obur makes an Intelligence (History) check related to the origin of stonework, he adds double his proficiency bonus (+4) to the check.
- **Stealth Disadvantage:** Obur has disadvantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks while wearing his Chain Mail.
- **Action Surge (1/Short or Long Rest):** Obur can push himself beyond his normal limits. On his turn, he can take one additional action.
#### **Combat & Actions**
- **Multiattack:** Obur makes two attacks: one with his Mace and one Shield Bash, or one attack with his Javelin.
- **Mace:** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: `dice: 1d6+2` bludgeoning damage.
- **Shield Bash:** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: `dice: 1d6+2`bludgeoning damage. If the target is a Medium or smaller creature, it must succeed on a DC 12 Strength saving throw or be knocked prone.
- **Javelin:** _Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack:_ +4 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 30/120 ft., one target. Hit: `dice: 1d6+2`piercing damage.
#### **Spellcasting**.
Obur is a pure martial fighter and does not possess spellcasting abilities, though his "optimism" often feels like a magical aura to his allies.
#### **Bonus actions**
**Encouraging Word:** As a bonus action, Obur can shout a word of encouragement to an ally within 30 feet. That ally gains a +2 bonus to their next attack roll or saving throw made before the start of Obur's next turn.
#### **Reactions**
- **Second Wind (1/Short or Long Rest):** Obur can draw on a well of stamina to protect himself. He regains `dice: 1d10+2` hit points.
- **Intercept:** When a creature adjacent to Obur makes an attack roll, Obur can attempt to block it. He makes an attack roll with his Mace; if his result is higher than the attacker's roll, the attack misses.
#### **Roleplaying Notes**
- **Personality Trait (Optimistic):** Obur always believes the best of people and situations. Even in the middle of a goblin raid, he’s likely to remark on how lovely the weather is for a scrap.
- **Demeanor:** Warm, loud, and approachable. He is the face of the hamlet's watch and spends as much time petting local dogs as he does checking gate locks.
#### **Specific storyline**
He is employed as the day watchman of Hotshoe and answers to [[Stefania]] the warden.  His nighttime relief is [[Glour]] who is better suited to the darkness.
Obur is **a fighter hiding a dark secret**. Despite his cheerful exterior, he fled a distant mountain hold after a catastrophic failure in the dwarven settlement (see the [[Dwarven cemetery]]) and mines that he blames on himself. He treats the hamlet as his second chance at redemption, which is why he is so fiercely protective of its people—he cannot afford to fail a village the way he feels he failed his clan.
#### **Logical and moral implications**
- **The Burden of the Smile:** Obur’s optimism is a mask. If the party pierces his high Deception (+4), they might find a dwarf who is terrified of his past catching up to him.
- **The Shield of the Weak:** Because of his Lawful Good nature, Obur will always prioritize the safety of commoners over the capture of a criminal. A clever thief might use a distraction to force Obur to choose between a chase and saving a falling child.
#### **Reward/treasure/favor**
- **The Favor:** Obur is a master of "the local grapevine." Befriending him gives the party advantage on checks to gather rumors or find hidden locations within the hamlet.
- **The Treasure:** If the party helps Obur face his "dark secret," he may gift them his _Shield of the Sentinel_ or a masterwork _Mace_ engraved with the Stonequarry crest.


Obur is the quintessential "Tank with a Heart of Gold." While the others you've shown (like Orion or Phanzis) are fragile specialists, Obur is the anchor. He isn't there to rack up a high kill count; he is there to ensure everyone else—the villagers, Stefania, and even the party—gets home alive.
Here is the combat strategy for Obur, the optimistic shield of Hotshoe.
Combat Strategy: The Unwavering Anchor
1. The Intercept Specialist
Obur’s most powerful tool is his Intercept reaction. Unlike the standard "Protection" fighting style which just imposes disadvantage, Obur can outright nullify an attack by winning a roll-off.
 * Positioning: Obur must always be adjacent to the party's "glass cannons" (like Phanzis) or the most vulnerable NPC.
 * The Math: With a Mace attack (likely around +4 or +5 to hit), he has a high statistical chance of beating a standard enemy's attack roll. He should save his Reaction exclusively for this.
2. Tactical Durability: The "Second Wind" Reset
Obur is meant to soak up damage that would otherwise kill his allies.
 * The Threshold: Do not use Second Wind the moment he takes damage. Wait until he is below 50% HP. Since he only regains 5 HP, it is most effective as a "clutch" heal to stay standing for one more round of Interceptions.
 * Crowd Control: Obur should use the Shove action frequently. With his likely high Strength, he can knock enemies prone or push them 5 feet away from a fragile ally, allowing that ally to move away without drawing an Opportunity Attack.
3. Moral Combat: The "Protective Shield"
Obur's "Dark Secret" makes him overcompensate. In combat, he will prioritize The Shield of the Weak.
 * The Choice: If an enemy targets a commoner or a downed ally, Obur will abandon his current duel to rush to their side, even if it means taking an Opportunity Attack himself.
 * Non-Lethal Force: Unless he is fighting something truly monstrous (like a Gnoll), Obur likely prefers to knock enemies unconscious rather than kill them, hoping for their "redemption."

Synergy: Working with the Warden
Obur and Stefania (the Warden) likely have a practiced combat rhythm:
 * The Hammer & Anvil: Obur stands his ground (The Anvil) and draws the enemies' attention with his loud, cheerful shouting.
 * The Strike: While Obur keeps the enemies busy and uses Intercept to keep Stefania safe, she uses her Multiattack (The Hammer) to finish them off.
Roleplay Flavor in Combat
 * The "Cheerful" Combatant: Every time he blocks an attack with Intercept, he should offer a compliment: "Right fine form on that swing, lad! Nearly had me!" or "Bit gloomy for a fight, don't you think? Put some heart into it!"
 * The Cracks in the Mask: If an ally is seriously hurt, his optimism should momentarily vanish, replaced by a grim, dwarven stoicism that hints at his failure at the mountain hold.
