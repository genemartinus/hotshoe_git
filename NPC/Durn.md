## Pack-Mule, Muscle and Shield

#### **Statistics**
- **Medium Human, Lawful Neutral**
- **Armor Class:** 14 (Hide armor and a heavy slate-shield)
- **Hit Points:** 15 ($2d8 + 6$)
- **Speed:** 25 ft.

| **STR** | **DEX** | **CON** | **INT** | **WIS** | **CHA** |
| ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- | ------- |
| 16 (-3) | 10 (+0) | 16 (+3) | 8(-1)   | 10 (+0) | 8 (-1)  |

---
- **Ability Scores:** STR 16 (+3), DEX 10 (+0), CON 16 (+3), INT 8 (-1), WIS 10 (+0), CHA 8 (-1)
- **Skills:** Athletics +5, Survival +2, Mason's Tools +3
- **Senses:** Passive Perception 10
- **Languages:** Common, Dwarvish (Fluent)
- **Challenge:** 1/4 (50 XP) | **Proficiency Bonus:** +2
#### **Special Traits**
- **Sturdy Stance:** Durn has **Advantage** on Strength and Constitution saving throws made against effects that would knock him prone or move him against his will.
- **Heavy Hauler:** Durn is considered a Large creature for the purpose of determining his carrying capacity.
- **Tunnel Intuition:** Having spent decades underground, Durn can automatically sense the direction of the nearest exit while in a cave or mine.
#### **Combat & Actions**
- **Iron Pry-Bar:** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: `dice: 1d8+3`bludgeoning damage.
- **Shield Shove:** _Melee Manual Attack:_ +5 to hit. Durn slams his heavy shield into a creature. The target must succeed on a DC 13 Strength saving throw or be pushed 5 feet and knocked prone.
#### **Spellcasting**.
- **1/Day:** _Detect Poison and Disease_ (Flavor: Durn tastes the air for "Bad Air" or volcanic gas pockets).
#### **Bonus actions**
- **"Stand Behind Me!":** Durn can use a bonus action to plant his slate-shield. Until the start of his next turn, an ally within 5 feet of him gains a +2 bonus to AC.
#### **Reactions**
- **Interpose:** When a creature within 5 feet of Durn (usually Kip) is targeted by an attack, Durn can use his reaction to become the target of that attack instead.
#### **Roleplaying Notes**
 - **Personality:** Stoic, dour, and deeply protective. He speaks in short, low rumbles. He doesn't scavenge for the gold; he does it because he doesn't know how to exist without the weight of stone nearby.
 - **Visuals:** A massive man with a back slightly hunched from years of heavy lifting. His hide armor is scorched, and his hands are permanently blackened by soot and stone dust.
#### **Specific storyline**
Durn is a shy artist with a fascination for death and the afterlife.  He has a **special gift for headstones** and some skill with stonecraft as an orphan raised by dwarf refugees from the dwarf stronghold at Hotshoe.  He spends his nights carving small basalt headstones for the men whose bodies were never recovered from the deep shafts.  He might welcome some help to carry those headstones up to the Dwarven cemetery.
#### **Logical and moral implications**
- **Displaced Loyalty:** Durn follows Orion not because Orion is a good leader, but because Orion provides a "crew." Without this structure, Durn fears he would simply sit down in the ash and wait for the mountain to take him.
- **Sunk Cost:** Durn knows the steam and gas vents are unstable. He calculates the risk every day, knowing that his strength is the only thing keeping Kip from being crushed, yet he continues to lead them back to the danger for a pittance of gold.
#### **Reward/treasure/favor**
- **The Favor:** If the party helps Durn recover a specific "Foreman's Seal" from a shallow, ash-filled tunnel, he will teach them the **"Dwarven Bracing Technique"** (giving them Advantage on saves against being knocked prone for 24 hours).
- **The favor** Help Durn care for the headstones at the Dwarven cemetery.
- **The Loot:** A heavy iron key to a locker that no longer exists, a flask of low-quality dwarven rotgut, and a small pouch of 15 sp.
- **The loot**t Durn makes [[Hot yam polish]] and he might give player some or show them how to make it.  Knowing how to make it is geographically limiting because the ingredients are only in Hotshoe.
- **The loot** - Durn will help the players get up the lava field to collect some obsidian shards.

Durn is the "Anvil" of the Black Gold scavenging team. While Orion provides the lethal damage and Kip provides the distraction, Durn’s job is to occupy the most dangerous player on the board and keep them from moving. With 15 HP and a 14 AC, he is relatively sturdy for a CR 1/4 NPC, but his real strength lies in his Sturdy Stance and Shield Shove.
Here is the combat strategy for Durn, the Pack-Mule and Muscle.
Combat Strategy: The Unmovable Sentry
1. The "Shield Slam" Lock
Durn’s primary contribution to a fight is not damage; it is Control.
 * The Combo: Durn should use Shield Shove as his primary action against the party's most mobile or high-AC character.
 * The Goal: If he knocks a target Prone, he creates a massive opening. Orion can then move in and deliver a Sneak Attack with automatic Advantage, or the rest of the team can pile on before the target can stand up.
2. Terrain Mastery: The Human Doorway
Because Durn has Sturdy Stance, he is incredibly difficult to push out of the way.
 * Chokepoint Control: In the narrow tunnels of a volcanic mine, Durn should stand in a 5-foot-wide gap. Because he has Advantage against being moved or knocked prone, even a high-strength Fighter will have a hard time shoving him aside.
 * The "Large" Capacity: Use his Heavy Hauler trait narratively. He can flip over heavy mining carts or move massive boulders to create instant cover for himself and his allies.
3. The "Iron Pry-Bar" Deterrent
If Durn has successfully knocked an enemy prone, he follows up with the Iron Pry-Bar.
 * Focus Fire: He should stay on one target until they are neutralized. He isn't a flashy fighter; he is a professional laborer who treats combat like "breaking stone." He hits the same spot until it breaks.
Tactical Summary Table
| Situation | Action | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Opening Move | Shield Shove | Knock the most dangerous melee threat Prone. |
| Target is Prone | Iron Pry-Bar | Attack with Advantage to chip away at their health. |
| Enemy Escaping | Opportunity Attack | Use the Pry-Bar to punish anyone trying to bypass him. |
| Defensive | Dodge / Shove | If low on health, just stay in the doorway and Dodge. |
Synergy: The Black Gold Team
Durn is the glue that keeps Orion and Kip alive.
 * The Protector: Durn stands 5 feet in front of Orion. By being adjacent to Orion's target, he guarantees that Orion gets Sneak Attack damage every turn, even if Orion doesn't have Advantage.
 * The Anchor for Kip: When Kip uses his Soot-Cloud, Durn is the only one who stays in the middle of the mess. He uses his high Strength to grapple anyone trying to stumble out of the cloud, keeping them trapped in the blindness.
Roleplay Flavor: The Silent Strength
 * Minimalist Grunts: Durn doesn't taunt. He breathes heavily and focuses on his footing. Every hit he takes is met with a stoic grunt and a readjustment of his slate-shield.
 * Tunnel Intuition: If the fight goes south, Durn is the one who signals the retreat. He knows exactly where the nearest exit is, and he will use his body to block the tunnel while Orion and Kip scramble to safety.
 * The Mason's Eye: He might shout out structural weaknesses during a fight: "Watch the ceiling! That pillar's cracked!"
