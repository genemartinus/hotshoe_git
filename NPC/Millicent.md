Millicent is not a warrior; she is a high-utility support NPC who uses chemistry and terrain to compensate for her low AC and poor Perception. With 15 HP and 10 AC, she is incredibly vulnerable. However, her CR 2 rating suggests that her "Apothecary" gear is much more dangerous than her physical stats.
Here is a combat strategy for Millicent based on her three distinct environments.
General Combat Logic: The Alchemical Bully
Millicent relies on Area of Effect (AoE) and Status Afflictions. She doesn't aim for a "hit"; she aims for a "zone."
 * Intimidation as a Weapon: Use her Abrasive Shield trait to end fights before they start. She doesn't ask the party to leave; she barks orders so aggressively they might actually obey just to avoid the headache.
 * The "Best Defense" is a Bad Smell: Since her AC is 10, her goal is to make it impossible for enemies to reach her without passing a saving throw against a foul-smelling or caustic cloud.
Strategy by Environment
1. At Home (The Laboratory)
This is Millicent’s "Lair." She has the home-field advantage and a shelf full of unstable prototypes.
 * The "Mistake" Bottle: Millicent breaks a bottle of "Failing Antitoxin" at her feet. This creates a 10ft cloud of Obscurement. She knows where her furniture is; the players don't.
 * Abrasive Shield: She screams at the players about "Contaminating the sterile environment!" forcing an Intimidation (+5 with Advantage) check to potentially Frighten them or make them hesitate.
2. On the Job (The Market/Clinic)
Here, Millicent is protective of her supplies.
 * Medicine as Poison: She uses her Medicine (+3) and Nature (+3) skills to identify the party’s "weakest link" (the one looking pale or coughing) and targets them with a customized debuff.
 * The Crowd Buffer: She uses her Master of the Still knowledge to splash a quick "Adrenaline Tonic" on an ally (like Obur), giving them a temporary boost while she retreats behind them.
3. Crossing the Grey Wash (The Bridge/Wilds)
The most dangerous location for her. She is exposed and her Passive Perception (9) makes her easy to surprise.
 * Survival Instinct: She uses her Auran language to whistle for a local wind spirit or simply to gauge the wind direction before throwing a Pouch of Blinding Powder.
 * The Bridge Chokepoint: On the bridge, she uses her chemicals to create "Slick Spots" (effectively the Grease spell). If players slip, she doesn't stab them; she uses her action to try and push them toward the edge (Athletics +0, but likely against a Prone target).

Logical & Moral Implications
 * The "Rough" Healer: Millicent is Lawful Good, but she is mean. She might heal a player while insulting their hygiene or their combat prowess.
 * The Reluctant Combatant: She values her life and her work above all else. If she is reduced to 5 HP, she will immediately surrender—but she will spend the entire time they are tying her up complaining about how they are "ruining the PH balance of her wrists."
