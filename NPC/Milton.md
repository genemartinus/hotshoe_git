Milton is not a soldier; he is a desperate shopkeeper with a green thumb and a very sharp rapier. With 18 HP and a 13 Passive Perception, he is more durable than a commoner but will fold quickly under sustained fire.
His combat style is "The Reluctant Professional"—he uses the skills he likely learned in a shadier past to keep himself alive long enough to see his apples grow.
Combat Strategy: The Horticultural Guerrilla
1. The "Salesman’s Slink" (Cunning Action)
Milton should never end his turn standing next to a player. He is a "Skirmish Rogue."
 * Bonus Action Disengage: If a player gets into melee range, Milton delivers a quick remark about the "poor quality of their steel," stabs with his rapier, and uses his Bonus Action to Disengage and move 30 feet away.
 * Bonus Action Hide: If the shop is dim or there are crates/apple barrels for cover, he will shoot his crossbow and immediately Hide. He is much more dangerous when the players lose track of him.
2. High-Value Targeting (Sneak Attack)
Milton’s Sneak Attack is modest (+2 damage), but his base weapon damage is high for his CR (9 with the Crossbow).
 * The "Orchard Guardian": If Milton is fighting alongside his pigs or an ally like Obur, he will focus fire on whoever the ally is currently distracting to ensure he gets that extra 2 damage.
 * The "Raw Deal" Insight: Because Milton has Advantage on Insight, he is excellent at reading feints. Narratively, he might see through a player's "distraction" or "trick" move, allowing him to stay one step ahead.
3. Home Field Advantage: The Shop & Pig Pen
If the fight happens at his shop, Milton uses the environment to frustrate the party:
 * The Pig Pen: If forced outside, Milton might lead players through the mud. He knows the footing; they don't. He might even release a pig to create a distraction (effectively an "ally" for Sneak Attack purposes).
 * The Stone Shrapnel: His shop is "a masterwork of stonemasonry." He will use the heavy stone counters as Three-Quarters Cover (+5 to AC and Dex saves), popping up only to fire his hand crossbow.

Milton’s theory is a stroke of "Wasteland Genius." By raising pigs on a strict diet of **Ash Yams**, he has effectively turned their hides into biological thermal-regulators before the tanning even begins. However, traditional tanning with water and oak bark would ruin the unique cellular "battery" of the Yam-fed skin.

To solve the puzzle, Milton has developed a radical new process.

## 🧪 The Solution: "Thermal-Gradient Tanning"

Instead of soaking the hides in a vat of liquid, Milton uses a dry-cure method that mimics the Ash Yam's own growth cycle.

1. **The Brine-Scour:** The pig hides are first scrubbed with a mixture of **Scald-Scale Serpent salt** and crushed **Cinder-Berry thorns**. This "tricks" the leather into a state of high reactivity, opening the pores.
    
2. **The Yam-Pulp Infusion:** A thick paste of fermented **Ash Yam cores** is pressed into the flesh side of the hide.
    
3. **The Solar-Flash:** The hides are stretched over **Dwarven Obsidian mirrors** and left in the noon-day sun. The mirrors reflect heat into the underside of the leather while the air cools the top. This recreates the **Thermal Gradient**, "locking" the Yam’s carbon-dense starches into the leather's fibers.
    
4. **The Mica-Finish:** Finally, the leather is buffed with fine dust from **Prismatic Willow** leaves, giving it a shimmering, heat-reflective seal.

## 🛡️ Product Description: "Conniption-Clad" Pigskin

**Market Name:** _Milton’s Iron-Sow Leather_

This leather doesn't look like the soft, pink pigskin of the lowlands. It is a deep, scorched umber with a faint, oily iridescence that shifts from orange to green in the light—a side effect of the **Jade-Glint** traces in the local soil.

- **Feel:** Heavy, dense, and slightly warm to the touch. It has the flexibility of fine suede but the "thrum" of a living organism.
    
- **Properties:**
    
    - **Heat Absorption:** It doesn't just resist heat; it _eats_ it. A wearer can walk through a **Steam Vent** without feeling a singe, as the leather converts the thermal energy into a dull, comforting glow.
        
    - **Acid Neutralization:** The serpent-salt cure makes the leather naturally basic, neutralizing the caustic properties of **Boiling Brine** on contact.
        
    - **The "Yam-Snap":** If the leather is cut, applying a small amount of heat (like a torch flame) causes the fibers to contract and "scab" over, partially repairing minor tears.

To bring the quantitative mechanics of the Hotshoe saga into focus, we need to bridge the gap between "flavor" and the "math" of the d20 system. Below are the specific combat and exploration stats for the regional materials, followed by the definitive recipe for Iron-Sow Plate.
🛠️ Material Mechanics: Combat & Perception
The environment of Mt. Conniption imposes specific modifiers. Using local materials can mitigate these or provide unique tactical advantages.
1. Prismatic Willow (Mica Dust)
 * Perception Hazard: In winds above 10 mph, the grove becomes a Restrained Light zone. Creatures without goggles or a face mask have Disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks.
 * Combat Utility: A handful of raw Mica Dust can be thrown as a Bonus Action (5 ft range). The target must succeed on a DC 12 Constitution Save or be Blinded until the start of their next turn.
2. Cinder-Berry (The "Phantom Burn")
 * Saving Throw: Eating a raw berry or being hit by a thorn-whip requires a DC 12 Wisdom (Insight) Save.
 * Combat Penalty: On a failure, the creature believes they are On Fire. They take 0 damage but must spend their Reaction to pat themselves down, or they suffer Disadvantage on their next attack roll due to the distraction.
3. Needle Grass (Conductive Iron)
 * AC Interaction: Raw Needle Grass is a hazard. Moving through a patch without a DC 13 Dexterity (Acrobatics) check deals 1d4 piercing damage.
 * The "Grounding" Property: If a creature is wearing metal armor reinforced with Needle Grass, they have Advantage on Saving Throws against lightning damage, as the grass "bleeds" the charge into the ash.
🛡️ The Master Recipe: "Needle-Vined Iron-Sow"
Milton has realized that while his Yam-fed pigskin is tough, it lacks the rigidity to stop a War Pick. By weaving Needle Grass directly into the "Iron-Sow" hide during the Solar-Flash process, he creates the ultimate wasteland armor.
Quantitative Stats: Iron-Sow Padded/Leather
 * Armor Class (AC): Base +1 (e.g., Iron-Sow Padded is AC 12 + Dex; Iron-Sow Studded is AC 13 + Dex).
 * Thermal Sink: The wearer has Resistance to Fire damage from environmental sources (lava, steam vents, ash).
 * Needle-Thorns (Combat): When a creature hits the wearer with an unarmed strike or a grapple, the attacker takes 1d4 piercing damage from the Needle Grass weave.
Quantitative Stats: The "Heavy" Variant (Splint/Plate)
 * AC Bonus: +1 AC compared to standard versions.
 * Weight: 10% heavier than standard.
 * Quantitative Perk: The armor is immune to the "Corrosive Brine" effect of Scald-Scale Serpents, which normally degrades non-magical metal AC by 1.
