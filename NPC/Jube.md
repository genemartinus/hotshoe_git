# **Jube Curnow.  Hotshoe cemetery protector

#### **Statistics**
- **Small Humanoid (Ghostwise Halfling), Lawful Neutral**
- **Armor Class:** 17 (Splint)
- **Hit Points:** 7 ($3d6 - 3$)
- **Speed:** 15 ft. (Reduced by Splint armor requirements)
- **Ability Scores:** STR 11 (+0), DEX 18 (+4), CON 8 (-1), INT 10 (+0), WIS 11 (+0), CHA 10 (+0)
- **Saving Throws:** Wis +2, Cha +2
- **Skills:** Deception +2, Insight +2, Medicine +2
- **Senses:** Passive Perception 10
- **Languages:** Common, Halfling, Telepathy 30 ft
- **Challenge:** 1 (200 XP) | **Proficiency Bonus:** +2
#### **Special Traits**
- **Brave.** Jube has advantage on saving throws against being frightened.
- **Nimble.** Jube can move through the space of any Medium or larger creature.
- **Lucky.** When Jube rolls a 1 on an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw, she can reroll the die and must use the new result, even if it is a 1.
- **Silent Speech.** Jube can speak telepathically to any creature within 30 feet. The creature understands her if both share a language.
- **Disciple of Life.** Whenever Jube uses a spell to restore hit points, the target regains 3 additional hit points.
- **Stealth Disadvantage.** Jube has disadvantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks while wearing splint armor.
#### **Combat & Actions**

- **Javelin:** _Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack:_ +2 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 30/120 ft., one target. _Hit:_ 3 ($1d6$) piercing damage.
- **Healing Touch:** As an action, Jube can cast _Cure Wounds_. Due to her Disciple of Life trait, her healing is surprisingly potent despite her lack of focus.
#### **Spellcasting**.

Jube uses Wisdom as her spellcasting ability (Spell Save DC 10, +2 to hit with spell attacks):
- **At will:** _spare the dying, thaumaturgy, resistance_
- **2/day each:** _burning hands, cure wounds, bless_
#### **Bonus actions**
**Mental Static:** Jube can use her telepathy to project a disorganized "thought-dump" into a creature's mind, giving them disadvantage on their next Concentration check made before the start of her next turn.
#### **Reactions**
**Halt!:** When a creature Jube can see within 30 feet makes an attack roll, she can use her reaction to cast _Thaumaturgy_ to create a sudden, booming clap of thunder, momentarily distracting the attacker.
#### **Roleplaying Notes**
- - **Personality Trait (Disorganized):** Jube is a whirlwind of misplaced holy symbols, half-finished prayers, and mismatched armor pieces. She often forgets which deity she is supposed to be representing that day.
- **Demeanor:** Frequently flustered and out of breath (due to her heavy armor and low speed). She tends to speak telepathically even when standing right next to someone because she "forgets" to use her actual voice.
- **The Clatter:** Jube doesn't walk—she rattles. Between her low Strength and heavy armor, she constantly trips over uneven cobblestones or her own greaves.
- **Telepathic Tics:** She often forgets to use her actual voice. You'll be mid-conversation when her voice suddenly pops into your brain: _"Wait, did I leave the incense burning? Also, hello."_
- **The slowest Cleric:** She forgets the words to prayers, loses her holy symbol in her many pockets, and her 15-foot movement speed means she usually arrives at the scene of an injury just as the patient has finished healing naturally.
#### **Specific storyline**
She didn't choose the life; her family forced her into the clergy to "straighten her out." She is currently trying to find a "miracle" to perform so her temple will finally stop sending her sternly worded letters.
She was sent to a prestigious temple of a life god, but they "stationed" her in this remote hamlet just to get her out of the main cathedral.  She might actually be a cleric of a god of peace or hearth (like **Eldath** or **Yondalla**) who accidentally keeps prepping combat spells because she "grabs the wrong prayer book" in the morning.
#### **Logical and moral implications**
- **The Clanking Healer:** Jube is the least "stealthy" halfling in existence. Her presence in a party means the group will almost certainly be heard coming from a mile away.
- **Incompetence vs. Intent:** While she is disorganized, her _Cure Wounds_ is actually more effective than most (thanks to Disciple of Life). This creates a contradiction: do the villagers trust the cleric who can actually knit bone together, or do they avoid the woman who once accidentally set a haystack on fire while trying to cast _Light_ (using _Burning Hands_ by mistake)?
#### **Reward/treasure/favor**
- **The Favor:** If the party helps Jube organize her medical kit or ghost-writes a report to her superiors, she will provide them with free (though chaotic) healing services whenever they return to the hamlet.
- **The Treasure:** Jube might "accidentally" give the party a _Seward’s Curative Draught_ or a _Scroll of Lesser Restoration_ that she found at the bottom of her cluttered pack.

Jube Curnow is a mechanical contradiction: a high-AC tank with the hit points of a housecat. With 7 HP and an AC of 17, she is the "Orchard of Hope" version of a bunker. If she gets hit once, she’s likely down, but hitting her is surprisingly difficult for low-level characters.
Because her speed is a dismal 15 ft., Jube doesn't "enter" combat—she is a stationary turret of support and psychic static.
Combat Strategy: The Clattering Bastion
1. The "Turret" Positioning
Jube cannot chase anyone. She must pick a spot (preferably behind half-cover like a gravestone) and stay there.
 * The Anchor: She should stand adjacent to a high-value ally (like Obur or Stefania). Her 17 AC makes her a frustrating obstacle for enemies trying to reach the backline.
 * Nimble Navigation: If she must move, she uses Nimble to walk right through the legs of Medium enemies. Since she doesn't care about Opportunity Attacks as much (due to her high AC), she can reposition to deliver a touch spell.
2. Reactive Disruption
Jube’s best tools are her Reactions and Bonus Actions, which don't require her to move her heavy, rattling splint armor.
 * The "Halt!" Reaction: Use this on the first enemy attack of every round. A booming clap of Thaumaturgy is a fantastic narrative way to explain a "near miss" against an ally.
 * Mental Static: Every single turn, Jube should use her Bonus Action to bombard the enemy spellcaster's mind. Giving a Wizard or Druid disadvantage on Concentration checks is a massive debuff for a CR 1 creature.
3. The "Surprise" Medic
Despite her disorganized nature, Jube’s healing is her strongest asset.
 * Disciple of Life: Her Cure Wounds heals for 1d8 + 3 (plus her spellcasting mod). For a commoner or a low-level NPC, this is a full reset.
 * Spare the Dying: Since she moves so slowly, she should use this on any fallen ally within reach to keep the "Hotshoe" team's action economy going.

Synergy: The "Slow and Steady" Duo
Jube and Obur (the Cemetery Protector and the Watchman) are a formidable pair.
 * The Wall: Obur uses Intercept to protect Jube’s 7 HP.
 * The Battery: Jube stays behind Obur, using Mental Static to distract his attackers and Cure Wounds to keep him at full health.
 * Telepathic Comms: Jube coordinates the team via Silent Speech, allowing the protectors to shift tactics without the enemies overhearing.
Roleplay Flavor: The Static of Chaos
 * Telepathic Overload: When she uses Mental Static, the player doesn't just feel "distracted." They hear Jube’s internal monologue: "Did I feed the pigs? Nerull's beard, this armor is heavy. I think I have a lemon in my pocket. STRIKE AT THE KNEES—oh, sorry, was that out loud?"
 * The Lucky Rattle: If she rolls a 1 and uses Lucky, describe it as her tripping over her own splint armor, which accidentally causes her to duck under an arrow or swing her javelin in a way that catches the enemy off guard.
