# **Dimble Pingun. Proprietor "The thirsty pebble" 
#### **Statistics**
- **Small Humanoid (Gnome), Neutral Good**
- **Armor Class:** 10
- **Hit Points:** 8 ($1d6 + 2$)
- **Speed:** 20 ft.
- **Ability Scores:** STR 12 (+1), DEX 8 (-1), CON 14 (+2), INT 12 (+1), WIS 14 (+2), CHA 15 (+2)
- **Skills:** Acrobatics +1, Animal Handling +4, Persuasion +4
- **Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft., Passive Perception 12
- **Languages:** Common, Gnomish, Halfling
- **Challenge:** 1 (200 XP) | 
- **Proficiency Bonus:** +2
#### **Special Traits**
- **Gnome Cunning:** Dimble has advantage on all Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma saving throws against magic.
- **Barkeep’s Hospitality:** Dimble has an uncanny knack for making people feel welcome. He has advantage on Charisma (Persuasion) checks made to de-escalate a fight or gather rumors in a social setting.
- **Sneak Attack (1/Turn):** Dimble deals an extra 3 ($1d6$) damage when he hits a target with a weapon attack and has advantage on the attack roll, or when the target is within 5 feet of an ally of Dimble that isn't incapacitated and he doesn't have disadvantage on the roll.
- **Legacy Discipline:** Despite his rags, Dimble’s soldier-like posture grants him advantage on saving throws against being frightened.
- **Secret sauce** Dimble's tavern's particular reputation for beer comes in part because he has a fermenter's stone, hence the name of the tavern.  Place the pebble in a container of water and overnight (12hrs) it becomes beer.  There are competing explanations about the magic involved, illusion, alchemy, druid craft or a combination.  Equally uncertain is if the pebble's incantation is permanent or requires some sort of recharge or sacrifice.  Dimble doesn't know and would like to find out because he's worried about the future of his tavern.  He is a shining star when it comes to economic success in Hotshoe and a mystery because Dimble isn't buying any of the ingredients needed to make beer.  Dimble is concerned that Millicent will figure out his secret even if she is not a beer drinker.
#### **Combat & Actions**
- **Lance (Father’s Legacy):** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +3 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. _Hit:_ 7 ($1d12 + 1$) piercing damage. Dimble has disadvantage when he uses the lance to attack a target within 5 feet of him.
- **Walking Cane (Club):** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +3 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. _Hit:_ 3 ($1d4 + 1$) bludgeoning damage.
- **Grapple:** _Melee Manual Attack:_ +4 to hit.
#### **Spellcasting**.
Dimble knows one minor trick from his days in the scriptorium:

- **At will:** _prestidigitation_ (used almost exclusively to clean ink stains or chill a mug of ale).
#### **Bonus actions**
**Attention Seeker:** Dimble is masterfully distracting. He performs a flamboyant flourish or a grand, melodious greeting. One creature within 30 feet that can see him must succeed on a DC 12 Wisdom saving throw or have disadvantage on its next attack roll not directed at Dimble.
#### **Reactions**
**Humble Trip:** When a creature Dimble can see moves out of a space within 5 feet of him, he can attempt to wedge his walking cane between its legs. The target must succeed on a DC 11 Dexterity saving throw or fall prone.
#### **Roleplaying Notes**
- **Personality:** Gregarious, optimistic, and deeply social. He believes that a stranger is just a friend he hasn't shared a drink with yet. He is quietly desperate for romantic validation—often asking travelers if they know any "fit dates" in the neighboring towns.
- **Voice:** Soft and melodious, carrying a gentle, song-like quality even when he's calling out orders in the pub.
- **Demeanor:** He is a walking contradiction—waist-length hair and rags paired with a perfectly polished, military-grade lance.
#### **Specific storyline**
Dimble is a former **scribe turned barkeep** who took up the apron to make ends meet, but his heart remains in the scriptorium. He carries his father's lance as a matter of family honor, though his tiny frame makes it a comical sight. He is the hamlet's primary source of "soft" information; if a traveler passed through in the last month, Dimble has likely shared a meal with them.
#### **Logical and moral implications**
- **The Fragile Peacemaker:** Dimble will actively try to talk his way out of a fight before drawing his lance. His high Charisma makes him effective, but his low HP (8) and Speed (20 ft.) make him a massive liability if the talking fails.
- **The Social Anchor:** Because he thrives on attention, he can be easily manipulated by someone who offers him genuine flattery or the promise of an office job.
#### **Reward/treasure/favor**
- **The Favor:** Dimble can forge documents with remarkable accuracy (a remnant of his scribe days) or put the party in touch with anyone in the Hotshoe's social circles.
- **The Loot:** A simple walking cane, a well-maintained soldier's lance, and the fermenter's pebble.

Dimble Pingun is a "Glass Turret" with a reach problem. As a Small creature with a 20 ft. speed and only 8 HP, he is incredibly easy to corner and kill. However, his Lance and Attention Seeker ability allow him to influence the battlefield from a safe distance—provided he has someone standing between him and the danger.
Here is the combat strategy for the proprietor of The Thirsty Pebble.
Combat Strategy: The Distracting Lancer
1. The "Reach & Reset" Protocol
Dimble’s Lance has a 10-foot reach, but he has Disadvantage if an enemy is within 5 feet.
 * The Sweet Spot: Dimble must stay exactly 10 feet away from his target. Since his speed is only 20 ft., he cannot outrun most enemies. He must use his Attention Seeker bonus action to force enemies to look at him, then strike with the Lance.
 * Sneak Attack Synergy: Dimble deals an extra 3 damage if an ally is within 5 feet of his target. He should always aim for the enemy currently engaged with Durn or Obur. A poke from his father's lance suddenly becomes much more painful (average 10 damage).
2. The "Humble Trip" Defense
If an enemy realizes Dimble is a threat and tries to move past him to reach a caster (or tries to flee), Dimble uses his Reaction.
 * The Cane Trip: He wedges his walking cane in their path. If they fall Prone, they are stuck. On Dimble’s next turn, he can step back 5 feet (to clear his Disadvantage) and skewered them with the Lance while they are on the ground.
3. De-Escalation First
With Barkeep’s Hospitality, Dimble should rarely be the one to roll initiative.
 * The Social Shield: He should use his first turn to attempt to Persuade (with Advantage) the attackers to stop. "Now, now, let's not ruin the floorboards! There's a fresh cask of 'Pebble Ale' on the house if we can just put the steel away!"
 * Prestidigitation: He might use this to "chill" the blood of an aggressive attacker or create a sudden sensory distraction (like the smell of spilled ale) to reinforce his Persuasion attempt.

The Secret of the Pebble
In combat, Dimble is terrified of losing his Fermenter’s Stone.
 * The Secret Stash: He likely keeps the pebble in a hidden pocket or even inside his mouth during a fight.
 * The "Millicent" Factor: If Millicent Preston is present, Dimble will be distracted, trying to look "soldier-like" to impress her or hide his rags, while simultaneously worrying she'll smell the lack of hops and barley on his breath.
Synergy: The Thirsty Protectors
Dimble is the social heart of Hotshoe, and the other NPCs will fight harder to protect him.
 * Obur & Jube: They will naturally form a perimeter around Dimble. Obur can Intercept hits meant for the gnome, while Jube uses Mental Static to help Dimble’s Attention Seeker DC land more effectively.
 * The Distraction: While Dimble is doing his "flamboyant flourish," Orion or Kip can move into position for their own Sneak Attacks.
Roleplay Flavor: The Soldier-Scribe
 * The Voice: Even in a life-or-death struggle, his voice remains "melodious." He might hum a tavern tune while stabbing with the lance.
 * Post-Combat: If he survives, he immediately uses Prestidigitation to clean his father's lance. "Can't have the old man's legacy smelling like a goblin, can we?"
