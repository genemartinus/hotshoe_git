**Scene:** The interior of Dimble's Tavern, _The Frothing Stein_, in Hotshoe. The air is thick with the rich, inviting scent of freshly brewed ale—too inviting, for a tavern that hasn't seen a shipment of grain in months. **Millicent Preston** (6'0", gaunt, in embroidered leathers and a feathered tan hat) stands aggressively close to the counter, her arms crossed, her intense hazel eyes, burning with focused, abrasive energy, locked on **Dimble**. Dimble (Gnome, Neutral, Level 1 Brewer) is practically vibrating with anxiety, dabbing at a fine layer of grey volcanic ash that coats his polished wooden bar. His eyes are wide, and a nervous, friendly-but-tight smile is plastered across his face.

---

**[Narrative]** Millicent moves with a martial cadence, stepping from one side of Dimble to the other, making her towering presence oppressive. Dimble uses his counter rag as a shield, focusing on a particularly stubborn ash-stain. The volcano, a brooding, smoky shape on the distant horizon, grumbles, emphasizing the strange, almost mocking prosperity of the tavern.

**Millicent Preston** (Her sharp Scottish accent is clipped and demanding) "It’s a peculiar harvest, Dimble. The rest of Hotshoe chokes on this ash. Crops are withering. Water elementals are screaming, if you ask the right people. Yet, I walk into _The Frothing Stein_, and it smells... ripe. It smells like a field of barley, right when it’s heavy and ready for the cut."

**Dimble** (His voice is a high-pitched, fast, and anxious prattle) "Ah, well, you know, Millicent... climate. It’s a micro-climate! The mountain, it does strange things. Buffers. Shelters. My tavern’s internal compass for climate justice ensures that only the best, most resilient barley flourishes in my... (he gestures wildly and vaguely)—_my_ specialized, sheltered fields. Yes. Specialized. That's the ticket."

**Millicent Preston** (A commanding, sharp Scottish snap—she slaps her gauntleted hand on the bar, causing a small cloud of ash to puff from a glass flask near the counter) "Specialized fields? In this ash desert? My internal compass for justice tells me you’re a blooming liar. I have an order pending with the only miller within fifty miles—the miller whose arts I fund—and he has not milled grain for you, Dimble. Nor have you bought yeast. Or hops. Or water purification alembics from my apothecary. What, are you simply _wishing_ this beer into existence?"

**Dimble** "Wishing! Ah, what an imagination, Millicent. No, no wishing. That’s... unprofessional. (He stops polishing and leans in, trying to look eerie and patient, like his hero Kaelen). We are all gleaners in Nerull’s harvest. I am simply... tending to a crop that is unique. It’s an alchemical marvel, really! Yes. Alchemical. I combine the elements: Water. Earth. Air. And... (he holds up his hand, which still holds a small, wet pebble he was just rinsing)—and a spark! (He quickly tucks the pebble back into his sleeve with an obvious, clumsy flourish)."

**Millicent Preston** (She locks onto the flash of movement, her gaze narrowing, a specific, focused abrasive flare almost illuminating her features) "A spark? What did you just put in your sleeve? That wasn't flint, and that wasn't steel. It looked like a river stone, wet. From one of my clean water alembics."

**Dimble** "Nothing! Just a... (his voice cracks)—a good luck charm. A reminder of Nerull’s patience. Look, Millicent, I have customers waiting. (He points desperately to an empty table). They... uh, they really, really appreciate the complex, earthy notes of the Nerull’s Rest Ale. It’s all the rage. Ash. Peat. It’s very popular."

**Millicent Preston** (Her tone drops, a cold, absolute command) "Nerull’s Rest is popular because it is the _only_ stable drinkable fluid for miles that doesn’t taste like sulfur. And that is why I am suspicious. This volcano is purification, the priests say. But this tavern is an island of muddy life. If this is illusion magic, Dimble, and I drink this water with a light show, I will shut you down. If this is unstable alchemy and it melts the glass and poisons my breath, your economic success won't save you."

**Dimble** (He practically implodes, his entire body shrinking, his internal voice screaming. He thinks: _Is it permanent? Does it need a sacrifice? It’s grumbling, just like the volcano! If the beer stops, my life ends! I need an alchemist! I need an elementalist! I need a Druid!_) (He finally blurts out) "It's... complex, Millicent! It’s highly complex! A fusion! Alchemy and... (he almost whispers)—and Druid craft. It... ripens. Overnight. In a controlled environment.  Leave the technical details to me. Just pay your rate, enjoy the product, and move along!"

**Millicent Preston** (She glares, a long, abrasive look that says she has not been moved.  'Druid craft' maybe something to do with that vagabond Kaelen?). This is not the end of the 'simple' question, Dimble. 

**Narrative** Millicent turns with a dramatic flash of her feathered hat and exits the tavern with a firm, military stride. Dimble stands frozen for a moment then quickly retrieves the fermenter’s pebble from his sleeve and drops it back into a large earthen cask of fresh, ash-free water. He knows he has twelve hours, but his stomach grumbles just as loudly as the distant volcano.
