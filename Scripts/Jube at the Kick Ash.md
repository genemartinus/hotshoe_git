In the high-stakes, soot-covered economy of **Hotstone**, a conversation between **Jube Curnow** and **Milton Runyon** about an apple tree is less a casual chat and more a collision of desperate optimism and crushing reality.

If Jube walked into **Kick Ash General Goods** and asked Milton about his apple tree, here is how that encounter would likely unfold:

### 1. The "Ghostly" Entrance

Jube, being a **Ghostwise Halfling** and a notoriously "disorganized" cleric, would likely drift in silently, perhaps smelling faintly of cemetery moss. Milton, already on edge because his mercantile business is struggling, would probably jump—reaching for a hidden dagger before realizing it’s just the village’s least-effective healer.

### 2. The Question: "How's the tree, Milton?"

The mere mention of an **Apple Tree** in a volcanic wasteland is a sensitive subject. To the rest of the village, it’s a pipe dream; to Milton, it’s his entire reason for participating in the "Kick Ash" grind.

- **Milton’s Reaction:** He would likely stop whatever he’s doing (probably polishing a rusted shovel or sorting through low-grade magnetite) and sigh. His eyes would glaze over with the vision of an orchard that doesn't smell like sulfur.
    
- **The Technical Problem:** Milton would likely explain the "Thermal Grafting" nightmare he’s facing. Growing a carbon-based, water-heavy fruit like an apple in Hotstone requires a specialized cooling system that he can’t afford. He might show Jube a withered sapling he’s keeping in a lead-lined pot behind the counter, trying to keep it alive with "Sweet-Water" from the springs.

### 3. Jube’s "Worst Cleric" Contribution

Being the "worst in her profession," Jube’s attempt to help would probably backfire:

- **The Failed Blessing:** She might attempt a _Ceremony_ or a minor blessing to encourage growth. However, given her track record, she’d likely accidentally bless the **Needle-Silt** weeds growing in the floorboards instead, causing them to sharpen and puncture Milton’s floor.
    
- **The Morbid Perspective:** Jube might unintentionally point out that the only place with enough organic "fertilizer" for a real tree is the cemetery she tends. This would likely horrify Milton, who wants a wholesome orchard, not a "Graveyard Gala" apple.
    

### 4. The Likely Conclusion

The conversation would probably end with Milton trying to upsell Jube on a "slightly used" respirator mask or a jar of **Ash Yam** boot polish to fund his "Orchard Foundation."

- **The Result:** Jube leaves with an item she didn't need and forgot to pay for; Milton goes back to dreaming of apples while the volcanic ash settles on his empty shelves.
